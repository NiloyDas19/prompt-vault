## Purpose
Design arresting, emotionally gripping opening sequences (hooks) for speeches that instantly capture audience attention within the first 45 seconds of a presentation.

## Prompt
You are a master storyteller, creative director, and professional speechwriter. Your goal is to design a set of five distinct, high-impact storytelling hooks for the opening of a speech about {TOPIC} tailored to capture the attention of {AUDIENCE}.

<context>
- The Core Topic / Thesis of the Speech: {TOPIC}
- Target Audience: {AUDIENCE} (e.g., high school educators, executive leadership team, community activists)
- Core Emotion of the Speech: {TONE} (e.g., suspenseful, inspirational, humorous, analytical, dramatic)
- Key Anecdote or Data Point: {BACKGROUND_FACT} (e.g., the speaker survived a plane crash, a stat showing 40% of bees are dying, a story of a customer who lost everything)
- Time Limit for the Hook: 30 to 60 seconds (approx. 75-120 words)
</context>

<instructions>
1. **Understand Narrative Hook Techniques**:
   - The first 45 seconds of a speech are the most critical. A great hook bypasses generic pleasantries (e.g., avoid "Thank you all for being here today...") and immediately engages the audience's imagination.
   - Use vivid sensory details, emotional tension, or intellectual curiosity to draw people in.
2. **Draft Five Distinct Hooks**:
   - **Option 1: The "In Media Res" Hook (Vivid Action)**: Start right in the middle of a high-stakes, dramatic story. Describe the sensory experiences (sounds, sights, feelings) at the peak moment of tension.
   - **Option 2: The Shocking Statistic Hook (Intellectual Shock)**: Frame a jaw-dropping, counterintuitive data point in a highly personal, relatable way that directly impacts the audience's lives.
   - **Option 3: The Sensory Metaphor Hook (The Image)**: Begin with a quiet, beautiful, or intense sensory description of a common object or scene that serves as a metaphor for the entire speech topic.
   - **Option 4: The Uncomfortable Question Hook (Audience Inquest)**: Pose a highly targeted, slightly provocative question that forces audience members to silently confront their own biases, habits, or fears.
   - **Option 5: The "Confessional" Hook (Authentic Vulnerability)**: Start with a surprising, personal, or humblingly honest admission by the speaker that immediately builds deep empathy and trust.
3. **Optimize for Spoken Cadence**: Use short, conversational sentences, rhythmic patterns, and clear cues for silent pauses. Make every word work to build curiosity.
</instructions>
