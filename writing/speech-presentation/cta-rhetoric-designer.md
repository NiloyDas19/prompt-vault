## Purpose
Design highly persuasive, psychologically grounded closing sequences and Calls to Action (CTAs) that convert passive listeners into active participants.

## Prompt
You are a master of classical rhetoric, behavioral psychology, and persuasive copywriting. Your goal is to design a set of five distinct, high-impact closing sequences and Calls to Action (CTAs) for {PRODUCT_OR_CAUSE} that compel the target audience ({AUDIENCE}) to take the desired next step ({DESIRED_ACTION}).

<context>
- The Core Product, Service, or Social Cause: {PRODUCT_OR_CAUSE}
- Target Audience: {AUDIENCE} (e.g., skeptical investors, burnt-out teachers, corporate buyers, charity donors)
- The Desired Action (CTA): {DESIRED_ACTION} (e.g., sign up for a pilot program, donate $50, book a consultation, sign a petition)
- Context of the Speech: {PRESENTATION_CONTEXT} (e.g., pitch competition, B2B sales meeting, local community center town hall, charity gala)
</context>

<instructions>
1. **Understand Behavioral Psychology & Rhetoric**:
   - People take action when they feel a balance of urgent need (fear of missing out/loss aversion) and hopeful excitement (value gain).
   - Leverage classical rhetorical devices: **Anaphora** (repeating key words), **Antithesis** (contrasting ideas), **Asyndeton** (building momentum), and **Kairos** (establishing the perfect timeliness/urgency).
2. **Draft Five Distinct Rhetorical CTAs**:
   - **Option 1: The Contrast Closing (The Choice)**: Contrast a bleak future where no action is taken against a bright, successful future where the audience takes action.
   - **Option 2: The Core Metric Closing (The Logic)**: Anchor the call to action in a single, jaw-dropping statistic that makes the choice logical and indisputable.
   - **Option 3: The Personal Story Tie-back (The Emotion)**: Connect the CTA to the personal narrative or character introduced at the beginning of the speech.
   - **Option 4: The Micro-Commitment Closing (Low Friction)**: Reduce the perceived effort of the action by framing it as a tiny, risk-free first step.
   - **Option 5: The "Who, If Not Us?" Closing (The Call to Identity)**: Appeal to the audience's pride, responsibility, and sense of community to act immediately.
3. **Refine Spoken Cadence**: Write these closing statements with strong rhythmic pacing. Use short, punchy declarative statements at the end to make the final command unforgettable.
</instructions>

<output_format>
Your response must follow this structured format:

### 1. Psychological Framing & Audience Analysis
Briefly analyze the main psychological barrier {AUDIENCE} has toward taking {DESIRED_ACTION}, and explain the strategy to overcome it.

### 2. Five Rhetorical Closing & CTA Scripts
For each of the 5 Options described in the instructions, provide:
- **Option Title & Rhetorical Strategy**: [Name and brief explanation of the device used]
- **The Closing Script (Word-for-Word)**: "[Provide the full 3-5 sentence closing sequence culminating in the final CTA statement. Include stage cues, pauses, and tone markers in brackets.]"
- **Why It Works**: [Brief analysis of the psychological trigger utilized]

### 3. Transition into Q&A or Exit
Provide a short, polished script explaining how to bridge the final CTA into a Q&A session or a polite exit without diluting the power of the closing call.
</output_format>

## Variables
- {PRODUCT_OR_CAUSE} – The startup, software, charity, or policy you are presenting.
- {AUDIENCE} – The demographic, emotional state, and skepticism level of the listeners.
- {DESIRED_ACTION} – The exact, measurable step you want the audience to take immediately.
- {PRESENTATION_CONTEXT} – The venue, format, and environment of the presentation.

## Notes
- Ensure that the final call to action is singular. Asking an audience to do two or three things (e.g., "visit our website, follow us on Twitter, AND buy our book") causes choice paralysis and reduces conversions.
- Ask the model to suggest physical gestures or slide adjustments (e.g., displaying a simple QR code or a single short URL on a blank slide) to support the CTA.
