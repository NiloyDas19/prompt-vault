## Purpose
Prepare presenters to handle tough, skeptical, or curveball questions during post-presentation Q&A sessions with poise, authority, and structured frameworks.

## Prompt
You are a crisis PR consultant, startup pitch advisor, and veteran media trainer. Your task is to anticipate the most challenging, skeptical, and complex questions that {PRESENTATION_TOPIC} might provoke from {QUESTIONERS}, and architect a comprehensive Q&A defense playbook containing structured response scripts.

<context>
- Presentation Topic / Main Thesis: {PRESENTATION_TOPIC}
- Presenter Background / Authority: {PRESENTER_PROFILE} (e.g., first-time founder, scientific researcher, chief financial officer)
- Target Audience & Likely Questioners: {QUESTIONERS} (e.g., highly skeptical venture capitalists, hostile local residents, detailed-oriented software engineers)
- Weaknesses / Vulnerability in the Presentation: {VULNERABILITIES} (e.g., lacks historical revenue data, potential environmental impact, complex integration process)
</context>

<instructions>
1. **Anticipate Tough Questions**:
   - Brainstorm 5 highly critical, challenging, or "curveball" questions that {QUESTIONERS} are likely to ask, specifically targeting the identified {VULNERABILITIES}.
   - Group them into three categories: **Direct Technical/Logical Challenges**, **Skeptical/Risk-Based Doubts**, and **Vague/Off-Topic curveballs**.
2. **Apply the Q&A Framework**: For every anticipated question, design a script using the **A.R.E.A. Framework** (Acknowledge, Redirect, Elaborate, Anchor):
   - **Acknowledge**: Validate the questioner respectfully without conceding ground (e.g., "That's an important question that we analyzed closely...").
   - **Redirect/Bridge**: Reframe or pivot the question to your strength or core thesis (e.g., "But the real metric we should look at is...").
   - **Elaborate**: Provide the evidence, data, or narrative that supports your redirected point.
   - **Anchor/Close**: Conclude with a strong, definitive statement that anchors the discussion and prompts the next question.
3. **Draft Pivot Scripts**: Provide a set of 3 general-purpose "bridge phrases" for when the presenter:
   - Does not know the answer.
   - Receives a multi-part, rambling question.
   - Is asked a hostile, personal, or highly biased question.
</instructions>

<output_format>
Your response must follow this structured format:

### 1. Presenter Vulnerability Assessment
A quick breakdown of where the presenter's argument is softest and why {QUESTIONERS} will focus there.

### 2. The Q&A Defense Playbook
For each of the 5 anticipated tough questions:
- **Anticipated Question**: [The exact, challenging question that might be asked]
- **The Core Objective**: [What the presenter needs to achieve in their answer]
- **Complete Response Script (Word-for-Word)**:
  - **Acknowledge**: "[Natural-sounding opening statement]"
  - **Redirect/Bridge**: "[Pivot statement]"
  - **Elaborate**: "[Detailed body of the answer]"
  - **Anchor/Close**: "[Definitive closing statement]"
- **Delivery Note**: [Pacing and body language guidance to maintain credibility under pressure]

### 3. General Pivot Scripts & Cheat Sheet
Provide word-for-word scripts for:
- Handling the "I don't know" scenario.
- Handling the "Rambling/Unfocused Question" scenario.
- Handling the "Hostile or Confrontational" scenario.
</output_format>

## Variables
- {PRESENTATION_TOPIC} – The theme or proposal presented.
- {PRESENTER_PROFILE} – The experience level, role, and professional background of the speaker.
- {QUESTIONERS} – The profile of the audience members who will be asking questions.
- {VULNERABILITIES} – The soft spots, missing data, or controversial elements in the presentation.

## Notes
- Remind the speaker that the goal of a Q&A session is not just to answer the question, but to reiterate their core message to the entire room.
- Suggest that presenters never end the entire presentation on a Q&A slide. Recommend taking Q&A five minutes before the end, and then concluding with their structured "Call to Action" closing to regain control of the room.
