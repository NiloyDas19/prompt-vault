## Purpose
Create a high-impact keynote speech outline designed to structure a compelling narrative arc, outline transitions, and prompt slide/stage directions.

## Prompt
You are a master speechwriter and public speaking coach who has crafted keynote speeches for world-class CEOs, politicians, and industry leaders. Your task is to draft a comprehensive, highly engaging keynote speech outline for {SPEAKER_TITLE} addressing a target audience on {THEME}.

<context>
- Speaker Identity/Title: {SPEAKER_TITLE} (e.g., VP of Engineering, tech founder, educational advocate)
- Main Topic / Theme: {THEME} (e.g., the future of AI in design, overcoming adversity in entrepreneurship)
- Target Audience: {AUDIENCE} (e.g., industry professionals, undergraduate students, prospective investors)
- Speech Duration: {DURATION} (e.g., 20 minutes, 45 minutes)
- Core Takeaway / Big Idea: {BIG_IDEA}
- Tone: {TONE} (e.g., visionary, authoritative, storytelling-heavy, energetic, humorous)
</context>

<instructions>
1. **Structure the Narrative Arc**: Organize the speech using a classic three-act structure optimized for public speaking:
   - **Act I: The Hook & Setup (approx. 15% of time)**: Grab attention immediately, state the core premise/theme, and build empathy and connection with the audience.
   - **Act II: The Journey & Core Arguments (approx. 70% of time)**: Deliver 3 core narrative or data pillars. Support each pillar with a personal anecdote, case study, or striking statistic. Focus on high tension, discovery, and conflict resolution.
   - **Act III: The Climax & Call to Action (approx. 15% of time)**: Synthesize the key points, deliver the emotional climax or vision of the future, and present a memorable call to action (CTA).
2. **Include Stage & Visual Directions**: For every section of the outline, suggest:
   - **Visual Cue**: Slide description or recommendations on whether to have a blank screen.
   - **Delivery Note**: Guidance on tone of voice, pacing, pauses, and movement on stage.
3. **Draft Key Quotes**: Provide 2-3 fully written, high-impact quotes or "soundbites" designed to be highly memorable and tweetable/sharable.
</instructions>

<output_format>
Your response must follow this structured format:

### 1. Keynote Blueprint
- **Title of the Speech**: [Compelling, title]
- **Core Message in One Sentence**: [Synthesized takeaway]
- **Audience Connection Strategy**: [How this specific audience's interest will be sustained]

### 2. Detailed Speech Outline
Structure the outline chronologically, using minutes/timestamps based on {DURATION}. For each section:
- **Timestamp / Duration**: [e.g., 0:00 - 3:00]
- **Theme / Goal**: [e.g., Introduce the villain of the story]
- **Key Talking Points**: [Sub-bullets with the argument and narrative threads]
- **Recommended Narrative Hook / Anecdote**: [Brief summary of the story to tell]
- **Slide Cue**: [Description of slide visuals]
- **Speaker Delivery Note**: [Pacing, body language, and voice guidance]

### 3. Shareable Soundbites
Provide 3 powerful, rhythmically polished sentences that represent the climax or key insights of the speech.
</output_format>

## Variables
- {SPEAKER_TITLE} – The professional background, title, or authority of the speaker.
- {THEME} – The central topic or thesis of the speech.
- {AUDIENCE} – The demographic, knowledge level, and emotional state of the listeners.
- {DURATION} – The time slot allocated for the speech.
- {BIG_IDEA} – The singular, powerful message you want the audience to remember.
- {TONE} – The primary emotional feel or speaker style.

## Notes
- Remind the speaker that slides should support, not duplicate, the spoken word. Use the "Slide Cue" section to recommend simple, image-heavy visuals over walls of bullet points.
- If the speech needs to be interactive, ask the model to incorporate specific audience-interaction prompts (e.g., raises of hands, interactive polls).
