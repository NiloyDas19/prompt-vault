## Purpose
Design a complete panel moderation guide and discussion flow, including panelist profiles, curated questions, introductions, and flow management tools to run a highly dynamic, engaging event.

## Prompt
You are a professional panel moderator, conference organizer, and talk show producer. Your task is to design a comprehensive Panel Discussion Guide for {TOPIC} that structures the entire session, coordinates panelist dynamic, and guarantees an engaging experience for {AUDIENCE}.

<context>
- Panel Topic / Theme: {TOPIC}
- Target Audience: {AUDIENCE} (e.g., industry professionals, local citizens, university students)
- Total Panel Duration: {DURATION} (e.g., 45 minutes, 60 minutes)
- Panelist Profiles (Names, Titles, & Expertise): {PANELIST_PROFILES}
- Main Goals of the Discussion: {GOALS} (e.g., debate policy, share career tips, showcase new research)
</context>

<instructions>
1. **Analyze Panelist Dynamics**: Review {PANELIST_PROFILES}. Identify potential areas of agreement, unique individual stories, and areas where panelists might have constructive disagreements or differing perspectives (to avoid a boring "agreement echo chamber").
2. **Apply the Dynamic Panel Flow**:
   - **Phase 1: The Launch & Hook (approx. 10% of time)**: Write a compelling opening hook by the moderator. Draft a warm, rapid-fire introduction for each panelist based on {PANELIST_PROFILES}.
   - **Phase 2: The Core Dialogue (approx. 60% of time)**:
     - Develop 3 distinct segments or themes within the {TOPIC}.
     - For each segment, draft 2 high-value, open-ended questions designed to spark discussion. Assign each question to the best-suited panelist, with suggestions on who should respond or pivot next.
     - Include "Follow-up Prompts" to keep the energy dynamic (e.g., "Sarah, do you agree with David's take on this?").
   - **Phase 3: Audience Q&A (approx. 20% of time)**: Draft 3 backup questions for the moderator to ask if the audience is slow to participate.
   - **Phase 4: The Wrap-up & Core Takeaway (approx. 10% of time)**: Write a closing question that asks panelists for a single, actionable piece of advice, and draft the moderator's closing remarks.
3. **Draft Moderator Intervention Scripts**: Provide word-for-word scripts for common moderating challenges:
   - Politely interrupting a panelist who is rambling or hogging the microphone.
   - Getting a quiet panelist back into the conversation.
   - Redirecting the discussion when it gets too dry or off-topic.
</instructions>

<output_format>
Your response must follow this structured format:

### 1. Panel Strategy & Dynamics
- **Theme Statement**: [A one-sentence summary of the panel's objective]
- **Constructive Friction Plan**: [How you will play the panelists' differing perspectives off each other to create engaging tension]

### 2. Complete Panel Agenda & Moderation Script
Present the complete chronological agenda based on {DURATION}. For each section:
- **Timestamp / Duration**: [e.g., 0:00 - 5:00]
- **Section Goal**: [e.g., Introductions & Hook]
- **Moderator Script (Word-for-Word)**: "[The exact words the moderator should say to introduce the segment or panelists]"
- **Curated Panelist Questions**:
  - **Question 1**: "[Detailed, open-ended question]" -> **Target Panelist**: [Name] -> **Follow-up Pivot**: [Name of another panelist to bring in]
  - **Question 2**: "[Detailed, open-ended question]" -> **Target Panelist**: [Name]

### 3. Moderator Crisis Management Cheat Sheet
Provide word-for-word scripts for the 3 intervention scenarios (The Rambler, The Silent Panelist, The Off-Topic Pivot).
</output_format>

## Variables
- {TOPIC} – The main theme or title of the panel discussion.
- {AUDIENCE} – The demographic and expectation level of the attendees.
- {DURATION} – The total time allocated for the entire panel event.
- {PANELIST_PROFILES} – A list of names, titles, organizations, and brief summaries of each panelist's unique expertise or perspective.
- {GOALS} – The core takeaways or debates you want to accomplish.

## Notes
- Tell the moderator to avoid having panelists go down the line answering the exact same question. Encourage them to direct specific questions to specific people to keep the panel conversational.
- Advise the moderator to run a brief "pre-panel huddle" with the panelists using this guide 15 minutes before going on stage to align on the general flow.
