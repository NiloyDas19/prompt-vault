## Purpose
Structure a persuasive, modular elevator pitch that conveys the problem, solution, unique value proposition, and call to action under strict time constraints (30, 60, or 90 seconds).

## Prompt
You are a startup pitch coach, angel investor, and venture capitalist. Your task is to design a set of three modular elevator pitches (30-second, 60-second, and 90-second versions) for {COMPANY_NAME} that clearly explain what the business does, why it matters, and how it wins.

<context>
- Company/Product Name: {COMPANY_NAME}
- Target Customer/Niche: {TARGET_CUSTOMER} (e.g., small local bakeries, enterprise cybersecurity teams)
- The Big Problem: {PROBLEM} (what painful frustration do you solve?)
- The Solution: {SOLUTION} (how does your product/service solve the problem?)
- Unique Value Proposition (UVP): {UVP} (why are you better or different from competitors?)
- Target Audience for the Pitch: {PITCH_AUDIENCE} (e.g., casual networking contact, prospective investor, potential customer)
- The Goal / Call to Action (CTA): {CTA} (e.g., book a 15-minute demo, exchange contact info, join a waitlist)
</context>

<instructions>
1. **Apply the Elevator Pitch Structure**:
   - **The Hook**: A surprising question, fact, or relatable statement about the {PROBLEM}.
   - **The Value Proposition**: Clear description of the {SOLUTION} and who it helps ({TARGET_CUSTOMER}).
   - **The Secret Sauce / Traction**: Why your company ({COMPANY_NAME}) is uniquely qualified ({UVP}), or a metric demonstrating success.
   - **The Call to Action**: A low-friction, conversational {CTA}.
2. **Draft Three Distinct Versions**:
   - **The 30-Second Spark (approx. 75 words)**: Focused entirely on hook, solution, and CTA. Used for casual networking.
   - **The 60-Second Story (approx. 150 words)**: Adds problem context, customer success/traction, and differentiators. Used for brief presentations.
   - **The 90-Second Showcase (approx. 225 words)**: Includes brief business model/market size or competitor contrast. Used for pitch contests or formal introductory meetings.
3. **Use High-Impact, Conversational Language**: Avoid corporate jargon, buzzwords (e.g., "revolutionary," "synergy," "cutting-edge"), and complex industry acronyms. Write sentences that are easy to say out loud without running out of breath.
</instructions>

## Variables
- {COMPANY_NAME} – The name of your product, service, or company.
- {TARGET_CUSTOMER} – The ideal client profile who pays for the solution.
- {PROBLEM} – The core pain point or market gap.
- {SOLUTION} – The description of how you resolve the pain point.
- {UVP} – Your unfair advantage, proprietary tech, or unique angle.
- {PITCH_AUDIENCE} – Who you are pitching to.
- {CTA} – The desired next step.

## Notes
- Remind the user to practice speaking the pitches out loud. What looks good on paper can sometimes sound robotic or awkward.
- If the company has early traction (e.g., "10,000 active users," "growing 20% month-over-month"), include that in the {UVP} or {SOLUTION} variable for best results.
