## Purpose
Build a complete brand story map using core storytelling elements to make a brand deeply memorable, humanized, and distinct.

## Prompt
You are a master brand strategist and narrative designer. Your goal is to construct a comprehensive Brand Storytelling Framework that elevates a company from a mere utility to a legendary, emotionally resonant narrative.

Here is the raw data about the brand:

<brand_details>
- **Brand Name:** {BRAND_NAME}
- **Target Audience:** {TARGET_AUDIENCE}
- **Core Values:** {CORE_VALUES}
- **Origin Story:** {ORIGIN_STORY}
- **Transformation Offered:** {TRANSFORMATION_OFFERED}
</brand_details>

Using this information, build a cohesive, deeply engaging brand storytelling book. Ensure the narrative is human, visceral, and entirely avoids generic corporate jargon. Organize your output into the following XML-like structured sections:

<output_format>

### 1. The Core Mythos (The Genesis)
- **The Call to Action:** What was the systemic injustice, frustration, or status quo in the industry that catalyzed the brand's birth?
- **The Threshold Moment:** A vivid retelling of the origin story ({ORIGIN_STORY}) structured as a cinematic inciting incident. 
- **The Core Belief:** A single, non-negotiable statement of what this brand believes about the world, rooted in ({CORE_VALUES}).

### 2. The Character Archetypes
- **The Hero (The Customer):** Define the customer's identity, deep-seated anxieties, hidden aspirations, and what is currently holding them back from greatness.
- **The Mentor (The Brand):** Define the brand's archetype (e.g., Creator, Sage, Rebel, Ruler) and explain how the brand guides the customer.
- **The Shadow (The Problem):** Give the main industry enemy or obstacle a name and personality. (e.g., "The Complexity Monster," "The Burnout Culture").

### 3. The Quest & Transformation Arc
- **The Call to Adventure:** How does the brand invite the Hero ({TARGET_AUDIENCE}) to change their life?
- **The Magic Gift (The Product/Service):** Position your product/service not as a list of features, but as a tool or superpower that enables {TRANSFORMATION_OFFERED}.
- **The Climax & Return:** Contrast the before-and-after states in vivid, emotional detail. What does the hero's life look like once they embrace the transformation?

### 4. Brand Narrative Voice & Lexicon
- **The Brand Vocabulary:** List 5 "Signature Words" the brand embraces and 5 "Forbidden Words" it rejects.
- **Tone Profile:** Describe the verbal style (e.g., rebellious but intellectual, warm but uncompromising) with brief examples of how to write a headline.

### 5. The Brand Anthem
- Write a 150-word, high-impact "Brand Anthem" or manifesto. This should be an emotional call-to-arms that can be read aloud in a commercial or printed on packaging.

</output_format>

Incorporate the brand values and origin story details naturally throughout the strategy. Ensure that the tone is inspiring, authentic, and evocative.

## Variables
- {BRAND_NAME} – The name of the brand or company.
- {TARGET_AUDIENCE} – The specific customer segment, including their struggles and aspirations.
- {CORE_VALUES} – The defining beliefs and principles of the brand (e.g., radical transparency, ruthless simplicity, adventurous spirit).
- {ORIGIN_STORY} – The background of how/why the brand was founded, including any pivotal moments.
- {TRANSFORMATION_OFFERED} – The ultimate change the brand enables for its customers (e.g., from overwhelmed to organized, from insecure to confident).

## Notes
- Remind stakeholders that in brand storytelling, **the customer is the hero, not the brand**. The brand acts as the mentor (Yoda, not Luke).
- This framework should be shared with copywriting, design, and product teams to ensure narrative alignment across every customer touchpoint.
- Review and refine this narrative yearly or whenever there is a major shift in product positioning or audience expansion.
