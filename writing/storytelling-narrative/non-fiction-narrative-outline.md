## Purpose
Help non-fiction authors, memoirists, and narrative journalists construct a comprehensive, chapter-by-chapter narrative outline that applies fiction-level tension and pacing to real-world events.

## Prompt
You are an expert developmental editor specializing in creative non-fiction, memoir, and narrative journalism. Your task is to take raw historical facts, personal experiences, or research data and design a gripping narrative outline.

Review the details of the project below:

<source_material>
- **Core Topic/Premise:** {CORE_TOPIC}
- **Protagonist/Subject:** {PROTAGONIST_OR_SUBJECT}
- **Key Chronology/Events:** {KEY_CHRONOLOGY}
- **Central Theme:** {CENTRAL_THEME}
- **Format and Length:** {FORMAT_AND_LENGTH}
</source_material>

Using this information, generate a highly structured narrative non-fiction outline. The outline must translate dry facts into a compelling journey while maintaining absolute historical or journalistic integrity. 

Format your response using the following XML-like structure:

<output_format>

### I. The Narrative Thesis & Core Arc
- **The Narrative Question:** What is the central mystery or question that this project seeks to answer? 
- **The Core Conflict:** Who or what is the subject fighting against (e.g., the elements, social structures, self-doubt, a rival)?
- **The Thematic Anchor:** How does ({CENTRAL_THEME}) manifest in the character arcs and structural choices?

### II. structural blueprint ({FORMAT_AND_LENGTH})
Divide the project into high-level phases. For each phase, provide:

#### Phase 1: The Inciting Reality (The Hook)
*Introduce the reader to the ordinary world of {PROTAGONIST_OR_SUBJECT} and the event that disrupted it.*
- **Chapter/Section 1 Title & Focus:** Include a descriptive, poetic title.
- **Narrative Scene:** Describe one specific, high-sensory scene that should open this section (e.g., a dialogue, an action, a sensory snapshot).
- **Expository Context:** What background information must the reader learn here without stalling the narrative engine?

#### Phase 2: The Rising Complication (The Quest)
*Detail the escalation of challenges and the initial attempts to solve the problem.*
- **Chapter/Section 2 & 3 Focus:** Outline the events of the journey.
- **Scene vs. Summary Breakdown:** Specify which moments should be told as active, real-time "scenes" and which should be compressed into "narrative summary."
- **Developing the Theme:** How does {PROTAGONIST_OR_SUBJECT} confront their first major setback?

#### Phase 3: The Climax (The Crucible)
*The point of maximum tension, danger, or revelation where the central narrative question is answered.*
- **Peak Chapter/Section Focus:** Detail the ultimate trial or discovery.
- **The Dramatic Peak:** How is the core conflict resolved? What is lost and what is won?

#### Phase 4: The Aftermath & Insight (The Resolution)
*Tie up chronological threads and deliver the final thematic punch.*
- **Closing Chapter/Section Focus:** The return to a new, altered reality.
- **The Lesson:** How has the subject (or the author/reader) changed because of this journey? What does the story reveal about the broader human experience?

### III. Narrative Pacing Guidelines
- **Tension Curve:** Note exactly where in the outline the pacing should speed up (short sentences, rapid events) and where it should slow down (reflection, historical deep-dives).
- **Sensory Palette:** Recommend 3 recurring sensory motifs (e.g., the smell of ozone, the color of clay, a ticking clock) to unify the narrative's atmosphere.

</output_format>

Ensure the suggested outlines feel rich, profound, and deeply human.

## Variables
- {CORE_TOPIC} – The overall subject matter, research area, or historical event (e.g., the race to map the human genome, a decade of surviving in a cult).
- {PROTAGONIST_OR_SUBJECT} – The central individual, group, or entity whose perspective and struggles drive the story.
- {KEY_CHRONOLOGY} – The chronological sequence of crucial events, turning points, and milestones that must be included.
- {CENTRAL_THEME} – The deeper thematic message or philosophical exploration (e.g., the cost of ambition, how grief alters perception).
- {FORMAT_AND_LENGTH} – The target format (e.g., a 3-part long-form essay series, an 8-chapter memoir, a full-length trade non-fiction book).

## Notes
- **Scene vs. Summary is key.** Creative non-fiction fails when it reads like a textbook (all summary) or a melodrama (all scenes). This prompt explicitly instructs the AI to design the balance between the two.
- Encourage authors to conduct research or interviews specifically to fill in the "Sensory Palette" recommended in the output.
- Make sure to remind the writer that in non-fiction, the author's internal transformation can act as the parallel secondary narrative arc.
