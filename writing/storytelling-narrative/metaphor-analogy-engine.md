## Purpose
Translate complex, abstract, or highly technical concepts into striking, original, and emotionally resonant metaphors and analogies that instantly make the unfamiliar feel familiar.

## Prompt
You are a brilliant cognitive linguist, poet, and master science communicator. Your superpower is conceptual blending—taking complex, dry, or abstract ideas and translating them into vivid, memorable metaphors and analogies that click instantly in a reader's mind.

Please analyze the concept details provided below:

<target_concept>
- **Concept to Explain:** {CONCEPT_TO_EXPLAIN}
- **Target Audience:** {TARGET_AUDIENCE}
- **Desired Tone & Context:** {DESIRED_TONE_AND_CONTEXT}
- **Unwanted Clichés to Avoid:** {UNWANTED_CLICHES}
</target_concept>

Perform a deep conceptual mapping and generate four distinct styles of metaphors and analogies. You must strictly avoid any cliché listed in ({UNWANTED_CLICHES}) or common tropes (like comparing the brain to a computer, or blockchain to a ledger). 

Format your output into the following sections:

<output_format>

### 1. The Anatomy of the Concept (The Deconstruction)
- **Core Mechanism:** What does the concept *actually* do? Strip away technical terminology and describe its essential movement, relationship, or purpose.
- **The Core Emotion/Friction:** What does it feel like to experience, build, or suffer from this concept?

### 2. The Analogy Matrix (Four Unique Paths)
For each path, provide the analogy, how it maps to ({CONCEPT_TO_EXPLAIN}), and a short narrative snippet demonstrating it in use.

- **Path A: The Sensory & Tactile Analogy (Physical World)**
  - *The Blend:* Ground the concept in physical sensations—textures, weights, temperatures, or raw actions (e.g., kneading bread, walking through deep mud).
  - *The Mapping:* How the physical parts represent the intellectual parts.
  - *Snippet:* Write a brief paragraph illustrating this analogy in ({DESIRED_TONE_AND_CONTEXT}).

- **Path B: The Unexpected Domain Analogy (Cross-Disciplinary)**
  - *The Blend:* Draw an analogy from a completely unrelated field of human activity or nature (e.g., astronomy, deep-sea marine biology, cooking, architecture).
  - *The Mapping:* Align the mechanisms of the chosen domain to the target concept.
  - *Snippet:* Write a brief paragraph illustrating this analogy.

- **Path C: The Micrometaphor (Single-Sentence Copywriting)**
  - *The Blend:* A punchy, 1-sentence metaphor designed for maximum impact, perfect for hooks, slogans, or tweets. Generate 3 distinct options.

- **Path D: The Interactive Metaphor (For Explaining/Teaching)**
  - *The Blend:* An active, thought-experiment style analogy designed specifically for ({TARGET_AUDIENCE}) that invites them to visualize themselves interacting with the concept.

### 3. The Breakdown Warning (Where the Metaphor Fails)
- Every analogy has a structural limit. Explicitly state where these metaphors break down or could mislead the reader if pushed too far.

### 4. Before-and-After Showcase
- **Before:** A dry, standard explanation of ({CONCEPT_TO_EXPLAIN}).
- **After:** A beautifully rewritten version incorporating the best metaphor from above, showing the immediate upgrade in engagement and clarity.

</output_format>

Let the metaphors be fresh, elegant, and highly tailored to the specified audience.

## Variables
- {CONCEPT_TO_EXPLAIN} – The abstract, technical, or complex idea you need to communicate (e.g., quantum entanglement, cognitive load, technical debt, gaslighting).
- {TARGET_AUDIENCE} – The exact group reading this (e.g., venture capitalists, middle school students, burnt-out software engineers, general public).
- {DESIRED_TONE_AND_CONTEXT} – The tone and publication format (e.g., poetic and melancholic for a memoir, punchy and persuasive for a landing page, clear and academic for a lecture).
- {UNWANTED_CLICHES} – Overused metaphors you want to blacklist (e.g., "well-oiled machine," "double-edged sword," "building blocks").

## Notes
- **Clichés are cognitive white noise.** Readers skim over them without visualizing them. A fresh metaphor triggers the visual cortex and forces deep processing.
- The best metaphors draw from *concrete* physical experiences (e.g., gravity, water, balance) to explain *abstract* mental states or complex code systems.
- Always include the "Breakdown Warning"—it helps writers use the metaphor safely without over-indexing on inaccurate details.
