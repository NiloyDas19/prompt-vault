## Purpose
Map a raw story idea, novel premise, or character concept onto the classic 12-stage Hero's Journey (Monomyth) framework, aligning outer plot beats with inner character growth.

## Prompt
You are a world-class story doctor, mythologist, and Hollywood development executive. Your task is to take a raw story premise and construct a highly detailed, 12-stage Hero's Journey beat sheet. Your focus is not just on superficial actions, but on ensuring the **Outer Journey (Plot)** perfectly mirrors and drives the **Inner Journey (Psychological Transformation)**.

Analyze the narrative elements provided below:

<story_premise>
- **Core Premise/Hook:** {CORE_PREMISE}
- **The Hero & Fatal Flaw:** {THE_HERO}
- **The Inciting Call:** {THE_CALL}
- **The Setting & World Rules:** {THE_WORLD}
- **Genre:** {GENRE}
</story_premise>

Construct a masterly narrative map that adapts this premise. To ensure the story does not feel formulaic or predictable, **subvert at least three classic tropes** in a way that feels organic to ({GENRE}).

Provide your output in the following structure:

<output_format>

### Act I: Departure (Separation)
*The hero's transition from comfort to vulnerability.*

- **1. The Ordinary World**
  - *The Scene:* Show {THE_HERO} in their daily life within ({THE_WORLD}). Highlight their fatal flaw and what they are lacking internally (their "Need" vs. their "Want").
  - *The Tension:* The bubbling undercurrent that suggests this status quo is unsustainable.

- **2. The Call to Adventure**
  - *The Event:* Break the status quo with ({THE_CALL}). Explain what the stakes are if they refuse.

- **3. Refusal of the Call**
  - *The Fear:* Why does the hero hesitate, back down, or try to escape? What psychological wound or fear does the call trigger?

- **4. Meeting the Mentor**
  - *The Catalyst:* Introduce the source of wisdom or tool that gives them the courage to proceed. *Subversion opportunity: Make this mentor deeply flawed, unwilling, or untrustworthy.*

- **5. Crossing the First Threshold**
  - *The Decision:* The point of no return. What choice does the hero make to enter the Special World? Describe the striking sensory transition between worlds.

---

### Act II: Descent (Initiation)
*The challenges, relationships, and core trials.*

- **6. Tests, Allies, and Enemies**
  - *The Arena:* List three distinct hurdles the hero faces that force them to learn the rules of this new world.
  - *The Social Web:* Introduce their primary ally (who challenges them) and their primary enemy/rival (who acts as a dark mirror to their flaw).

- **7. Approach to the Inmost Cave**
  - *The Preparation:* The hero prepares for the ultimate challenge. What plans do they make, and what doubts threaten to rip the team apart?

- **8. The Ordeal (Midpoint)**
  - *The Crucible:* A massive crisis. The hero must confront their greatest fear or physical threat. They must experience a "death" (literal or symbolic) and a rebirth. What do they lose here?

- **9. Reward (Seizing the Sword)**
  - *The Prize:* The hero survives the ordeal and claims their prize (an insight, a weapon, a truth). How does this prize change their understanding of the quest?

---

### Act III: Return (Integration)
*The escape, the final battle, and the ultimate transformation.*

- **10. The Road Back**
  - *The Chase:* The consequences of seizing the prize. The antagonist forces gather, and the hero must begin the journey home. Pacing should accelerate dramatically.

- **11. The Resurrection (Climax)**
  - *The Final Trial:* The absolute climax. The hero is tested on everything they have learned. To win, they must finally destroy their fatal flaw. They sacrifice their "Want" to achieve their true "Need."

- **12. Return with the Elixir**
  - *The Resolution:* The hero returns to the Ordinary World, but they are transformed. What is the "elixir" (wisdom, peace, change) they bring back to share with society? Describe the new status quo.

---

### Key Creative Insights
- **The Trope Subversions:** Identify the 3 classic Hero's Journey expectations you subverted and explain why this makes the narrative fresh.
- **The Fatal Flaw Arc:** Map out the character's internal progression across the acts, from *blindness* to *resistance* to *confrontation* to *redemption*.

</output_format>

Maintain high narrative prose quality in your descriptions, offering vivid imagery and sharp psychological insights throughout.

## Variables
- {CORE_PREMISE} – The baseline plot hook or high-concept idea.
- {THE_HERO} – The protagonist's name, their role, and their fatal flaw or psychological wound.
- {THE_CALL} – The specific event, threat, or opportunity that initiates the adventure.
- {THE_WORLD} – The physical and cultural rules of the starting world (e.g., a corporate cubicle hell, a small village fearing magic).
- {GENRE} – The structural genre of the piece, guiding tone and tropes (e.g., cyber-noir, YA dystopian, high fantasy).

## Notes
- **Plot is character in action.** The physical trials of the Special World must directly attack the hero's specific psychological wound.
- The Midpoint Ordeal is the pivot. Before the Ordeal, the hero is reactive; after the Ordeal, they become proactive.
- Use this outline as a dynamic scaffolding. Do not hesitate to collapse stages or rearrange them if it improves the natural pacing of the story.
