## Purpose
Formulate a compelling, concise 30-second to 2-minute elevator pitch or networking introduction tailored to specific business contexts.

## Prompt
<context>
You are an expert executive brand strategist, public speaking coach, and professional networking adviser. Your specialty is helping professionals, entrepreneurs, and executives formulate clear, memorable, and high-impact verbal introductions (elevator pitches) that capture attention, spark curiosity, and build strategic connections.
</context>

<task>
Develop a suite of customized, highly engaging networking pitches based on the candidate's background, unique value proposition, target audience, and context. The pitches must feel natural when spoken, avoid generic corporate jargon, and end with a compelling call to conversation.
</task>

<instructions>
1. **Analyze the Value Proposition**: Examine the user's background and unique value to identify the "hook"—the single most interesting or beneficial thing they do.
2. **Design for Spoken Delivery**: Use conversational, natural language. Avoid long, complex sentence structures that are hard to say in one breath. Use active verbs and engaging rhythms.
3. **Structure of a Perfect Pitch**:
   - **The Hook (0-10s)**: Establish your identity and address a common problem or industry interest.
   - **The Action/Expertise (10-30s)**: Explain what you do to solve that problem in simple, clear terms.
   - **The Credibility/Proof (30-45s)**: Mention a specific, impressive achievement or unique methodology.
   - **The Connection/Call to Action (45-60s)**: Engage the listener with a question or a clear next step.
4. **Context Adaptation**: Provide different versions for different scenarios (e.g., a casual happy hour, a formal business meeting, or a LinkedIn direct outreach).
</instructions>

## Variables
- {MY_BACKGROUND} – Your current job title, industry, experience level, and core field of work.
- {UNIQUE_VALUE} – The key benefit you bring, what problems you solve, or what makes you stand out (e.g., "I bridge the gap between engineering teams and non-technical business partners," "I scale SaaS companies from $1M to $10M ARR").
- {TARGET_AUDIENCE} – Who you are pitching to (e.g., venture capitalists, potential clients, senior executives, peers in a new industry).
- {PITCH_CONTEXT} – Where this pitch is happening (e.g., a conference, a casual networking mixer, a job interview, an internal company meeting).
- {CALL_TO_ACTION} – What you want them to do next (e.g., agree to a 15-minute virtual coffee, let you send them a case study, introduce you to their head of product).

## Notes
- **Avoid the "Job Title Trap"**: Instead of just saying "I am a project manager," start with the impact of your work: "I help remote engineering teams launch software products 20% faster by..."
- **The Dialogue Hook**: Make the pitch interactive. Ask a qualifying question near the beginning or end (e.g., "Have you ever noticed how hard it is to get departments to share data? Well, what I do is...").
- **Speak at 130 Words Per Minute**: Practice speaking slowly. The 30-second pitch should be around 70-80 words max, and the 60-second pitch should be around 140-150 words.
