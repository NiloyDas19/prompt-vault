## Purpose
Design a step-by-step risk-mitigated transition plan to shift from one career field or industry into a completely new one.

## Prompt
<context>

Your specialty lies in helping mid-to-senior level professionals execute clean, risk-mitigated, and highly successful career pivots from one domain or industry into a completely new one, without sacrificing their seniority or starting from the absolute bottom.
</context>

<task>

Formulate a robust risk mitigation strategy, map transferable skills to the target industry's terminology, outline a learning and portfolio-building plan, draft networking directives, and construct a detailed month-by-month timeline to execute the pivot successfully.

5. **Resume & LinkedIn Positioning Guide**: Provide explicit instructions on how to rewrite their professional bio, resume summary, and experience bullet points to emphasize future capability over past irrelevance.
</instructions>

## Variables
- {CURRENT_CAREER} – Your current job title, primary industry, and key day-to-day duties (e.g., High School Teacher, Retail Store Manager, Mechanical Engineer).
- {TARGET_CAREER} – The brand new role and industry you are aiming to break into (e.g., UX Researcher in Tech, Operations Manager at a logistics startup, Product Manager).
- {TRANSFERABLE_SKILLS} – Core skills that are valuable across different industries (e.g., public speaking, curriculum design, client relations, resource scheduling, basic data analysis).
- {TIMELINE_MONTHS} – Your target time frame to complete this career transition successfully (e.g., 6 months, 12 months).
- {FINANCIAL_RUNWAY} – The number of months of living expenses you have saved up or the level of financial flexibility you have while executing this transition (e.g., 3 months, 6 months, keeping current job throughout).

## Notes
- **Never Highlight Lack of Experience**: Focus entirely on your transferable skills and your ability to bring fresh, diverse perspectives from your previous career that pure industry insiders lack.
- **The "Bridge" Role Technique**: If the jump is too large (e.g., teacher to software developer), look for a bridge role that sits in between (e.g., customer education specialist at an ed-tech startup) where you can build industry tenure before pivoting again.
- **Connect with Career Switchers**: Find people on LinkedIn who have successfully executed the exact same transition. They are far more likely to offer valuable guidance and referrals than native industry insiders.
