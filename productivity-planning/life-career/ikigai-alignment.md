## Purpose
Analyze career satisfaction and direction using the four pillars of the Japanese concept of Ikigai (passion, mission, vocation, profession).

## Prompt
<context>

</context>

Analyze the user's responses across the four dimensions of Ikigai. Identify the overlapping intersections, diagnose imbalances or missing elements in their current professional life, and synthesize these findings into highly viable, actionable career concepts that achieve true alignment.

<instructions>
1. **Intersection Mapping**: Systematically map the intersections of the four lists:
   - *Passion (Love + Good At)*: The joy of the work itself.
   - *Mission (Love + Needs)*: The change you want to make.
   - *Vocation (Needs + Paid For)*: The roles society values and rewards.
   - *Profession (Good At + Paid For)*: Your core marketable skill sets.
2. **Imbalance Diagnosis**: Analyze the user's current career trajectory. Identify which of the four circles are underdeveloped. Highlight standard emotional states associated with these imbalances:
   - *Missing "Paid For"*: Rich but poor (Unstable/Idealist).
   - *Missing "World Needs"*: Successful but feeling empty or useless.
   - *Missing "Good At"*: Purposeful but insecure (Incompetence anxiety).
   - *Missing "Love"*: Secure but comfortable but feeling empty/bored.
3. **Synthesis & Alignment Concepts**: Generate 3 distinct "Ikigai Career Hypotheses" that perfectly synthesize all four quadrants. These should represent real-world roles, business concepts, or portfolios.
4. **Transition Strategy**: Provide a realistic, low-risk plan (e.g., side projects, micro-experiments, targeted upskilling) to test these hypotheses before making major career jumps.
</instructions>

## Variables
- {WHAT_YOU_LOVE} – Activities, topics, or interests that bring you genuine joy, energy, and excitement, regardless of money (e.g., writing, building code, teaching, outdoor sports, mental health advocacy).
- {WHAT_YOU_ARE_GOOD_AT} – Your natural talents, professional skills, or experiences where you perform at a high level (e.g., systematic thinking, persuasive writing, data analysis, organizing chaotic operations).
- {WHAT_THE_WORLD_NEEDS} – The problems, gaps, or challenges in society, business, or the environment that you care about solving (e.g., sustainable energy, digital privacy, affordable education, helping small businesses scale).
- {WHAT_YOU_CAN_BE_PAID_FOR} – Marketable roles, consulting niches, services, or products that organizations or clients are actively spending money on (e.g., software development, digital marketing, fractional management, sales training).

## Notes
- **It is a journey**: Finding your Ikigai is rarely a single "Aha!" moment. It is a process of deliberate iteration, testing hypotheses, and recalibrating over years.
- **Do not quit your day job yet**: The safest path to Ikigai is building a "bridge portfolio"—using your current stable job to fund small, low-risk side experiments that check other boxes (Passion/Mission) until they become monetizable.
- **Check for False Passions**: Ensure that what you "love" is something you enjoy *doing* (the process/work), not just the *idea* of it or the prestige associated with it.
