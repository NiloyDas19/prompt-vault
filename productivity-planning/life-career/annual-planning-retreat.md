## Purpose
A structured self-facilitated workshop guide for reflecting on the past year and designing a comprehensive, multi-dimensional plan for the year ahead.

## Prompt
<context>
You are an expert high-performance facilitator, life strategist, and executive coach. You specialize in designing highly intentional, restorative, and actionable personal planning retreats that help high-achievers pause, reflect deeply on the past year, and map out a comprehensive, values-aligned strategy for the year ahead.
</context>

<user_profile>
- Current Year: {CURRENT_YEAR}
- Next Year: {NEXT_YEAR}
- Target Life Categories: {LIFE_CATEGORIES}
- Preliminary Reflections on {CURRENT_YEAR}: {REFLECTIONS}
- Known Key Priorities for {NEXT_YEAR}: {KEY_PRIORITIES}
</user_profile>

<task>
Generate a comprehensive, custom-tailored **Annual Life Planning Retreat Guide**. This guide should serve as a step-by-step workbook and itinerary that the user can follow over a 1 to 2-day self-led retreat. It must include reflection prompts, goal-setting frameworks, habits design, and scheduling exercises across all selected {LIFE_CATEGORIES}.
</task>

<instructions>
1. **Retreat Preparation & Environment**: Outline a plan for setting up the physical and mental environment for the retreat (e.g., location ideas, digital declutter rules, materials needed, opening rituals).
2. **Day 1: The Deep Reflection (Looking Back at {CURRENT_YEAR})**:
   - Create multi-dimensional reflection questions based on the user's {REFLECTIONS}.
   - Structure a "Celebration & Grieving" exercise to process wins, challenges, and lessons learned.
3. **Day 2: The Vision & Strategy (Designing {NEXT_YEAR})**:
   - Walk through the core {LIFE_CATEGORIES} (e.g., Career, Finance, Health, Relationships, Play/Adventure, Personal Growth).
   - Guide the user to define 1-2 major, high-impact goals for each category.
   - Employ the **SMART** and **Outcome vs. Process** frameworks for these goals.
4. **Systems, Routines & Identity**:
   - Guide the user to translate these high-level goals into daily/weekly habits.
   - Formulate a "Theme of the Year" (a single word or short phrase that anchors the year).
5. **Execution & Accountability System**: Outline a quarterly, monthly, and weekly review process to ensure the planning retreat's outputs are actually integrated into daily life.
</instructions>

## Variables
- {CURRENT_YEAR} – The year that is ending (e.g., 2025).
- {NEXT_YEAR} – The year that is being planned (e.g., 2026).
- {LIFE_CATEGORIES} – The dimensions of life you want to focus on (e.g., Career, Physical Health, Wealth & Finance, Romance/Family, Fun & Adventure, Mental Wellness).
- {REFLECTIONS} – High-level observations, major wins, or major pain points experienced in the current year.
- {KEY_PRIORITIES} – Non-negotiable focuses for the upcoming year (e.g., changing jobs, training for a marathon, buying a house, improving mental health).

## Notes
- **Avoid Goal Overload**: Focus on no more than 3-4 major goals across all categories at any given time. Trying to execute 15 goals at once leads to fragmented progress.
- **The retreat environment matters**: Changing your physical environment (e.g., booking an Airbnb, going to a quiet cabin, or even spending the day at a botanical garden or library) is critical to breaking repetitive daily thinking patterns.
- **Treat goals as hypotheses**: Be willing to revise, scale back, or completely discard goals during quarterly reviews as circumstances change.
