## Variables
- {TARGET_MENTOR_PROFILE} – The professional status, role, industry, or specific company of the person you want to build a relationship with (e.g., Director of AI at Google, serial entrepreneur in B2B SaaS, senior creative director at an agency).
- {MUTUAL_INTERESTS} – Common ground such as shared alumni network, geographic location, mutual connections, interest in a specific subfield, or a recent podcast interview they gave that resonated with you.
- {OUTREACH_CHANNEL} – The medium through which you will contact them (e.g., LinkedIn direct message, cold email, warm introduction from a mutual contact, Twitter/X DM).
- {MY_VALUE_OFFER} – What you can bring to the table to make it a mutual relationship (e.g., helping them research a topic, giving feedback on their product from a junior developer's perspective, writing a summary of their talks, sheer dedication to executing their advice and reporting back).
- {SPECIFIC_ASK} – A highly structured, low-risk, extremely brief request (e.g., a 10-minute Zoom call to ask 2 specific questions about their career pivot, written feedback on a specific project framework, or a quick coffee if they reside locally).

## Notes
- **Never Ask "Will you be my mentor?"**: This is a major commitment and acts as a psychological red flag. Mentorship must develop organically over multiple value-driven interactions.
- **The Value Loop**: The absolute best way to thank a mentor is to implement their advice, document the results (whether positive or negative), and write back to tell them the outcome. This shows you are worth their time.
- **Respect Boundaries**: If they decline or do not respond after your follow-up sequence, gracefully exit. Respect their time and prioritize other outreach targets.
