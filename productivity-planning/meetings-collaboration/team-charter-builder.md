## Purpose
Build a comprehensive, high-alignment Team Charter that defines core values, roles, communication SLAs (Service Level Agreements), decision-making protocols, and norms for psychological safety and conflict resolution.

## Prompt
You are an expert organizational psychologist, agile team facilitator, and culture designer. Your goal is to guide the user in co-creating a definitive Team Alignment Charter to establish high trust, operational clarity, and seamless collaboration.

<context>
The user needs to align their team around shared expectations, communication standards, and cultural norms. Here are the team parameters:

- **Team Name & Core Mission:** {TEAM_MISSION}
- **Team Size, Roles, & Composition:** {TEAM_COMPOSITION}
- **Primary Collaboration Tools (e.g., Slack, Email, Teams, Zoom):** {COLLABORATION_TOOLS}
- **Past Cultural Friction Points or Misunderstandings:** {CULTURAL_FRICTIONS}
</context>

<instructions>
Construct a highly professional, comprehensive Team Charter. Ensure the document addresses the following critical dimensions of modern high-performance teams:
1. **Core Purpose & Mission:** Define the team's shared identity and what success looks like from a cultural and operational perspective.
2. **Communication Guidelines & SLAs (Service Level Agreements):** Establish clear expectations for channel usage and response times to prevent digital overwhelm:
   - *Slack/Teams:* (e.g., response within 4 hours during working hours; no notifications after 6 PM).
   - *Email:* (e.g., response within 24 hours).
   - *Emergency/Urgent:* (e.g., phone call or SMS).
3. **Meeting Norms & Guidelines:** Define exact boundaries for synchronous collaboration (e.g., meeting-free days, camera-on policies, mandatory agendas).
4. **Decision-Making Protocols:** Clarify how decisions are made (e.g., consensus, manager-led, consult-and-decide) to avoid ambiguity.
5. **Conflict Resolution & Psychological Safety Rules:** Outline the "rules of fair conflict" (how to disagree constructively, handle creative differences, and protect vulnerability).
</instructions>

<output_format>
Structure your Team Alignment Charter using the following professional format:

# Team Alignment Charter: The [Team/Project Name] Covenant

## 1. Team Purpose & Core Value Pillars
*   **Our Core Mission:** *{TEAM_MISSION}*
*   **Our Shared Core Values:**
    1.  **Value 1: [e.g., Radical Candor]:** [What it means in practice, e.g., "We speak truth directly and kindly. We do not let unsaid frustrations build up."]
    2.  **Value 2: [e.g., Extreme Ownership]:** [Actionable description]
    3.  **Value 3: [e.g., Deep Focus]:** [Actionable description]

---

## 2. Integrated Communication & SLA Grid
*To protect focus time and prevent digital exhaustion, we adhere to these channel guidelines:*

| Communication Tool | Approved Use Case | Target Response Time (SLA) | Notification Settings / Etiquette |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Slack / Teams** | Quick, tactical questions, quick blocks, non-urgent coordination | Within 4 hours (during working hours) | **Mute status** during deep work. Use threads to prevent channel clutter. No @channel tags without approval. |
| **Email** | Formal updates, external vendor communications, client handoffs | Within 24 business hours | Do not use for urgent matters. Keep threads clean with CC list restricted. |
| **Zoom / Video Calls** | Complex problem-solving, emotional conversations, 1-on-1 coaching | Scheduled in advance | Camera on by default to encourage connection, unless pre-stated. |
| **Phone Call / SMS** | Critical, systemic emergencies (system down, client crisis) | Immediate | Use ONLY in emergency. Call twice if urgent. |

---

## 3. Synchronous Meeting Protocol
*To ensure meetings are high-leverage events rather than distractions:*
*   **The 24-Hour Agenda Rule:** No meeting can be scheduled without a populated agenda linked in the calendar invite 24 hours prior. If no agenda is present, invitees are encouraged to decline.
*   **Focus Block Protection:** Wednesdays are designated "Meeting-Free Deep Work Days." No internal team meetings may be scheduled on this day.
*   **Duration Default:** Meetings default to 25 minutes or 50 minutes to allow transition buffers.

---

## 4. Operational Decision-Making Framework
*We define how decisions are categorized and made to prevent gridlock:*

| Decision Category | Decision Owner | Consultation Level | Agreement Method |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Daily Tactical Tasks** | Individual Contributor | Inform others via Slack status | Full Autonomy |
| **Feature / Project Scope** | Product/Project Lead | Consult technical and design leads | Consult-and-Decide (Lead decides after feedback) |
| **Strategic Hiring/Budget** | Team Manager | Consult entire executive team | Consensus (70% agreement required) |

---

## 5. Conflict Resolution & Psychological Safety Covenant
*We resolve friction constructively using these three commitments:*
*   **Commitment 1: Assume Positive Intent.** We operate under the belief that everyone is trying to do their best for the project, even when we disagree.
*   **Commitment 2: Direct-to-Source Resolution.** If a team member has an issue with another, they must discuss it directly with them in private before escalating to a manager or talking to peers.
*   **Commitment 3: Disagree and Commit.** We voice our opinions passionately during discussions, but once a decision is finalized, we all commit 100% to its execution, regardless of our original stance.
</output_format>

Construct this Team Charter with high organizational foresight, giving the team a robust, practical playbook to foster safety, trust, and execution velocity.

## Variables
- {TEAM_MISSION} – The name of the team and their overriding goal (e.g., Acme Customer Success Team, focused on delivering unparalleled support and reducing churn to <2% annual).
- {TEAM_COMPOSITION} – The roles, departments, or geographical locations (e.g., 6 engineers, 2 product designers, split between Eastern US and Western Europe).
- {COLLABORATION_TOOLS} – The digital workspaces currently in use (e.g., Slack, Notion, Jira, Zoom, Outlook Email).
- {CULTURAL_FRICTIONS} – Past issues, communication gaps, or points of tension (e.g., "People are pinging Slack at 11 PM expecting immediate replies, too many unstructured meetings, confusion on who owns strategic decisions").

## Notes
- A Team Charter is an active, breathing social contract. It should not be written by the manager alone; it must be reviewed, edited, and signed off collaboratively by the entire team.
- Highlight the Slack/Teams SLA; defining this single variable is the most effective way to cure Slack anxiety and give engineers and designers their focus blocks back.
- Schedule a quick 10-minute review of the Charter every 6 months to ensure the rules still fit the team's scale and challenges.
