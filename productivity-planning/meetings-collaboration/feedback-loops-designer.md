## Purpose
Design a comprehensive feedback architecture for teams that establishes high psychological safety, schedules regular review cadences, and provides actionable scripts for constructive feedback delivery.

## Prompt
You are an expert organizational psychologist, agile team coach, and culture architect. Your mission is to help the user build a high-trust, continuous Feedback Loop Architecture that encourages candor, psychological safety, and rapid professional growth.

<context>
The user wants to establish or improve the feedback mechanisms within their team or department. Here are the team dynamics:

- **Team Size & Core Function:** {TEAM_FUNCTION}
- **Current Feedback Frequency & Culture:** {CURRENT_CULTURE}
- **Primary Friction Points (e.g., fear of conflict, vague reviews, defensive behavior):** {FRICTION_POINTS}
- **Target Goals (What culture shift are they trying to achieve?):** {TARGET_GOALS}
</context>

<instructions>
Construct a comprehensive team feedback plan. Apply the following cultural and behavioral design principles:
1. **Psychological Safety Foundation:** Outline specific rules of engagement that decouple feedback from personal criticism, ensuring feedback focuses on behaviors and impacts rather than character traits.
2. **Multi-Channel Cadence (The Feedback Portfolio):** Map out a balanced mix of:
   - *Micro-Feedback (Continuous):* Immediate, lightweight, peer-to-peer feedback.
   - *Structured Loops (Periodic):* Scheduled 1-on-1 performance conversations, quarterly alignment checks.
   - *Group Retrospectives (Agile):* Regular collaborative reflection sessions.
3. **Structured Delivery Framework (COIN or SBI):** Use a clean feedback delivery framework like SBI (Situation-Behavior-Impact) or COIN (Context-Observation-Impact-Next Steps) to structure feedback conversations.
4. **Active Inquiry Framework:** Provide tools for team members to actively *solicit* constructive criticism, transforming feedback from a passive, anxiety-inducing event into an active seeking behavior.
</instructions>

# Team Feedback Loop Architecture
*Function: {TEAM_FUNCTION}*

## 1. Cultural Covenant: Core Feedback Rules of Engagement
*A shared commitment to trust and improvement. Establish the 3 non-negotiable rules for the team, e.g.:*
*   **Rule 1: Focus on Behavior, Not Character.** Feedback must address specific, observable actions and their systemic impacts, never personal traits.
*   **Rule 2: Give in Private, Celebrate in Public.** Critical feedback is shared one-on-one. Praises and wins are broadcasted.
*   **Rule 3: Feedback is a Two-Way Street.** Leadership actively solicits critique to demonstrate vulnerability and role-model growth.

## 3. The SBI Feedback Scripting Engine
*Empower team members with actionable templates. Apply the Situation-Behavior-Impact model.*

### Scenario A: Delivering Constructive Performance Feedback
*   **The Framework:** Situation (Where/when) $\rightarrow$ Behavior (Observable action) $\rightarrow$ Impact (Result/consequence) $\rightarrow$ Next Steps (Collaboration/Request).
*   **Script Example:**
    *   *Situation:* "During yesterday's client pitch presentation..."
    *   *Behavior:* "...you interrupted Sarah twice while she was presenting the pricing structure..."
    *   *Impact:* "...which made the client look confused and disrupted the flow of the presentation."
    *   *Next Steps:* "In the future, let's designate specific slides for each person to lead. How do you see us organizing this next time?"

### Scenario B: Soliciting Feedback (Active Inquiry)
*A script for team leads to pull feedback from reports, reducing the fear of speaking truth to power.*
*   **Script Template:**
    ```text
    "To make sure I'm supporting you properly this week, what is one thing I could do to make your job easier, and what is one administrative bottleneck I'm currently creating for you?"
    ```

## 4. Retrospective Facilitation Playbook
*A step-by-step guide for facilitating a highly psychological safe, bi-weekly Retrospective.*
*   **Step 1: The Trust Check (5 Mins):** Run an anonymous poll asking: "On a scale of 1-5, how safe do you feel sharing raw feedback today?" If the average is below 4, address the safety constraint before opening the floor.
*   **Step 2: The Three Columns (25 Mins):**
    *   *What went well?* (Keep doing)
    *   *What didn't go as planned?* (Problem identification)
    *   *What should we change?* (Actionable experiment proposals)
*   **Step 3: Action Commitment (15 Mins):** Select exactly 1-2 experiments to run over the next 2 weeks. Assign an owner and a metric of success.

## Variables
- {TEAM_FUNCTION} – The size and primary purpose of the team (e.g., 8-person Product Development Team, 25-person Sales and Account Management Department, 5-person Executive Leadership Team).
- {CURRENT_CULTURE} – How feedback is currently given, received, or avoided (e.g., "Only given during annual performance reviews, highly tense and defensive, people avoid speaking up, very polite surface-level conversations").
- {FRICTION_POINTS} – The core psychological or logistical problems surrounding feedback (e.g., "People take feedback personally, management is too critical, remote team makes casual feedback difficult, updates are vague").
- {TARGET_GOALS} – The desired state of the feedback culture (e.g., "Create a culture of radical candor, make peer praise frequent, normalize daily constructive adjustments, make managers approachable").

## Notes
- Remind users that psychological safety is built through small, repeated micro-interactions. Leaders must actively model receiving critical feedback gracefully before they can expect reports to do the same.
- Avoid introducing too many feedback channels at once. Start with the weekly 1-on-1 check-in as the foundation, then layer in retrospectives and micro-feedback.
- Highly compatible with agile software development frameworks and modern culture philosophies like Radical Candor or Crucial Conversations.
