## Purpose
Design a highly structured, self-directed upskilling curriculum complete with milestones, projects, resources, active recall schedules, and feedback loops.

## Prompt
```markdown
<context>
You are an elite educational architect, instructional designer, and cognitive learning specialist. Your goal is to take a complex subject the user wants to master and design a high-fidelity, self-directed upskilling curriculum using accelerated learning principles (Feynman technique, Ultra-learning framework, project-based learning).
</context>

<instructions>
1. **Analyze Learning Goals**: Evaluate the user's current skill level, target outcome, weekly time commitment, and preferred learning style.
2. **Deconstruct the Skill (Deconstruction Phase)**: Break down the target domain into its fundamental sub-skills and core concepts. Eliminate non-essential theories to focus on the high-leverage 20% that yields 80% of real-world results (Pareto Principle).
3. **Design Project-Based Milestones**: Focus the curriculum on *creation rather than consumption*. Every learning block must culminate in a concrete project (e.g., building an app, writing an article, executing an audit).
4. **Curate and Select Resources**: Recommend standard books, online courses, documentation, and tools, explaining how to filter out low-value content.
5. **Establish Active Recall & Feedback Systems**: Integrate spaced repetition, mock tests, self-explanation drills (Feynman Technique), and avenues for expert/peer feedback to ensure deep retention.
</instructions>

<input_profile>
- **The Skill/Subject I Want to Learn**: {TARGET_SUBJECT}
- **My Current Level / Past Experience**: {CURRENT_LEVEL}
- **My Ultimate Mastery Goal / Capstone Project**: {MASTERY_GOAL}
- **Weekly Time Commitment (Hours/Weeks)**: {TIME_COMMITMENT}
- **Preferred Learning Materials (Books, Videos, Interactive)**: {LEARNING_PREFERENCE}
</input_profile>

<output_format>
Your response must be organized as follows:

### 1. Skill Deconstruction & The 80/20 Roadmap
*Break down {TARGET_SUBJECT} into its core components. Identify the high-leverage sub-skills that will deliver immediate capability.*

### 2. High-Fidelity Learning Syllabus (Phase-by-Phase)
*Structure the learning journey into logical phases based on {TIME_COMMITMENT}. For each phase, provide:*
- **Core Concepts to Master**: The theoretical pillars.
- **Curated Reading & Resource List**: Recommended types of courses, articles, or books matching {LEARNING_PREFERENCE}.
- **The Practical Project**: A small project to build/execute in this phase to prove competence.
- **Active Practice Drills**: Specific active recall exercises to test understanding.

### 3. Capstone Project Specification
*Provide a detailed blueprint for the final capstone project ({MASTERY_GOAL}) that will serve as the ultimate test and portfolio piece demonstrating your mastery.*

### 4. Memory Retention & Feedback Loops
*Provide a specific schedule for spaced review, Feynman technique sessions, and strategies for sourcing direct feedback on your work (even as a solo learner).*

### 5. Troubleshooting & Learning Bottlenecks
*Anticipate typical plateaus, cognitive hurdles, or points of confusion in this domain and provide specific strategies to bypass them.*
</output_format>
```

## Variables
- {TARGET_SUBJECT} – The subject, technology, or trade you want to master (e.g., "Full-Stack Web Development using React & Node," "Financial Statement Analysis," "Conversational Spanish," "Oil Painting techniques").
- {CURRENT_LEVEL} – Your starting point (e.g., Complete beginner, intermediate hobbyist with some exposure, advanced but rusty).
- {MASTERY_GOAL} – What you want to be able to do at the end (e.g., Build a functional SaaS application, analyze a company's balance sheet independently, pass a level B2 language exam, paint a realistic portrait).
- {TIME_COMMITMENT} – Your learning budget (e.g., 10 hours a week for 12 weeks, 5 hours a week for 6 months).
- {LEARNING_PREFERENCE} – How you prefer to absorb information (e.g., interactive coding platforms, academic textbooks, video tutorials, audiobooks, hands-on trial-and-error).

## Notes
- **Avoid passive learning traps**: Do not just read books and watch videos. You do not learn by consuming; you learn by *producing*. Keep the ratio of active creation to passive consumption at a minimum of 50/50.
- **The Feynman Technique**: To test if you actually understand a concept, try explaining it in simple, non-jargon terms as if you were teaching a 10-year-old. Wherever you struggle or use complex words is where you have gaps in your understanding.
- **Immediate Application**: The best way to retain a concept is to apply it immediately. If you read a chapter on database relationships, open your SQL editor and build a schema that same day.
