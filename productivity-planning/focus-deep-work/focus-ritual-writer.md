## Purpose
Drafts highly customized, step-by-step physical and cognitive scripts for your Morning Launch, Inter-Task Reset, and Evening Shutdown rituals to ensure clean transitions between work and rest.

## Prompt
<context>
You are an expert behavioral architect, flow state coach, and human performance researcher. Your focus is studying high-performers and designing customized routines that minimize cognitive friction and manage mental energy throughout the day. You know that successful people rely on structured, repeatable transition rituals to signal to their nervous system exactly when to ramp up focus and when to shut it down.
</context>

<task>
Write a set of three highly customized Focus Ritual Scripts (Morning Launch, Inter-Task Reset, Evening Shutdown) based on the user's workflow, environment, and personal bottlenecks.

Ensure your designed rituals are highly physical, immediate, and actionable. Map them across these three distinct periods:
1. **The Morning Launch Ritual (15 Minutes)**: A routine executed first thing upon sitting at the workspace. It must bypass early morning procrastination, align focus with the day's primary objective, and prepare the physical environment.
2. **The Inter-Task Reset Ritual (5 Minutes)**: A rapid clearing routine to execute when switching from a high-focus creation task to a different task. This ritual should clean the cognitive slate, flush "attention residue," and refresh physical posture.
3. **The Workday Shutdown Ritual (10 Minutes)**: A routine to execute at the end of the working day. It must close outstanding open loops, record progress to reduce cognitive anxiety, and cleanly disconnect the user's mind, allowing them to rest and enjoy their personal life.
</task>

<inputs>
- **Typical Workday Schedule & Setup**: {WORKDAY_SETUP}
- **Morning Focus Bottleneck (E.g., opening social media, feeling overwhelmed by email)**: {MORNING_BOTTLENECK}
- **End-of-Day Bottleneck (E.g., thinking about work all night, laptop stays open on the couch)**: {SHUTDOWN_BOTTLENECK}
- **Energy Peaks & Troughs throughout the day**: {ENERGY_PROFILE}
- **Sensory & Physical Preferences (E.g., coffee, stretching, music, walking, journaling)**: {USER_PREFERENCES}
</inputs>

## 2. The Morning Launch Ritual (Duration: 15 Mins)
*Execute this ritual immediately upon sitting down at your desk. Do not open your email or browser first.*

### Step-by-Step Sequence:
| Time | Action | Physical Modality | Step-by-Step Instructions |
|---|---|---|---|
| **0-3 Mins** | **Physical Prep** | Tactile / Visual | - Clear everything off your desk except your notepad and keyboard.<br>- Fill a glass of water/pour your drink of choice.<br>- Adjust your seat and posture. |
| **3-8 Mins** | **Strategic Alignment** | Cognitive / Written | - Write down the **single most important outcome** for today on a physical index card or notepad.<br>- List the 3 micro-steps required to start that task. |
| **8-13 Mins** | **Sensory Activation** | Auditory / Visual | - Put on your noise-canceling headphones.<br>- Queue your dedicated deep focus playlist.<br>- Close all browser windows unrelated to the first task. |
| **13-15 Mins** | **Launch Affirmation** | Verbal / Physical | - Say out loud or think: "My focus is single-threaded. My phone is away. I am starting [Task Name] now."<br>- Immediately open the first file and begin. |

## 3. The Inter-Task Reset Ritual (Duration: 5 Mins)
*Execute this short ritual when switching between major tasks to flush "attention residue."*

- **Minute 1: The Progress Log**: Open your notepad. Write 1-2 quick sentences on exactly where you are stopping, what needs to happen next, and where the files are located.
- **Minute 2: Physical Disconnect**: Close all open browser tabs from the finished task. Close the software application.
- **Minute 3: Re-Oxygenate**: Stand up from your chair. Walk for 60 seconds (around your room, office, or hallway). Take 3 deep belly breaths. Stretch your neck and shoulders.
- **Minute 4: Hydration & Sensory Shift**: Drink a glass of water. If switching task types, change your background audio (e.g., switch from fast electronic music to quiet ambient noise).
- **Minute 5: Target Initialization**: Sit back down. Open *only* the applications required for the next task. Define your next action. Begin.

## 4. The Workday Shutdown Ritual (Duration: 10 Mins)
*Execute this ritual exactly 15 minutes before you want to stop working. This provides a clean mental separation between work and life.*

### Step-by-Step Sequence:
- [ ] **Step 1: The Master Task Review (3 Mins)**: Look at your calendar and task manager. Move any unfinished tasks from today to tomorrow or next week. Do not leave them lingering.
- [ ] **Step 2: Write tomorrow's "Top 3" list (2 Mins)**: Write down the top 3 high-impact tasks you want to tackle tomorrow. This offloads them from your subconscious.
- [ ] **Step 3: Clear the Workspace (2 Mins)**: Tidy your desk. Put away loose papers, coffee cups, or pens. Close all browser tabs. Shutdown your computer or put it to sleep.
- [ ] **Step 4: The Physical Break Ceremony (3 Mins)**: Perform a physical action that signals to your body that you are off the clock.
  - *Action*: [E.g., physically closing your laptop lid, putting your work badge in a drawer, changing your clothes, or taking a 5-minute walk outside.]
  - *Verbal Shutdown Cue*: Say out loud: "The workday is complete. I am stepping away from work now. My mind is free."

## Variables
- {WORKDAY_SETUP} – Your normal work hours, whether you work from home or an office, and what your physical desk looks like.
- {MORNING_BOTTLENECK} – The habits that derail your focus in the morning (e.g., "Answering emails immediately, which gets me stuck in firefighting mode," or "Checking social media and losing 30 minutes").
- {SHUTDOWN_BOTTLENECK} – The problems that prevent a clean finish to your day (e.g., "Thinking about code bugs all evening," "Checking Slack on my phone on the couch," "Answering emails at 9 PM").
- {ENERGY_PROFILE} – When your mental energy spikes and drops throughout the day (e.g., "High focus 8-11 AM, massive slump 2-4 PM, second energy wave 7-9 PM").
- {USER_PREFERENCES} – Things that help you shift states (e.g., "Drinking peppermint tea," "Listening to movie soundtracks," "Doing light yoga stretches," "Writing in a paper journal").

## Notes
- **Never Check Slack First**: The most destructive morning habit is checking your communication channels before you have set your priorities. It immediately puts you in a reactive, survival state, letting other people dictate your energy.
- **The physical shutdown signal**: Having a physical action (like turning off a desk lamp or changing your shirt) is critical. It grounds the mental transition in physical reality, signaling the parasympathetic nervous system that it is safe to down-regulate.
- **Model Recommendation**: Works beautifully with conversational models like Claude 3.5 Sonnet, GPT-4, or Gemini 1.5 Pro due to their ability to weave narrative structure and empathetic habit coaching.
