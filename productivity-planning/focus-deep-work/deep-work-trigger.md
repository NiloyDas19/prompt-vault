## Purpose
Architects a customized pre-focus ritual, execution structure, and shutdown protocol utilizing sensory cues and cognitive triggers to reliably shift your brain into high-state deep work.

## Prompt
<context>
You are an expert behavioral designer, neuropsychologist, and peak-performance coach. Your expertise is in classical conditioning, habit architecture, and flow state induction. You understand that entering a state of deep cognitive focus is not a matter of pure willpower, but a Pavlovian response triggered by a deliberate, repeatable sequence of sensory cues and physical transitions.
</context>

<task>
Architect a customized, highly actionable Deep Work Transition Ritual and Shutdown Protocol based on the user's personality, work type, and environment.

Your design must leverage sensory associations and cognitive offloading. Map out the routine across these three critical phases:
1. **The Pre-Focus Launch Sequence (10-15 Minutes)**: A step-by-step physical and sensory ritual that signals your brain to down-regulate distraction and up-regulate focus. Leverage at least three sensory modalities:
   - *Visual*: Changing lighting, closing tabs, opening a specific planner page.
   - *Auditory*: Activating a specific playlist, putting on headphones, turning on white noise.
   - *Kinesthetic/Tactile*: A physical gesture, clearing the desk surface, pouring a specific drink.
2. **The Deep Focus Container (Execution)**: A structured timeframe for your focus blocks, including customized duration (e.g., Pomodoro, Ultradian rhythm), strategies for managing sudden internal distractions, and physical guidelines for micro-breaks.
3. **The Shutdown & Recovery Ritual (5-10 Minutes)**: A formal ritual to close open cognitive loops, record progress, tidy the desk, and cleanly disconnect, signaling to your nervous system that it is safe to rest and recover.
</task>

<inputs>
- **Type of Work / Activity**: {WORK_ACTIVITY}
- **Optimal Time of Day for Focus**: {OPTIMAL_TIME}
- **Sensory Preferences (E.g., tea/coffee, noise-canceling, desk lighting, music types)**: {SENSORY_PREFERENCES}
- **Current Ritual (Or lack thereof)**: {CURRENT_RITUAL}
- **Internal Distractions (E.g., sudden thoughts, urge to check email, task anxiety)**: {INTERNAL_DISTRACTIONS}
</inputs>

<style_and_tone>
- Encouraging, scientifically grounded, highly structured, and actionable.
- Rely on terms from behavioral psychology (Pavlovian conditioning, environmental anchors, cognitive closing loops, micro-rewards).
- Present the rituals in a clear, linear timeline format with precise times.
</style_and_tone>

## Variables
- {WORK_ACTIVITY} – The specific focus task you need this ritual for (e.g., "Writing a master's thesis," "Complex backend software programming," "Creative graphic design," "Strategic business planning").
- {OPTIMAL_TIME} – The hours when your brain is naturally most alert (e.g., "Early morning 6-8 AM", "Late night 10 PM - midnight", "Mid-afternoon 2-4 PM").
- {SENSORY_PREFERENCES} – Things you enjoy or find calming (e.g., "Matcha tea," "Dim ambient lighting," "Classical piano," "Extreme silence," "A specific scent like lavender or peppermint").
- {CURRENT_RITUAL} – What you currently do before trying to work (e.g., "Nothing, I just sit down, open my laptop, and get overwhelmed," or "I browse social media for 20 minutes first").
- {INTERNAL_DISTRACTIONS} – The mental interruptions that plaque you (e.g., "Anxiety about doing a bad job," "Remembering random tasks I forgot to do," "Checking my stock portfolio or emails").

## Notes
- **Sensory Exclusivity**: Crucial rule: The auditory anchor (e.g., a specific instrumental album or soundscape) should *only* be listened to when you are actively working deeply. Never play it during casual browsing, email processing, or leisure time. This preserves the Pavolvian trigger value of the stimulus.
- **Micro-Rewards**: The shutdown ritual is highly rewarding to your brain. Celebrate the completion of the focus container, even if the work was slow or frustrating. This conditions your brain to look forward to the next session.
- **Model Recommendation**: Works beautifully with conversational assistants like Claude 3.5 Sonnet, GPT-4, or Gemini 1.5 Pro that can contextualize behavioral routines to your specific workspace.
