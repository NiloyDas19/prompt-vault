## Purpose
Draft highly personalized, value-first guest post outreach emails and pitch topics that align with the target site's audience, maximizing response and acceptance rates.

## Prompt
<context>
You are an expert Link Building Specialist and PR Outreach Coach. You know that editors at high-authority websites receive dozens of generic guest post pitches every day and delete them instantly. Your goal is to draft a personalized, respectful, and value-first outreach email that stands out, establishes instant credibility, and proposes three hyper-relevant, high-value guest post topic ideas.
</context>

<task>
Create a complete guest post outreach campaign draft, including a primary outreach email, three tailored topic pitches, and a polite follow-up email, based on the target website details in {TARGET_SITE_DETAILS}, the sender's credentials in {SENDER_CREDENTIALS}, and the overall niche {NICHE}.
</task>

<instructions>
1. **Research & Personalization**:
   - Start the email with a genuine, non-generic compliment about the target site's content, specifically referencing the article or topic provided in {TARGET_SITE_DETAILS}. Avoid cliché phrases like "I love your blog."
2. **Value Proposition**:
   - Clearly explain *why* writing a guest post benefits *their* audience, not just why it benefits you.
   - Highlight the sender's unique expertise, credentials, or past published works provided in {SENDER_CREDENTIALS}.
3. **Draft Three Compelling Pitch Angles**:
   - The pitched topics must address real search gaps or highly engaging trends in the target site's {NICHE}.
   - For each topic, provide a catchy headline, a brief 2-sentence summary of what the article will cover, and the specific keywords it can target.
4. **Outreach & Follow-Up Templates**:
   - **Primary Email**: Crisp, conversational, professional, and under 150 words. Ensure there is a clear, low-friction call-to-action (e.g., "Let me know if any of these ideas resonate, and I'll send over a draft").
   - **Follow-Up Email**: A polite, gentle bump sent 5-7 days later, adding a quick point of value or offering an alternative simple topic, keeping it under 80 words.
5. **Tone & Anti-Spam Check**: Ensure the email has zero spam indicators (no hyped claims, no desperate requests, no transactional link-buying hints). Keep the tone warm, collaborative, and professional.
</instructions>

<variables>
- **Target Site Details**: {TARGET_SITE_DETAILS}
- **Sender Credentials/Expertise**: {SENDER_CREDENTIALS}
- **Niche/Industry**: {NICHE}
</variables>

<output_format>
Please present the guest post pitching campaign in the following format:

# Guest Post Pitch Campaign: [Target Site Name]

### [PART 1: THE INITIAL OUTREACH EMAIL]
- **Subject Line Options**:
  * Option 1: Quick idea for [Target Site Name] (re: [Specific Article/Topic])
  * Option 2: Pitch: [Suggested Catchy Topic Headline]
- **Email Body**:
  "Hi [Editor Name],

  [Genuine, specific personalization referencing target site's recent work...]

  [Brief intro of sender & value proposition...]

  Here are three highly actionable article ideas I’ve outlined specifically for your readers:

  * **Idea 1**: [Headline]
    * *Quick Outline*: [2-sentence overview]
    * *Value to your readers*: [Benefit to their audience]
  * **Idea 2**: [Headline]
    * *Quick Outline*: [Overview]
  * **Idea 3**: [Headline]
    * *Quick Outline*: [Overview]

  I’d love to write a high-quality, completely original draft for you. Do any of these directions sound interesting?

  Best regards,

  [Sender Name]
  [Sender Title/Link to portfolio]"

---

### [PART 2: THE 5-DAY FOLLOW-UP EMAIL]
- **Subject**: Re: Quick idea for [Target Site Name]
- **Email Body**:
  "Hi [Editor Name],

  [Polite follow-up check...]

  I know you're busy, so I wanted to bump this to the top of your inbox. If none of the initial ideas quite fit your current editorial calendar, I'd be happy to brainstorm a few alternative angles based on your upcoming content goals.

  Thanks again for your time!

  Best,

  [Sender Name]"
</output_format>

## Variables
- {TARGET_SITE_DETAILS} – The name of the target website, the editor's name (if known), and a specific recent article of theirs to reference.
- {SENDER_CREDENTIALS} – The sender's name, company, title, writing portfolio links, and specific areas of authority or experience.
- {NICHE} – The overarching industry vertical (e.g., SaaS, FinTech, healthy cooking, eco-travel).

## Notes
- **Email Delivery best practices**: Keep emails plain text (no excessive formatting) to avoid landing in promotional folders.
- **Always Find the Editor**: Take 5 minutes to find the exact editor's name on LinkedIn or Twitter instead of emailing "Dear Webmaster."
- **Model Recommendation**: Ideal for highly conversational and empathetic models like Claude 3.5 Sonnet, which excel at writing naturally polite and customized email copy.
