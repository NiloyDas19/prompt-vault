## Purpose
Compile and organize a highly linkable, search-optimized statistics page or research compilation that serves as a magnet for organic backlinks from journalists, bloggers, and industry researchers.

## Prompt
<context>
You are an expert Research Analyst and SEO Link-Building Strategist. You know that statistics posts (e.g., "[Topic] Statistics: Key Facts & Trends") are among the most powerful passive link-building assets in existence. Writers, journalists, and bloggers search for "stats" to back up their articles, and they link directly to pages that compile these statistics clearly. Your goal is to draft a comprehensive, beautifully structured statistics compilation page.
</context>

<task>
Analyze the raw research data or topic provided in {RAW_DATA_OR_TOPIC}, organize it into clear categories, and write a high-ranking, shareable statistics resource optimized for the primary search query {PRIMARY_KEYWORD}.
</task>

<instructions>
1. **Categorize and Structure**:
   - Do not just dump a list of bullets. Group statistics into logical thematic categories (e.g., "General Market Size," "Customer Preferences," "Adoption Challenges," "Future Growth Projections").
   - Use clear H2 headings for each category.
2. **Draft the Content**:
   - **Main Title / H1**: Make it descriptive and highly searchable (e.g., "50+ Essential [Keyword] Statistics to Know in 2026").
   - **Intro**: Keep it very brief (under 100 words), highlighting why these stats matter right now.
   - **Key Takeaways Checklist**: A summarized box featuring the 5 most shocking or critical statistics for quick scanning.
   - **Categorized Lists**: Under each category H2, present the statistics with bolded key metrics (e.g., "**73% of executives** report...") and include clear attribution placeholders indicating the source and year.
   - **Analytical Commentary**: Write a brief paragraph (40-60 words) under each section explaining the *meaning* or *implication* of those trends. This adds unique value and prevents the page from looking like thin content.
3. **SEO & Linkability Optimization**:
   - Integrate {PRIMARY_KEYWORD} in the Title and early in the introduction.
   - Design each statistic to be easily tweetable or copy-pastable.
   - Provide a section with clear citation instructions, inviting other writers to easily link back to this source.
</instructions>

<variables>
- **Topic or Raw Data**: {RAW_DATA_OR_TOPIC}
- **Primary Keyword**: {PRIMARY_KEYWORD}
- **Secondary/LSI Keywords**: {SECONDARY_KEYWORDS}
- **Year/Context**: {YEAR_CONTEXT}
</variables>

<output_format>
Please present the statistics compilation draft in the format below:

- **Meta Description**: [Actionable summary highlighting top stats, under 155 chars]

---

[E.g., The Ultimate List of [Primary Keyword] Statistics for [Year]]

- **Stat 1**: **[Key Metric/Percentage]** of [Subject] [Action/Detail]. (Source: [Placeholder, Year])
- **Stat 2**: **[Key Metric]** [Details]. (Source: [Placeholder, Year])
- **Key Trend Analysis**: [50-word narrative summarizing what these stats mean for the industry]

## [H2 Category 2, e.g., Consumer Behavior & Preferences]
- **Stat 3**: [Stat detail]
- **Stat 4**: [Stat detail]
- **Key Trend Analysis**: [Narrative summary]

[Short guide explaining how researchers can cite and link back to this compilation, ensuring a high-quality backlink flow.]
</output_format>

## Variables
- {RAW_DATA_OR_TOPIC} – A dump of raw statistics, research reports, surveys, or a broad topic you want the AI to gather/organize based on internal/external knowledge.
- {PRIMARY_KEYWORD} – The main search term (e.g., "remote work statistics," "B2B SaaS churn rates").
- {SECONDARY_KEYWORDS} – Related LSI terms (e.g., "telecommuting trends," "remote employee data").
- {YEAR_CONTEXT} – The target year to make the statistics relevant, fresh, and highly searchable.

## Notes
- **E-E-A-T & Authority**: Always double-check and update the placeholder sources before publishing. Citing outdated sources hurts your site's credibility.
- **Visual Assets**: These pages benefit immensely from simple infographics or social share graphics. Add visual layout suggestions to the outline.
- **Model Recommendation**: Works exceptionally well with data-literate models like GPT-4o or Claude 3.5 Sonnet to ensure logical categorizations and strong analytical takeaways.
