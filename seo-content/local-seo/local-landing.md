## Purpose
Draft highly optimized, unique, and high-converting copy and structural layouts for location-specific landing pages targeting individual suburbs, cities, and neighborhood areas.

## Prompt
<context>
You are an expert Local Conversion Copywriter, geo-targeting strategist, and Local SEO Architect. Your specialty is creating location pages that rank for high-intent queries (e.g., "[Service] in [Suburb]") while completely avoiding search engine duplicate content penalties. You know how to balance local relevance (landmarks, community references) with persuasive sales copy to turn local web visitors into paying customers.
</context>

<task>
Create the full copy and layout blueprint for a localized, geo-targeted landing page. The page must be optimized for search engine crawl bots (structured data, semantic keywords, local citations) and meticulously designed to build trust and drive conversions in the target location.
</task>

<inputs>
- **Target Neighborhood, Suburb, or City:** {TARGET_LOCATION}
- **Core Service Offering & Industry:** {CORE_SERVICE}
- **Unique Selling Points & Value Props:** {VALUE_PROPS}
- **Local Landmarks, Roads & Regional Trust Details:** {LOCAL_LANDMARKS_TRUST}
- **Primary Conversion CTA (e.g., Book Online, Call Now):** {PRIMARY_CTA}
</inputs>

<instructions>
Using the inputs provided, write complete, production-ready landing page copy. Ensure it is organized into a clean, logical landing page skeleton by following these steps:

1. **Local Meta & On-Page SEO Optimization**:
   - Write a high-CTR Title Tag (under 60 characters) and Meta Description (under 155 characters) that integrate both the core service and target city/suburb.
   - Design a clear, logical URL slug (e.g., `/locations/target-location-service`).

2. **Hero Copy & Headline Architecture**:
   - Write an H1 that clearly combines the service name and the target geographic keyword (e.g., "Premier Plumbing Services in [Suburb] | Available 24/7").
   - Write a compelling subheadline that highlights key value props (speed, ratings, guarantees) and outlines the primary CTA.

3. **Localized Hook & Problem Statement**:
   - Draft a highly engaging introductory section tailored specifically to the residents of {TARGET_LOCATION}.
   - Mention localized issues (e.g., climate considerations, historic housing styles, soil types, or local traffic dynamics) to establish instant connection and credibility.

4. **Core Service Breakdown (Geo-Optimized)**:
   - Detail the primary services, framing their benefits to appeal to local residents.
   - Natural, flow-based inclusion of geo-modified terms (e.g., "our team provides expert [Service] across [Street Name] and surrounding areas near [Landmark]").

5. **Regional Relevance & trust Builder Section**:
   - Weave in {LOCAL_LANDMARKS_TRUST} seamlessly. Show that your business is deeply active in and physically knows {TARGET_LOCATION}.
   - Address common service areas, regional travel corridors, or local landmarks (e.g., "From [Subdivision A] up to [Major Road], we serve all neighborhoods surrounding [Local Park]").

6. **Local Proof, Testimonials, & FAQ Hub**:
   - Write 3 mock localized customer testimonials speaking to the exact location (e.g., "Excellent customer service here in [Neighborhood]!").
   - Create a 4-question FAQ block addressing local queries (pricing, availability in the suburb, licensing, local permits) using structured headers for schema friendliness.
</instructions>

<style_and_tone>
Write in a trustworthy, helpful, neighborhood-friendly, and authoritative tone. Avoid overly generic marketing speak. Make the copy sound like it was written by a local business owner who is an active member of the community.
</style_and_tone>

## Variables
- {TARGET_LOCATION} – The specific suburb, town, neighborhood, or city district being targeted (e.g., "Silver Lake", "Bellevue", "North End").
- {CORE_SERVICE} – The primary service you are trying to rank for on this specific page (e.g., "Residential Electrician", "Roof Replacement", "Pet Grooming").
- {VALUE_PROPS} – Key selling points of the company (e.g., "100% satisfaction guarantee, licensed and insured, over 15 years in business, background-checked employees").
- {LOCAL_LANDMARKS_TRUST} – Local geographic markers, famous parks, major highway names, high schools, or neighborhood subdivisions (e.g., "serving areas near Bellevue Downtown Park, Interstate 405, Somerset, and Crossroads Mall").
- {PRIMARY_CTA} – The primary action you want the visitor to take (e.g., "Book Your Inspection", "Get a Free Estimate", "Call Now").

## Notes
- **Duplicate Content vs. Programmatic Templates**: Caution the user against using identical, copy-pasted templates for 50 location pages where only the city name is swapped out. Search engines easily flag this as programmatic spam. Suggest rewriting at least 40% of the body text to address hyper-local factors.
- **Topographical Language**: Encourage the inclusion of terms like "down the road from", "across the street from", and naming actual highways to build spatial relations in Google’s Knowledge Graph.
- **Internal Link Equity**: Best practice is to link from the homepage or a top-level location directory to these geo-landing pages, and back to the main category pages to distribute crawl equity.
