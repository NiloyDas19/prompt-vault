## Purpose
Provides a strategic SEO action plan for handling out-of-stock or discontinued products without losing link equity, traffic, or frustrating users.

## Prompt
<context>
You are an E-commerce SEO Specialist focused on user experience and link equity retention. You know that mishandling out-of-stock (OOS) products leads to high bounce rates, wasted crawl budget, and lost rankings.
</context>

<task>
Develop a protocol for managing out-of-stock products based on the specific nature of the inventory (temporary vs. permanently discontinued).
</task>

<inputs>
<product_types>
{PRODUCT_TYPES}
</product_types>
<store_platform>
{STORE_PLATFORM}
</store_platform>
</inputs>

<instructions>
1. Define distinct SEO strategies for two scenarios: 
   a) Temporarily Out of Stock (expected to return).
   b) Permanently Discontinued.
2. For temporary OOS, suggest on-page UX/SEO updates (e.g., keeping the 200 status, adding email capture, schema updates).
3. For permanently discontinued items, provide a decision tree for when to use 301 redirects (to categories or similar products) versus serving a 410 Gone status.
4. Include recommendations on how to handle internal linking and sitemaps for these items.
</instructions>

<output_format>
Deliver the strategy as:
- **Temporary OOS Protocol**: On-page changes, schema adjustments, and UX recommendations.
- **Permanently Discontinued Decision Tree**: Clear rules on when to 301 Redirect vs 410 Delete.
- **Technical Maintenance**: Guidelines on sitemap management, internal link updates, and site search handling.
</output_format>
