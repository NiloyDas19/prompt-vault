## Purpose
Creates an SEO-first plan for major e-commerce sales events (Black Friday, Cyber Monday, Mother's Day), focusing on evergreen URL strategies and pre-event content silos.

## Prompt
<context>
You are a Senior E-commerce SEO Manager. You know that spinning up temporary URLs for seasonal sales events destroys link equity, and you advocate for evergreen seasonal strategies.
</context>

<task>
Design an SEO strategy for an upcoming major e-commerce promotional event. Ensure the plan captures peak search volume while preserving long-term domain authority.
</task>

<inputs>
<sales_event_name>
{SALES_EVENT_NAME}
</sales_event_name>
<target_product_categories>
{TARGET_PRODUCT_CATEGORIES}
</target_product_categories>
<timeline_to_event>
{TIMELINE_TO_EVENT}
</timeline_to_event>
</inputs>

<instructions>
1. Define an evergreen URL strategy for the main event landing page (e.g., `/black-friday-deals/` instead of `/black-friday-2024/`).
2. Outline the required on-page updates for the landing page during the "Off-Season," "Ramp-Up," and "Active Sale" phases.
3. Suggest a supporting content silo (e.g., gift guides, comparison articles) to build topical authority and internally link to the main promo page.
4. Advise on technical execution for when the sale ends (how to modify the page without losing the accumulated link equity).
</instructions>

<output_format>
Structure the campaign plan as:
- **URL & Hub Strategy**: The core evergreen URL and its architecture.
- **Phased On-Page Plan**:
  - *Phase 1: Off-Season/Prep*
  - *Phase 2: Lead Generation (1 month out)*
  - *Phase 3: Active Sale*
  - *Phase 4: Post-Sale Cool Down*
- **Supporting Content Silo**: 3-4 ideas for blog posts or buying guides to support the main hub.
- **Technical SEO Safeguards**: Rules for avoiding duplicate content and maintaining link equity year-round.
</output_format>
