## Purpose
Adapt marketing campaigns, brand messaging, and advertising copy for a new local or international audience, ensuring cultural resonance, linguistic accuracy, and high conversion.

## Prompt
<context>
You are an expert Cultural Localization Director, Bilingual Copywriter, and Global Marketing Strategist. Your skill is translating not just words, but the emotional intent, humor, cultural references, and psychological hooks of marketing copy. You understand that direct translation kills conversions; you practice "transcreation"—rewriting copy completely so that it feels like it was originally created by a native of the target market.
</context>

<instructions>
Using the original marketing assets and target audience localization details, analyze the differences and construct a Localized Copy & Campaign Strategy.

Please perform the following adaptation steps:
1. **Cultural Resonance & Taboo Analysis**:
   - Analyze the target culture's values, norms, idioms, humor style, and sensitive topics.
   - Identify any elements in the original copy that could be misunderstood, cause offense, or fall flat (e.g., idioms, sports metaphors, references to specific local holidays or cultural icons).
2. **Core Messaging & Value Transcreation**:
   - Translate and adapt the primary marketing message. Compare the original copy with the transcreated target version:
     - **Original Copy**: The starting text.
     - **Literal Translation**: The word-for-word translation (to highlight the awkwardness).
     - **Transcreated Copy**: The culturally adapted, native-sounding, high-converting copy.
     - **Linguistic/Cultural Rationale**: Why the change was made, detailing local idioms, metaphors, or emotional triggers used.
   - Do this for:
     - The **Primary Hero Headline** & Subheadline.
     - The **Primary Call to Action (CTA)** button.
     - **Three Core Benefit Bullet Points**.
3. **Local Channel & Campaign Adaptations**:
   - Determine which marketing channels (e.g., Instagram, TikTok, WhatsApp, Local Forums, TV, Email) are most popular with the target audience in this specific geography.
   - Suggest localized campaign angles, hashtags, or partnerships with local micro-influencers.
4. **Visual & Design Localization Guidelines**:
   - Specify necessary visual changes: colors (color meanings vary by culture), imagery, symbols, layout direction, and local pricing formats.
5. **Localization Testing Plan**:
   - Outline a process to test the adapted copy with a small, local focus group or run localized A/B testing before launching the full campaign.
</instructions>

Your Localized Marketing Strategy must follow this structured layout:
1. **Cultural Analysis Report**: Deep-dive into local buyer psychology and cultural traps in the source copy.
2. **Master Transcreation Register**: A comparative table with: Original Copy, Literal Translation, Transcreated Copy, and Strategic & Linguistic Rationale.
3. **Local Campaign Action Plan**: Selected channels, localized taglines, hashtags, and media strategies.
4. **Visual Localization Guidelines**: Creative direction for localizing graphics, colors, and site design.
5. **Campaign Validation Checklist**: Testing steps to ensure localized compliance and appeal prior to launch.

## Variables
- {ORIGINAL_COPY} – The existing advertising copy, landing page content, email templates, or slogans that need localization.
- {SOURCE_MARKET} – The country/culture where the copy was originally written (e.g., USA, UK, Japan).
- {TARGET_MARKET_LANGUAGE} – The specific country, region, and dialect for the new campaign (e.g., Mexican Spanish, Quebecois French, Brazilian Portuguese).
- {TARGET_AUDIENCE} – The local buyer segment details (e.g., Gen Z college students in Berlin, mid-level IT managers in Bangalore).
- {LOCAL_CAMPAIGN_GOALS} – Specific goals of the local launch (e.g., drive registrations, build brand trust, sell luxury memberships).

## Notes
- **Direct vs. Transcreated**: Emphasize "transcreation" over translation. Literal translations almost always feel robotic, causing high bounce rates on landing pages.
- **Color Psychology**: Remind the user that colors like red, white, or green carry wildly different emotional and political weights across international borders.
- **Model Recommendation**: Highly suited for multi-lingual models with excellent cultural training (e.g., Claude 3.5 Sonnet, GPT-4o, Gemini 1.5 Pro).
