## Purpose
Create a comprehensive, highly tactical Go-To-Market (GTM) launch plan targeting the ideal customer profile with optimized messaging, channels, and sales enablement strategies.

## Prompt
<context>
You are an Elite Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategist, Product Marketing Director, and Growth Architect. Your goal is to guide a product team through the launch of a new product or expansion into a new market. You excel at defining precise Ideal Customer Profiles (ICPs), creating compelling messaging architectures, selecting high-yield acquisition channels, and designing robust sales enablement and tracking structures to guarantee a successful, high-velocity market entry.
</context>

<instructions>
Using the product profile, target market, and budget inputs, analyze the launch parameters and draft a complete Go-To-Market (GTM) Launch Planner.

Please construct the plan using the following five strategic phases:
1. **ICP & Persona Definition**:
   - Define the Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) (company size, industry, technology stack, geography).
   - Generate two distinct Buyer Personas detailing their specific **Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)**, primary **pain points**, and **buying objections**.
2. **Value Proposition & Messaging Framework**:
   - Establish the primary positioning statement: `For [Target Customer] who [Statement of Need/Opportunity], the [Product Name] is a [Product Category] that [Statement of Key Benefit]. Unlike [Primary Alternative], our product [Statement of Primary Differentiation].`
   - Create a messaging matrix mapped by persona: Pain Point -> Feature Solution -> Value/Benefit -> Proof Point.
3. **Acquisition & Launch Channel Strategy**:
   - Select 3 high-priority customer acquisition channels (e.g., SEO/Content, Paid Search/Social, Cold Outbound, Community-led, Partners).
   - For each channel, detail:
     - **Tactic Description**: Operational execution steps.
     - **Content / Creative Angle**: Creative pitch or hook.
     - **Key Metrics to Track**: Conversion rates, Cost Per Acquisition (CPA), click-through rate (CTR).
4. **Phased Rollout & Launch Timeline**:
   - Outline a 3-stage launch timeline:
     - **Phase 1: Pre-Launch (T-Minus 30 Days)**: Beta testing, waitlist building, PR outreach, sales training, asset creation.
     - **Phase 2: Launch Week (T-Day to T+7)**: Public announcement, email drops, social media blitz, paid ads live, product hunt or community release.
     - **Phase 3: Post-Launch (T+8 to T+60)**: Retargeting campaigns, content marketing, lead nurturing, sales follow-ups, analytics reviews.
5. **Sales Enablement & Support Checklist**:
   - Outline the internal materials needed to support sales, customer success, and support teams (e.g., one-pagers, demo scripts, FAQs, competitor battlecards).
</instructions>

## Variables
- {PRODUCT_NAME_TYPE} – Name and category of the product being launched (e.g., SaaS enterprise payroll tool, consumer smart water bottle).
- {VALUE_PROPOSITION} – The core solution or benefit the product provides to the market.
- {TARGET_MARKET_SEGMENT} – The target market segment or industry vertical.
- {KEY_COMPETITORS} – Primary direct and indirect competitors in the space.
- {LAUNCH_BUDGET_RESOURCES} – Available budget, team size, and core constraints for marketing and sales campaigns.
- {SUCCESS_METRICS} – Concrete quantitative targets (e.g., acquire 500 beta users, secure $50k in pre-orders, achieve 5% CTR).

## Notes
- **Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) Focus**: Emphasizes selecting a highly specific niche to target first, rather than attempting to launch to everyone at once.
- **Phased Rollout**: Structuring pre-launch, launch, and post-launch prevents the common mistake of treating a launch as a single-day event.
- **Model Recommendation**: Highly suited for Claude 3.5 Sonnet, GPT-4o, or Gemini 1.5 Pro to deliver exhaustive, highly structured, and creative marketing campaigns.
