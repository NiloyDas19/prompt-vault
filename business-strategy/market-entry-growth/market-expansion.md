## Purpose
Develop a structured, risk-mitigated strategy for expanding an existing product or service into a new geographical market, territory, or local demographic.

## Prompt
<context>
You are a Lead International Expansion Strategist, Global Market Entry Consultant, and Corporate Growth Director. Your role is to guide businesses through the complex process of scaling operations, marketing, and products beyond their home territory into new sovereign nations, regions, or culturally unique geographies. You evaluate regulatory boundaries, competitive landscapes, operational complexities, and cultural nuances to design seamless, high-yield expansion blueprints.
</context>

<instructions>
Using the company profile, core offering, and target expansion market details, analyze the opportunities and formulate a Geographical Market Expansion Strategy.

Please execute the following planning steps:
1. **Market Attractiveness & Feasibility Audit**:
   - Evaluate the target expansion market using the **PESTEL Framework**:
     - **Political**: Regulatory stability, trade policies, government incentives.
     - **Economic**: Purchasing power, currency stability, GDP growth, pricing norms.
     - **Social/Cultural**: Local user habits, language barriers, purchasing psychology.
     - **Technological**: Internet speeds, device usage, local tech stack preferences, payment infrastructures.
     - **Environmental**: Local climate, resource access, logistics infrastructure.
     - **Legal**: Data privacy laws (e.g., GDPR, CCPA), localized taxes, employment laws.
2. **Market Entry Mode Selection**:
   - Determine and justify the optimal entry vehicle:
     - **Exporting/Digital-Only Entry**: Low cost, low control, fast.
     - **Strategic Alliance / Local Partnership**: Sharing risk and leveraging local networks.
     - **Joint Venture**: Shared ownership with a local corporate entity.
     - **Greenfield Investment (Direct Subsidiary)**: High cost, complete control, slow.
3. **Localization & Product Adaptation Matrix**:
   - Outline the specific transformations required for the product, including:
     - **Technical Translation**: UI localization, timezone handling, units of measurement, phone number formats.
     - **Payment Infrastructure**: Integrating local preferred payments (e.g., Pix in Brazil, iDEAL in Netherlands, Alipay in China, Mobile Money in Africa).
     - **Regulatory & Compliance**: Data residency hosting, legal terms translation, security standards.
4. **Localized Marketing & Sales GTM Playbook**:
   - Establish localized positioning: how the core value proposition shifts in this new territory.
   - Outline organic and paid acquisition strategies customized for local habits (e.g., leveraging WhatsApp in LatAm/Europe vs. SMS/WeChat in other markets).
5. **Operational Rollout Timeline & Phased Milestone Plan**:
   - Define a 4-phase rollout:
     - **Phase A: Market Discovery & Legal Setup** (Months 1-3).
     - **Phase B: Product Localization & Beta Testing** (Months 4-6).
     - **Phase C: Soft Launch & Initial Feedback Loop** (Months 7-9).
     - **Phase D: General Availability & Scale** (Months 10-12).
6. **Risk Management & Mitigation Register**:
   - Construct a table with identified expansion risks (e.g., currency risk, local competitor retaliation, slow registration), their impact, and active mitigation steps.
</instructions>

## Variables
- {CURRENT_BUSINESS_PROFILE} – Detailed description of the current company, its core product, revenue, home market success metrics, and value proposition.
- {TARGET_GEOGRAPHY} – The exact country, state, or region targeted for expansion (e.g., entering the German market, expanding to Southeast Asia, targeting rural US states).
- {TARGET_AUDIENCE_NEW_MARKET} – Demographics, culture, and psychographics of the buyer personas in the new territory.
- {REGULATORY_LEGAL_CONSTRAINTS} – Known laws, tariffs, data hosting guidelines, tax rules, or license demands.
- {EXPANSION_RESOURCES} – Available expansion budget, executive dedication, and localized team capacity.

## Notes
- **Local Payment Support**: A key failure point for digital expansion is ignoring local payment habits; the prompt explicitly requires looking into localized transaction preferences.
- **Phased Rollout**: Pushes businesses away from massive, expensive launches in favor of a soft-launch and iteration loop.
- **Model Recommendation**: Highly suited for Claude 3.5 Sonnet, GPT-4o, or Gemini 1.5 Pro to manage the massive amount of global structural, legal, and operational planning.
