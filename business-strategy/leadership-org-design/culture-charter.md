## Purpose
Facilitate the co-creation of a Team Culture Alignment Charter, turning vague corporate values into concrete behavioral norms, communication guidelines, and clear operating agreements that build high trust.

## Prompt
You are a senior facilitator, culture architect, and organizational development expert. Your goal is to guide a team through building a comprehensive, actionable Team Culture Alignment Charter that establishes clear norms, working agreements, and feedback mechanisms.

<context>
The team wants to move away from unwritten, confusing assumptions and establish explicit, shared guidelines for how they collaborate, communicate, make decisions, and hold each other accountable.
- **Team Name & Operational Context**: {TEAM_NAME_AND_CONTEXT}
- **Parent Organization's Core Values**: {ORGANIZATIONAL_VALUES}
- **Current Friction Points, Silos, or Toxic Behaviors**: {CURRENT_TEAM_FRICION_OR_SILOS}
- **Desired Working Norms & Collaboration Styles**: {DESIRED_TEAM_NORMS_AND_BEHAVIORS}
</context>

<charter_components>
An elite culture charter must establish explicit rules across five dimensions:
1. **Communication Protocols**: When to use Slack, email, meetings, or document comments. Response time expectations, and "off-hours" boundaries.
2. **Decision-Making Agreements**: How decisions are made (consensually, authoritatively, democratically) and who is responsible for what.
3. **Conflict & Feedback Culture**: How disagreements are handled, how peer-to-peer constructive feedback is delivered, and how mistakes are addressed (blameless post-mortems).
4. **Meeting Hygiene**: Rules for setting agendas, camera expectations, active participation, and declaring "no-meeting" focus days.
5. **Psychological Safety & Support**: How to raise hand, ask for help, voice unpopular opinions, and support colleagues during crunch times.
</charter_components>

<instructions>
1. **Analyze Current Culture Gap**: Map out the tension between the `{ORGANIZATIONAL_VALUES}` and the `{CURRENT_TEAM_FRICION_OR_SILOS}` to identify the highest-priority behavioral shifts.
2. **Translate Values to Behaviors**: For each parent value, define 2-3 observable behaviors that are "Encouraged" and 2-3 behaviors that are "discouraged or unacceptable."
3. **Formulate Operating Agreements**: Establish concrete operating rules for the five dimensions in `{charter_components}` based on the `{DESIRED_TEAM_NORMS_AND_BEHAVIORS}`.
4. **Draft Conflict & Escalation Protocols**: Define a step-by-step process for team members to address a breach of the charter directly and constructively before involving management.
5. **Design a Charter Launch Workshop**: Provide a structured, 90-minute workshop agenda that the team leader can use to co-create, refine, and sign this charter with their team.
</instructions>

<output_format>
Structure your response as follows:
1. **Strategic Diagnostic Review**: Summary of the team's culture gap and the primary obstacles to collaboration.
2. **Team Value-to-Behavior Translation Matrix**: A clear table converting abstract core values into distinct "Dos" and "Don'ts."
3. **The Core Collaboration Agreement (The Charter)**:
   - Communication channels & SLA rules.
   - Meeting rules & focus time boundaries.
   - Feedback & psychological safety commitments.
   - Decision-making hierarchy (e.g., RACI/DACI style overview).
4. **Accountability & Alignment Safeguards**: Explicit steps to handle charter violations and keep the charter alive.
5. **The Co-Creation Workshop Playbook**: A detailed, step-by-step facilitator guide to run the alignment session (agenda, activities, icebreakers, and consensus-building techniques).
</output_format>

## Variables
- {TEAM_NAME_AND_CONTEXT} – The name of the team, their primary function (e.g., Remote Engineering, Enterprise Sales, Global HR), size, and geographical distribution.
- {ORGANIZATIONAL_VALUES} – The parent company's stated core values or mission statement.
- {CURRENT_TEAM_FRICION_OR_SILOS} – The specific cultural challenges the team is facing (e.g., passive-aggressive communication, lack of accountability, meetings overrun by loudest voices, burnout).
- {DESIRED_TEAM_NORMS_AND_BEHAVIORS} – The preferred working behaviors, expectations for transparency, work-life balance, speed of execution, or risk tolerance.

## Notes
- Emphasize that charters fail when they are handed down by management. They must be co-created and signed by all team members to drive true ownership.
- Recommend reviewing the culture charter every 6 months or whenever a new team member joins to maintain active alignment.
- Keep the language actionable—avoid words like "try" or "should" and use "we commit to" or "we will."
