## Purpose
Design a comprehensive, legally compliant, and culturally supportive remote or hybrid work policy that maximizes team performance, defines operational expectations, and ensures equal opportunities for distributed staff.

## Prompt
You are a senior workplace strategist, labor relations expert, and remote-first operations consultant. Your task is to review an organization's business needs and build a highly structured, operational Remote & Hybrid Work Policy Playbook.

<context>
The organization is seeking to transition, stabilize, or codify its distributed work model to attract global talent, cut overhead costs, and maintain productivity without sacrificing work-life balance or collaborative trust.
- **Operational Model (e.g., Remote-First, 3-Day Hybrid, Fully Distributed)**: {ORGANIZATIONAL_OPERATIONAL_MODEL}
- **Eligibility Criteria & Job Role Classifications**: {ELIGIBILITY_CRITERIA_AND_ROLES}
- **Equipment, Security, & Expense Policies**: {EQUIPMENT_AND_EXPENSE_PROVISIONS}
- **Timezone Expectations & Core Collaboration Hours**: {CORE_WORKING_HOURS_AND_TIMEZONES}
</context>

<regulatory_and_operational_pillars>
Your policy must cover the following operational pillars:
1. **Working Hours & Availability**: Clarifying boundaries for synchronous vs. asynchronous work, core meeting windows, and response time SLAs (e.g., Slack within 4 hours, email within 24 hours).
2. **Workspace & Information Security**: Rules for home networks (VPN usage), physical security of devices, public Wi-Fi restrictions, and compliance with data privacy standards (GDPR, CCPA, etc.).
3. **Expense Reinsurance & IT Logistics**: What equipment (laptops, monitors, ergonomic chairs) is provided, stipends for internet/utilities, and procedures for remote technical support.
4. **Performance & Results Tracking**: Transitioning from "presence-based evaluation" to output-based performance management.
5. **Ergonomics, Safety & Well-being**: Defining the employer's responsibility for home workspace safety and providing guidelines on screen breaks and mental wellness.
</regulatory_and_operational_pillars>

<instructions>
1. **Define Core Structure**: Based on the `{ORGANIZATIONAL_OPERATIONAL_MODEL}`, draft a detailed policy document defining hybrid schedules (e.g., specific anchor days in-office vs. flexible days) or remote-first expectations.
2. **Determine Eligibility & Roles**: Categorize positions based on `{ELIGIBILITY_CRITERIA_AND_ROLES}` (e.g., fully remote eligible, hybrid mandatory, on-site required) and detail the rationale for these decisions.
3. **Build Security & Compliance Protocols**: Detail the technology standards and physical environment requirements, focusing on device safety, secure connections, and handling proprietary business data.
4. **Establish Cost & Stipend Structures**: Map out a clean breakdown of equipment allowances, recurring stipends (internet, coworking space access), and travel reimbursements for company retreats using the inputs from `{EQUIPMENT_AND_EXPENSE_PROVISIONS}`.
5. **Formulate Communication Norms**: Using `{CORE_WORKING_HOURS_AND_TIMEZONES}`, construct a "collaboration charter" that protects focus blocks and defines expected availability schedules.
</instructions>

<output_format>
Structure your playbook into the following sections:
1. **Executive Statement & Core Rationale**: Alignment of the remote policy with company values and business goals.
2. **Policy Scope & Eligibility Matrix**: Clear classification of roles and permissions for remote/hybrid setups.
3. **Operational Guidelines**:
   - Availability and communication rules.
   - Core overlap hours & scheduling etiquette.
   - Output-based performance expectation metrics.
4. **Information Security & Equipment Standards**:
   - IT hardware delivery and return procedures.
   - Cyber-hygiene expectations (VPN, MFA, Wi-Fi rules).
   - Ergonomic workspace checklist for home office self-audits.
5. **Compensation, Expenses & Stipends**: A structured table outlining startup allowances and recurring stipends.
6. **Legal & Compliance Disclaimers**: Section focusing on tax residency implications, cross-jurisdiction labor compliance, and incident reporting (workspace injuries).
</output_format>

## Variables
- {ORGANIZATIONAL_OPERATIONAL_MODEL} – The chosen model: fully remote, hybrid with mandatory days, hybrid with flexible days, localized hub models, or remote-allowed.
- {ELIGIBILITY_CRITERIA_AND_ROLES} – Criteria for who can work remotely, which roles are bound to physical locations, and how employees apply for remote status.
- {EQUIPMENT_AND_EXPENSE_PROVISIONS} – Budget limits, home-office stipends, phone/internet offsets, hardware packages, and travel expense structures.
- {CORE_WORKING_HOURS_AND_TIMEZONES} – Expected overlap hours (e.g., 10 AM to 3 PM EST), timezone flexibility, holiday policies, and rules for international travel while working ("digital nomadism").

## Notes
- Remind users that local tax laws can vary heavily; hiring workers in another state or country often establishes corporate tax nexus and requires specialized legal reviews.
- Encourage putting a heavy emphasis on psychological safety, warning managers against installing invasive monitoring software ("bossware"), which severely degrades trust.
- Remind leaders that hybrid environments run the risk of "proximity bias" where on-site employees are favored for promotions and assignments over remote colleagues.
