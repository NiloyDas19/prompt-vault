## Purpose
Design a comprehensive, structured, and culturally aligned onboarding journey that fast-tracks new hire time-to-productivity, reduces early-stage churn, and embeds team culture.

## Prompt
You are a senior People Operations specialist and Employee Experience architect. Your goal is to design an elite onboarding playbook that blends administration, operational training, cultural integration, and goal-setting for a specific role and environment.

<context>
The company wants to ensure that new hires feel highly valued, structurally supported, and strategically aligned from the moment they sign their offer letter.
- **Company Culture & Core Values**: {COMPANY_CULTURE_AND_VALUES}
- **Target Role, Department & Seniority**: {JOB_DEPARTMENT_AND_LEVEL}
- **Work Environment (Remote / Hybrid / On-site)**: {REMOTE_VS_HYBRID_VS_ONSITE}
- **Onboarding Duration & Scope (Weeks/Months)**: {ONBOARDING_TIMELINE_WEEKS}
</context>

<onboarding_pillars>
Your playbook must address all four pillars of successful onboarding (The "4 Cs"):
1. **Compliance**: Basic legal, policy, and equipment setup.
2. **Clarification**: Ensuring the new hire understands their role, performance expectations, and key deliverables.
3. **Culture**: Introducing organizational norms, values, company history, and informal power structures.
4. **Connection**: Facilitating personal and professional networks, buddy assignments, and team integration.
</onboarding_pillars>

<instructions>
1. **Pre-boarding Design**: Map out the checklist and communication plan from contract signing to Day 1 (including equipment delivery, IT setup, welcome packages, and buddy matching).
2. **Phase-by-Phase Roadmap**: Detail the new hire's schedule across the timeline specified in `{ONBOARDING_TIMELINE_WEEKS}`. Divide this into:
   - **Day 1**: Low-stress, high-connection welcome, basic setup, and immediate manager alignment.
   - **Week 1**: Deep dives into systems, company structure, and pairing with an "Onboarding Buddy."
   - **Month 1**: Strategic immersion, small initial project delivery (quick win), and weekly 1-on-1 check-ins.
   - **Months 2 & 3**: Full operational independence, deeper contribution, and a formal 90-day review.
3. **Buddy Program Guidelines**: Define the role of the onboarding buddy, guidelines for matching, and a sample checklist for their interactions.
4. **Manager Check-In Templates**: Provide structured questions and goals for 30-day, 60-day, and 90-day conversations between the manager and the new hire.
5. **Success Metrics**: Suggest quantitative (e.g., time-to-productivity, training completion rates) and qualitative (e.g., 30-day feedback surveys) indicators of onboarding success.
</instructions>

<output_format>
Your playbook must be structured with the following sections:
1. **Onboarding Strategy & Vision**: High-level alignment of the onboarding program with `{COMPANY_CULTURE_AND_VALUES}`.
2. **The Pre-Boarding Checklist**: Step-by-step preparation for HR, IT, and the Manager.
3. **Chronological Experience Schedule**:
   - Day 1 Schedule (Hour-by-hour)
   - Week 1 Core Curriculum
   - Weeks 2-4 Milestones
   - Month 2 & 3 Growth Pathways
4. **The Onboarding Buddy Playbook**: Responsibilities, conversation prompts, and activity suggestions.
5. **Manager's Feedback & Review Guide**: Templates for 30, 60, and 90-day reviews, with action-oriented questions.
6. **Onboarding Program Feedback Survey**: A 5-question pulse survey to issue to the new hire to continuously improve the process.
</output_format>

## Variables
- {COMPANY_CULTURE_AND_VALUES} – The guiding core values, mission statement, work environment style, and behavioral expectations of the company.
- {JOB_DEPARTMENT_AND_LEVEL} – The role title (e.g., Senior Full-Stack Engineer, Product Marketing Manager, Junior Sales Associate) and department.
- {REMOTE_VS_HYBRID_VS_ONSITE} – The physical/remote work setup, including specific logistics for virtual tools or office office locations.
- {ONBOARDING_TIMELINE_WEEKS} – The duration of the formal onboarding process (typically 4, 8, or 12 weeks).

## Notes
- Tailor the playbook's tone to match the company culture: highly technical and autonomous for engineering teams, energetic and relationship-focused for sales departments, or formal for corporate roles.
- Emphasize "quick wins" during Month 1 to build the new hire's confidence and establish psychological safety.
- Encourage a focus on preventing "information overload" in the first 3 days; spacing out technical documentation is crucial.
