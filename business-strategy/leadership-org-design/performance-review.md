## Purpose
Design a comprehensive, growth-oriented performance review framework and meeting agenda that transforms evaluative ratings into actionable development plans, fostering trust and long-term retention.

## Prompt
You are an expert in organizational development, human resources, and performance psychology. Your task is to build a structured, growth-oriented performance review kit consisting of self-assessment prompts, manager evaluation guidelines, and a forward-looking action plan.

<context>
The organization is moving away from purely retrospective, anxiety-inducing review checklists and shifting toward developmental, balanced, and collaborative performance conversations.
- **Target Role & Core Responsibilities**: {ROLE_AND_RESPONSIBILITIES}
- **Review Cycle (e.g., Annual, Semi-Annual, Quarterly)**: {REVIEW_PERIOD_AND_CADENCE}
- **Key Performance Indicators & Soft Skills Focus**: {PERFORMANCE_CRITERIA_AND_SKILLS}
- **Employee Growth & Career Development Goals**: {GROWTH_AND_DEVELOPMENT_OBJECTIVES}
</context>

<philosophical_tenets>
To ensure constructive feedback, the review must adhere to these tenets:
1. **The 70/30 Rule**: 30% of the conversation should look back at achievements and gaps; 70% should look forward to goals, skills, and support.
2. **Behavior-Impact-Next Steps (BIN)**: Feedback must be grounded in specific actions and outcomes, not general personality traits.
3. **Psychological Safety**: The process must encourage open dialogue, allow employees to raise challenges, and treat mistakes as learning opportunities.
4. **Radical Candor**: Feedback is clear and direct, delivered with deep personal care and investment in the employee's success.
</philosophical_tenets>

<instructions>
1. **Self-Assessment Questionnaires**: Draft a 5-question self-reflection form for the employee, focusing on self-awareness, personal highlights, major blocks, and resource needs.
2. **Manager Assessment Rubric**: Create a detailed review matrix mapping the key performance dimensions and soft skills from `{PERFORMANCE_CRITERIA_AND_SKILLS}`.
   - For each dimension, define what "Developing," "Performing," and "Leading" looks like.
   - Provide a section to compile constructive feedback on alignment with `{ROLE_AND_RESPONSIBILITIES}`.
3. **Feedback Synthesis Guide**: Write a structured section for managers to synthesize peer feedback and business results, highlighting major strengths and 1-2 key areas for improvement.
4. **The Review Meeting Agenda**: Outline a detailed, minute-by-minute guide for the actual face-to-face performance conversation to keep the discussion positive and growth-centered.
5. **Growth & Career Progression Plan**: Design a collaborative template to establish future OKRs/KPIs and map out training, mentorship, or stretch projects in line with `{GROWTH_AND_DEVELOPMENT_OBJECTIVES}`.
</instructions>

<output_format>
Structure the complete review kit as follows:
1. **Employee Self-Reflection Form**: Questions with guidance on how to answer honestly and constructively.
2. **Manager Evaluation Template**:
   - Skills & Competency Matrix (with specific behavioral indicators).
   - Accomplishments vs. Objectives scorecard.
   - Values & Collaboration assessment.
3. **Feedback Synthesis (Retrospective Section)**:
   - "Keep Doing" (Strengths).
   - "Start Doing" (Growth areas/opportunities).
   - "Stop Doing" (Blindspots or negative behaviors).
4. **The Performance Conversation Agenda (45 Minutes)**:
   - Phase 1: Connection & Self-Reflection (10 mins)
   - Phase 2: Manager Review Synthesis (15 mins)
   - Phase 3: Alignment & Growth Strategy (15 mins)
   - Phase 4: Immediate Next Steps & Commitment (5 mins)
5. **The Professional Development Plan (PDP) Template**: Structured fields for training requirements, resource requests, target milestones, and follow-up check-in dates.
</output_format>

## Variables
- {ROLE_AND_RESPONSIBILITIES} – The key responsibilities, daily duties, and expectations of the employee's job.
- {REVIEW_PERIOD_AND_CADENCE} – The timeframe of performance being reviewed and how frequently formal reviews are held.
- {PERFORMANCE_CRITERIA_AND_SKILLS} – The operational KPIs, technical skills, and behavioral soft skills (e.g., communication, collaboration, leadership) being evaluated.
- {GROWTH_AND_DEVELOPMENT_OBJECTIVES} – The employee's stated career aspirations, target promotions, or interest in learning new skills.

## Notes
- Remind users that performance reviews should have zero surprises; if a manager is raising an issue for the first time in a formal review, they have failed to provide timely feedback.
- Advise managers to actively document performance notes throughout the review cycle to avoid recency bias.
- Encourage a follow-up check-in 2 weeks after the formal review to confirm the PDP is actively supported and being executed.
