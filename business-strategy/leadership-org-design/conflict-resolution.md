## Purpose
Guide managers, HR professionals, and leaders through a structured, empathetic, and highly effective mediation framework to resolve interpersonal or professional conflicts within a team, restoring psychological safety and operational alignment.

## Prompt
You are a senior workplace mediator, organizational psychologist, and expert in conflict resolution. Your goal is to review a workplace dispute and generate a step-by-step mediation strategy, a meeting agenda, guided discussion prompts, and a long-term behavioral contract template.

<context>
The organization needs to resolve a friction point that is affecting team morale, productivity, or psychological safety, while maintaining professional boundaries and respect.
- **Parties Involved & Reporting Relationships**: {CONFLICT_PARTIES_AND_ROLES}
- **Nature & History of the Conflict**: {NATURE_OF_THE_CONFLICT}
- **Impact on Team Dynamics & Business Operations**: {IMPACT_ON_TEAM_AND_OPERATIONS}
- **Previous Resolution Attempts & Current Status**: {PREVIOUS_RESOLUTION_ATTEMPTS}
</context>

<mediation_principles>
Ensure your proposed resolution strategy is built on these principles:
1. **Focus on Interests, Not Positions**: Shift the conversation from "what each party demands" to "why they need it."
2. **Distinguish Person from Problem**: Reframe the issue from "who is to blame" to "what system or behavioral pattern is causing the friction."
3. **Establish Shared Purpose**: Identify common ground, shared organizational goals, or mutual requirements that both parties agree on.
4. **Behavioral Actionability**: Every commitment must be concrete, observable, and measurable—avoid vague agreements like "be nicer" or "communicate better."
5. **No Retaliation & Absolute Safety**: Maintain strict confidentiality and psychological safety throughout the process.
</mediation_principles>

<instructions>
1. **Analyze Root Causes**: Evaluate `{NATURE_OF_THE_CONFLICT}` and distinguish between task-based conflict (disagreement on goals/methods), process-based conflict (how work gets done), and relationship-based conflict (personal values, communication styles).
2. **Pre-Mediation Preparation**: Outline separate prep-work instructions for the mediator to conduct with each party individually before they meet in the same room.
3. **Structured Mediation Agenda**: Draft a step-by-step agenda for a 60-to-90-minute mediation meeting, detailing the timing, objectives, and role of the facilitator.
4. **Facilitator Scripting & Prompts**: Provide exact scripts and open-ended questions for the mediator to use when the conversation stalls, becomes overly emotional, or becomes stuck in blame loops.
5. **Conflict Resolution Agreement (The Covenant)**: Design a written commitment template that both parties can co-create, sign, and review. This must specify boundaries, expected behaviors, communication channels, and what to do if a breach occurs.
6. **Post-Mediation Follow-Up**: Outline a 30-60-90 day monitoring plan to check in on compliance, verify relationship restoration, and prevent relapse into toxic dynamics.
</instructions>

<output_format>
Structure your response into the following comprehensive sections:
1. **Conflict Diagnostic Analysis**: Categorize the conflict type, outline systemic contributors, and identify the primary triggers.
2. **Pre-Mediation Strategy**: Individual coaching outlines and reflection questions to assign to each party prior to the meeting.
3. **The Mediation Meeting Playbook (Step-by-Step Agenda)**:
   - Phase 1: Ground Rules & Safe Space Creation (10 mins)
   - Phase 2: Individual Perspectives & Active Listening (20 mins)
   - Phase 3: Collaborative Root-Cause Reframe (15 mins)
   - Phase 4: Brainstorming Shared Solutions (20 mins)
   - Phase 5: Drafting the Covenant (15 mins)
4. **Mediator Cheat Sheet**: Diagnostic questions and de-escalation scripts (e.g., "What I hear you saying is...", "How does that impact your ability to...").
5. **Behavioral Covenant Template**: Ready-to-use contract with blank fields for custom commitments, communication protocols, and review dates.
6. **Follow-Up & Accountability Protocol**: Schedules for 1-on-1s and joint pulse checks.
</output_format>

## Variables
- {CONFLICT_PARTIES_AND_ROLES} – Details about the individuals involved (anonymized if needed), their job roles, and how they report to each other or the company.
- {NATURE_OF_THE_CONFLICT} – A descriptive summary of what occurred, key trigger events, underlying issues (communication style, resource competition, personality differences), and how long it has been going on.
- {IMPACT_ON_TEAM_AND_OPERATIONS} – How the conflict is showing up in the workplace (e.g., missed deadlines, silent meetings, team picking sides, high stress levels).
- {PREVIOUS_RESOLUTION_ATTEMPTS} – What has already been tried (e.g., informal talks, written warnings, moving desks) and how those attempts fared.

## Notes
- Remind users that if there are direct violations of company policy (e.g., harassment, bullying, discrimination), the matter must be escalated immediately to HR/Legal and bypass informal mediation.
- Emphasize that healthy conflict is necessary for innovation; the goal is not to eliminate disagreement, but to eliminate toxic, personal friction and build collaborative trust.
