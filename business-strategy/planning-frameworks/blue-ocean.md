## Purpose
Guides the creation of a Blue Ocean Strategy using the ERRC Grid (Eliminate-Reduce-Raise-Create) and Strategy Canvas framework to escape cutthroat competition ("Red Ocean") and unlock uncontested market space ("Blue Ocean").

## Prompt
<context>
You are an expert growth strategist and innovation consultant trained in Blue Ocean Strategy. Your specialty is helping established businesses and startups pivot away from bloody, commodity-driven competition ("Red Oceans") by pursuing "Value Innovation"—simultaneously driving down costs while driving up value for customers, thereby creating a leap in buyer value and making the competition irrelevant.
</context>

<task>
Analyze the current industry landscape and target customer profiles to construct a robust Blue Ocean Strategy. Your analysis must define the industry's current competing factors, outline a comprehensive ERRC Grid, describe the future Strategy Canvas, and formulate a compelling tagline for the new offering.
</task>

<inputs>
- **Target Industry & Market Segment:** {INDUSTRY_SEGMENT}
- **Current Industry Standards (Red Ocean Players):** {RED_OCEAN_STANDARDS}
- **Primary Customer Pain Points & Non-Consumers:** {PAIN_POINTS_NON_CONSUMERS}
- **Unique Capabilities/Assets of the Firm:** {FIRM_CAPABILITIES}
</inputs>

<instructions>
1. **Identify Industry Competing Factors**: List the 6-8 core variables that companies in {INDUSTRY_SEGMENT} currently compete on (e.g., price, specific features, speed, premium packaging, high-touch support).
2. **Conduct the ERRC Grid Analysis**: Apply the Four Actions Framework to break the trade-off between differentiation and low cost:
   - **Eliminate:** Which factors that the industry has long competed on should be completely eliminated? (Factors that buyers no longer value).
   - **Reduce:** Which factors should be reduced well below the industry standard? (Over-designed factors that increase costs without driving value).
   - **Raise:** Which factors should be raised well above the industry standard? (High-value elements that solve critical pain points).
   - **Create:** Which factors should be created that the industry has never offered? (Entirely new sources of buyer value).
3. **Map the Strategy Canvas**: Verbally plot the Value Curve of the Red Ocean standard vs. your newly designed Blue Ocean curve across the competing factors.
4. **Draft the Strategic Tagline**: Create a clear, compelling, and honest tagline that summarizes the new value proposition (Focus, Divergence, and a Compelling Tagline).
</instructions>

## 4. The Three Characteristics of a Good Strategy
- **Focus:** How the new model concentrates its energy on a few select factors rather than spreading resources thin.
- **Divergence:** How the Value Curve stands apart from the herd, refusing to benchmark against rivals.
- **Compelling Tagline:** A clear, memorable tagline (e.g., "The back-pocket personal trainer for busy parents") that represents the essence of this strategy.

## 5. Execution Risks & Non-Consumer Acquisition Plan
- **De-risking Hurdles:** Address operational, utility, or business model hurdles.
- **Acquisition Plan:** Practical steps to win over the 3 tiers of non-consumers (Soon-to-be, Refusing, and Unexplored).
