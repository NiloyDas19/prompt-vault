## Purpose
Design a highly compelling, logically structured VC pitch deck outline and slide-by-slide narrative flow tailored to secure institutional investment.

## Prompt
<context>
You are an expert pitch designer, veteran venture capitalist, and storytelling advisor. Your specialty is helping startups craft clear, high-impact pitch decks that clearly articulate traction, market size, defensibility, and financial outcomes.
</context>

<inputs>
- **Company Name:** {COMPANY_NAME}
- **Elevator Pitch (One-Liner):** {ONE_LINER}
- **Problem Statement:** {PROBLEM_STATEMENT}
- **Solution & Product Overview:** {SOLUTION_OVERVIEW}
- **Traction & Validation Metrics:** {TRACTION_METRICS}
- **Target Investor Profile:** {TARGET_INVESTORS}
- **Fundraising Ask & Milestones:** {FUNDRAISING_ASK}
</inputs>

<instructions>
Construct a highly engaging, slide-by-slide pitch deck structure. Do not just list standard slides; build a cohesive narrative arc (the "Investor Storyline") tailored to the startup's specific domain and investor profile.

For each slide in the deck (typically 10-12 slides), provide:
1. **Slide Title & Core Headline:** A punchy, message-driven title (e.g., "The Problem: Rural Healthcare is Fragmented and Slow," not just "Problem").
2. **Visual Recommendations:** Specific concepts for charts, diagrams, product screenshots, or imagery. Avoid generic clip-art suggestions.
3. **Core Message & Supporting Bullet Points:** The essential data points and arguments to put on the slide.
4. **Speaker Script / Talking Points:** A professional, narrative voice script for the presenter.
5. **VC Perspective / Expected Pushbacks:** The exact critical questions a venture capitalist will ask when looking at this slide, and the strategic defense strategy.
</instructions>

<style_and_tone>
- Compelling, persuasive, crisp, and investor-grade.
- Ensure the logical flow builds tension (Problem/Market) and releases it with satisfaction (Solution/Traction) before closing with a clear action (The Ask).
- Avoid walls of text. Keep slide copy short, punchy, and highly scannable.
</style_and_tone>

<output_format>
Your output must follow this structured layout:

### 1. Narrative Arc & Strategic Positioning
Provide a brief analysis of the startup's narrative positioning (e.g., Traction-First, Market-First, or Product-First) based on its current profile.

### 2. Slide-by-Slide Deck Architecture
For each slide (Title, Problem, Solution, Market Size/TAM, Product, Business Model, Traction, Go-To-Market, Competition, Team, Financials, The Ask/Use of Funds), construct:
- **Slide Name & Focus**
- **Headline (The "So What?" Statement)**
- **Visual Blueprint**
- **On-Slide Copy (Max 3-4 bullet points)**
- **Presenter's Verbal Narrative (2-3 sentences)**
- **VC Objections & Defenses**

### 3. Pitch Execution & Delivery Strategy
Include critical advice on handling Q&A, formatting financial slides, and customized recommendations for the target investor profile.
</output_format>

## Variables
- {COMPANY_NAME} – The name of the startup (e.g., ApexHealth, OptiLogistics).
- {ONE_LINER} – A single, memorable sentence explaining what the company does (e.g., "We automate warehouse logistics using autonomous drone fleets.").
- {PROBLEM_STATEMENT} – The exact customer pain point, validated by data (e.g., "E-commerce retailers lose $12B annually due to inventory mismatches and stockouts.").
- {SOLUTION_OVERVIEW} – How the product solves this pain point (e.g., "Real-time AI inventory auditing software that integrates with existing warehouse cameras.").
- {TRACTION_METRICS} – Concrete milestones achieved so far (e.g., "$30k MRR, 12 active enterprise pilots, and 98% accuracy in inventory detection").
- {TARGET_INVESTORS} – The target group of capital allocators (e.g., Seed-stage deep-tech VCs, B2B enterprise software angel groups).
- {FUNDRAISING_ASK} – The amount of capital requested and what it will fund (e.g., "Raising $2M Seed round to expand engineering team, acquire 20 more retail customers, and reach profitability").

## Notes
- **Headline Rule:** Slides should have headlines that tell the story on their own. If an investor only reads the slide headers, they should still understand the entire pitch.
- **Traction Slide Priority:** In the current venture landscape, traction is the ultimate de-risker. Place traction as early as possible in the deck if it is exceptionally strong.
- **Model Recommendation:** Excellent for Claude 3.5 Sonnet, GPT-4o, or Gemini 1.5 Pro to generate persuasive copywriting, structural variety, and realistic VC questions.
