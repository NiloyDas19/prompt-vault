## Purpose
Helps sales managers, executives, and commercial ops professionals plan strategic discounting frameworks and close-out incentives to protect gross margins while accelerating late-stage deals.

## Prompt
```markdown
You are a senior commercial operations director and strategic pricing consultant. Your goal is to design a highly structured, corporate-grade Pricing Discount & Close-Out Incentive Framework for {PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE_NAME}. 

Sales reps often resort to early, unearned discounting to close deals, eroding gross margins. You must build a disciplined commercial playbook that establishes clear delegation of authority limits, defines conditional "Give-to-Get" concessions, and structures high-impact end-of-quarter accelerators to close late-stage opportunities safely.

<context>
- Product/Service: {PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE_NAME}
- Base Pricing Structure: {BASE_PRICING_STRUCTURE}
- Target Profit Margins / Business Objectives: {TYPICAL_MARGIN_TARGETS}
- Absolute Maximum Permitted Discount (Floor): {MAX_ALLOWABLE_DISCOUNT}

- Reciprocal Concessions We Must Request (e.g., logo rights, upfront billing): {CONCESSION_REQUIREMENTS}
</context>

<instructions>
Develop the Pricing Discount & Incentive Playbook by detailing these core areas:

1. **Delegation of Authority (DoA) Matrix**:
   - Establish a tiered approval framework for discounts up to the absolute floor of {MAX_ALLOWABLE_DISCOUNT}.
   - Define discount authority tiers (e.g., Tier 1: AE/Rep Authority, Tier 2: Sales Director Approval, Tier 3: VP of Sales/CRO Approval, Tier 4: CFO/CEO Approval). Assign explicit percentage ranges to each.

2. **The "Give-to-Get" Concession Menu**:
   - Discounts must never be given for free. Map out the concessions from {CONCESSION_REQUIREMENTS} against permitted discount levels.
   - For every 5% discount granted, mandate a corresponding reciprocal business benefit (e.g., longer commitment, case study participation, accelerated payment terms, logo usage rights).

3. **Value-Added Deal Accelerators (Non-Cash Incentives)**:
   - Identify high-value, low-cost alternatives to price discounting using {DEAL_ACCELERATORS}.
   - Detail how sales reps can offer these non-cash incentives (e.g., free training, implementation support, API integrations) to solve buyer budget constraints without lowering contract list value.

4. **Strategic Close-Out Campaign Structures (End-of-Quarter/Year)**:
   - Design two distinct, highly compelling year-end or quarter-end closing campaigns.
   - Each campaign must have a clear time-bound urgency hook, a designated buyer benefit, and strict qualification rules to prevent buyers from timing the system.

5. **Conversational Discounting Scripts for AEs**:
   - Draft exact scripts for sales reps to pivot away from early price pressure, maintain value positioning, and frame final negotiation packages productively.
</instructions>

- Tone: Highly disciplined, commercial, protective of margins, strategic, and practical.
- Layout: Use structured governance matrices, clear bulleted guidelines, and copy-pasteable scripting blocks for reps.
- Vocabulary: Professional corporate finance and sales operations terminology.
</style_and_tone>
```

## Variables
- {PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE_NAME} – The name of your product, service, or suite.
- {BASE_PRICING_STRUCTURE} – How your product is priced (e.g., "$150/user/month with a 10-user minimum, plus a one-time $2,500 implementation fee").
- {TYPICAL_MARGIN_TARGETS} – The business's financial goal (e.g., "75% gross margins, prioritizing upfront annual cash flow").
- {MAX_ALLOWABLE_DISCOUNT} – The absolute maximum discount percentage a representative can potentially request (e.g., "25%").
- {DEAL_ACCELERATORS} – Non-monetary perks you can throw in to close a deal (e.g., "free onboarding training, waiving API integration fees, 3 months of free sandbox access").
- {CONCESSION_REQUIREMENTS} – Valuable trade-offs you want to extract from the customer in exchange for discounts (e.g., "logo rights, 3-year commitment, net-15 payment terms, hosting an internal case study").

## Notes
- **Stop discounting early**: Discounting too early in the sales cycle signals that your list price is inflated and that you lack confidence in your product's core value. Never negotiate price until value has been fully established.
- **Create a "Give-to-Get" habit**: Ensure your sales team understands that every discount requires a concession. When reps ask for a concession in return, buyers often stop asking for arbitrary price cuts because they realize it will cost them something.
- **Engage Sales Ops quarterly**: Review your discounting metrics quarterly. If more than 50% of your deals are closing with a discount, your list pricing may be misaligned with the market, or your sales team may need additional value-selling training.
- **Model Recommendation**: Best used with GPT-4o or Claude 3.5 Sonnet to draft nuanced financial guidelines, clear business frameworks, and highly realistic negotiation scripts.
