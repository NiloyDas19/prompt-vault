## Purpose
Write high-performing LinkedIn posts that build thought leadership, drive engagement, and generate inbound leads.

## Prompt
Write 5 LinkedIn post variations on the following topic. Each should use a different format and engagement strategy.

**Topic:** {TOPIC}
**My Role/Title:** {YOUR_ROLE}
**Industry:** {INDUSTRY}
**Target Audience on LinkedIn:** {AUDIENCE}
**Goal:** {GOAL}
**Personal Brand Voice:** {BRAND_VOICE}

### Post 1: The Personal Story
- Open with a vulnerable or surprising personal moment related to the topic
- 3-line hook → story → lesson → CTA
- Format: Short paragraphs, line breaks for readability
- End with a question to drive comments

### Post 2: The Framework/Listicle
- "After {X years/projects}, here are the {N} things I know about {topic}"
- Numbered list with one-line explanations
- Each point should be a mini-insight, not generic advice
- End with "What would you add to this list?"

### Post 3: The Contrarian Take
- Challenge a widely held belief in your industry
- "Unpopular opinion: {bold statement}"
- Back it up with evidence or personal experience
- Invite respectful debate

### Post 4: The Before/After
- "3 years ago, I {old behavior}. Today, I {new behavior}."
- Walk through the transformation
- Key lesson in the middle
- Motivational but grounded — avoid hustle-porn energy

### Post 5: The Carousel Outline
- 10-slide carousel concept on the topic
- Slide 1: Bold title/hook
- Slides 2–9: One insight per slide with visual suggestion
- Slide 10: CTA + your brand/offer

For each post:
- Full copy, ready to post
- Character count (LinkedIn sweet spot: 1,200–1,500 characters)
- Best day/time to post
- Predicted engagement type: likes, comments, shares, or saves

## Variables
- {TOPIC} – What you're posting about
- {YOUR_ROLE} – Your job title or description
- {INDUSTRY} – Your industry
- {AUDIENCE} – Who you want to reach (e.g., "CMOs", "startup founders", "hiring managers")
- {GOAL} – Post objective (e.g., "establish authority", "generate inbound leads", "build network")
- {BRAND_VOICE} – Your personal brand tone (e.g., "direct and analytical", "warm and encouraging", "witty and provocative")

## Notes
- LinkedIn favors posts without external links (they suppress reach). Put links in the first comment instead.
- Posts with line breaks and white space perform better than walls of text.
- Commenting on others' posts is just as important as posting your own. The LinkedIn algorithm rewards engagement reciprocity.
