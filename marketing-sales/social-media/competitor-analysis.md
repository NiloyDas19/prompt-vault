## Purpose
Analyze competitors' social media strategies to identify what's working, find content gaps, and build a differentiated approach.

## Prompt
Conduct a deep analysis of my competitors' social media presence and help me build a strategy that outperforms them.

**My Brand:** {MY_BRAND}
**My Platforms:** {MY_PLATFORMS}
**Competitors to Analyze:** {COMPETITORS}
**My Current Strategy (briefly):** {CURRENT_STRATEGY}

For each competitor, analyze:

### 1. Content Audit
- What content types do they post most? (video, carousels, text, stories)
- What topics get the most engagement?
- What's their posting frequency?
- What's their average engagement rate?
- What content flopped? (low engagement posts — often more revealing than hits)

### 2. Voice & Positioning Analysis
- How would you describe their brand voice in 3 adjectives?
- Are they educational, entertaining, inspiring, or promotional?
- What psychological triggers do they use most? (FOMO, social proof, authority, humor)

### 3. Audience Analysis
- Who engages with their content? (look at commenters: job titles, demographics, language)
- What questions are people asking in their comments?
- What complaints or frustrations appear in comments?

### 4. Gap Analysis
- What topics are they NOT covering that their audience would want?
- What formats are they not using that perform well in this niche?
- Where is their engagement weak (which platforms, which content types)?

### 5. Differentiation Opportunities
Based on this analysis:
- 3 content angles my competitors are ignoring that I should own
- 3 format experiments to try that none of them are doing
- A positioning statement that differentiates me from all of them
- 5 specific post ideas inspired by (but differentiated from) their best-performing content

## Variables
- {MY_BRAND} – Your brand name
- {MY_PLATFORMS} – Where you're active
- {COMPETITORS} – 2–4 competitor accounts to analyze (with handles/links)
- {CURRENT_STRATEGY} – Brief description of what you're currently doing

## Notes
- Don't copy competitors — understand WHY their content works and apply the underlying principles differently.
- Pay special attention to comments and questions on competitor posts — this is free market research.
- The biggest opportunity is usually in the gaps: what competitors AREN'T doing, not what they ARE doing.
