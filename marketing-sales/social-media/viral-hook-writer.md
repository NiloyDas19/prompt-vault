## Purpose
Generate scroll-stopping opening lines (hooks) for social media posts that capture attention in the first 1–2 seconds.

## Prompt
Write 30 scroll-stopping hooks for social media posts about {TOPIC}. These hooks must make someone stop scrolling and read the rest.

**Topic:** {TOPIC}
**Platform:** {PLATFORM}
**Audience:** {AUDIENCE}
**Content Goal:** {GOAL}
**Brand Voice:** {BRAND_VOICE}

Generate 5 hooks for each style:

### 1. Bold Claim
- Start with a provocative statement that challenges conventional wisdom
- Example pattern: "{Common belief} is dead. Here's what's replacing it."

### 2. Curiosity Gap
- Tease information without revealing it
- Pattern: "I discovered something about {topic} that changed everything..."

### 3. Story Opener
- Drop the reader into the middle of a compelling scene
- Pattern: "Last Tuesday at 2 AM, I got an email that changed my business..."

### 4. Statistical Shock
- Lead with a surprising number or data point
- Pattern: "97% of {audience} get this wrong about {topic}."

### 5. Direct Challenge
- Call out a behavior or belief the reader likely holds
- Pattern: "If you're still doing {X}, you're leaving money on the table."

### 6. Relatable Confession
- Start with vulnerability or a "me too" moment
- Pattern: "I used to {embarrassing thing} until I learned..."

For each hook:
- The hook text (max 15 words)
- Why it works psychologically
- The ideal post format to pair it with (carousel, text post, video, reel)
- A sample "second line" that keeps the momentum going

Then rank your **top 5** across all categories.

## Variables
- {TOPIC} – What the post is about (e.g., "email marketing mistakes", "startup fundraising", "fitness myths")
- {PLATFORM} – Where it'll be posted (e.g., "LinkedIn", "Instagram Reels", "Twitter/X")
- {AUDIENCE} – Who you're targeting
- {GOAL} – Post objective (e.g., "get saves/bookmarks", "drive comments", "build authority")
- {BRAND_VOICE} – Tone

## Notes
- You have 1.3 seconds to stop someone from scrolling. Your hook is everything.
- On LinkedIn, the first 2 lines show before "...see more" — make them irresistible.
- Hooks that create an "information gap" (curiosity without resolution) consistently outperform direct statements.
