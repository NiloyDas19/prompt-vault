## Purpose
Write viral Twitter/X threads that teach, entertain, or persuade — optimized for retweets, bookmarks, and follower growth.

## Prompt
Write a 12-tweet Twitter/X thread on the following topic. Make it so valuable that people bookmark it and share it with their network.

**Topic:** {TOPIC}
**Angle:** {ANGLE}
**Target Audience:** {AUDIENCE}
**Goal:** {GOAL}
**Tone:** {TONE}

### Thread Structure:

**Tweet 1 (Hook):**
- The most important tweet — it determines whether anyone reads the rest
- Pattern: Bold claim + promise of value + "🧵👇" or "Thread:"
- Max 200 characters for maximum visibility

**Tweets 2–10 (Content):**
- One insight per tweet
- Use a mix of: frameworks, examples, stats, stories, analogies
- Each tweet should be valuable as a standalone quote
- Use "→" or numbered lists for scannability
- Include 1–2 tweets with a visual (chart, screenshot, or diagram description)

**Tweet 11 (Recap):**
- TL;DR summary of the thread in 3–4 bullet points
- "Save this thread for later 🔖"

**Tweet 12 (CTA):**
- Ask for a retweet: "If this was useful, RT tweet #1 to help others find it"
- Plug your newsletter, product, or profile: "Follow me @{handle} for more {topic}"
- Optional: link to a resource or lead magnet

### Also Provide:
- **Thread title** for when you screenshot and share the thread as an image
- **Best time to post** for maximum impressions
- **3 quote-tweet ideas** — shorter rephrases of key points you can use to resurface the thread later
- **Engagement strategy** — How to keep the thread alive in the algorithm after posting

## Variables
- {TOPIC} – Thread topic
- {ANGLE} – Your unique take (e.g., "lessons from 10 years in the industry", "mistakes I made so you don't have to")
- {AUDIENCE} – Who this is for
- {GOAL} – Objective (e.g., "follower growth", "drive traffic to my site", "establish expertise")
- {TONE} – Voice (e.g., "punchy and direct", "storytelling and personal", "data-driven and analytical")

## Notes
- The hook tweet gets 80% of the engagement. Spend 50% of your time on it.
- Threads with personal stories and specific examples outperform generic advice threads.
- Post threads between 8–10 AM (your audience's timezone) on weekdays for maximum visibility.
- Self-reply with "Follow @handle for more" at the end — not in the middle. Mid-thread self-promotion kills engagement.
