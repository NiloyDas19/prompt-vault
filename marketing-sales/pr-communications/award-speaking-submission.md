## Purpose
Write compelling award nominations, speaking proposals, and conference abstracts that get accepted.

## Prompt
Help me write a submission for {SUBMISSION_TYPE}. I need to stand out from hundreds of other applicants.

**Submission For:** {SUBMISSION_TARGET}
**Submission Type:** {SUBMISSION_TYPE}
**Applicant:** {APPLICANT}
**Topic/Achievement:** {TOPIC}
**Word/Character Limit:** {WORD_LIMIT}
**Evaluation Criteria (if known):** {CRITERIA}

### For Award Nominations:

**1. Executive Summary** (1 paragraph)
- The achievement in compelling, specific terms
- Why it matters to the industry

**2. The Challenge**
- What problem was being solved
- Why it was difficult or unprecedented

**3. The Approach**
- What was done differently from conventional approaches
- Innovation or creativity demonstrated

**4. The Results**
- Specific, quantifiable outcomes
- Before/after comparison
- Third-party validation if available

**5. Impact**
- Why this achievement matters beyond the organization
- Industry, community, or customer impact

### For Speaking Proposals:

**1. Talk Title** (3 options: descriptive, intriguing, provocative)

**2. Abstract** (150 words)
- Hook: why this topic is urgent or interesting
- Promise: what the audience will learn
- Credibility: why YOU should give this talk

**3. Outline** (3–5 key takeaways)
- Specific, actionable insights attendees will leave with

**4. Speaker Bio** (50 words and 150 words)
- Written in third person
- Relevant credentials and achievements
- Personal touch

**5. Why This Talk, Why Now**
- Connection to current industry trends
- Why the audience needs this at this specific moment

## Variables
- {SUBMISSION_TARGET} – Award name or conference (e.g., "Webby Awards", "SaaStr Annual", "SXSW")
- {SUBMISSION_TYPE} – Type (e.g., "award nomination", "conference talk proposal", "panel discussion", "workshop")
- {APPLICANT} – Who is being nominated / who is speaking
- {TOPIC} – The achievement or talk topic
- {WORD_LIMIT} – Submission length constraints
- {CRITERIA} – How submissions are judged (if known)

## Notes
- Award submissions that include SPECIFIC numbers win more often than those with vague claims. "Increased revenue by 340% in 12 months" beats "significantly grew the business."
- For speaking proposals: conference organizers want talks that are practical, specific, and deliver takeaways — not thought leadership fluff. Promise something concrete the audience can use Monday morning.
- Apply to 10+ awards/conferences. Acceptance rates are typically 10–20%. Volume matters.
