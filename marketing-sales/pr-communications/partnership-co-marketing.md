## Purpose
Write compelling partnership proposals for co-marketing initiatives, content collaborations, or strategic alliances.

## Prompt
Write a partnership proposal for a co-marketing initiative between my company and a potential partner.

**My Company:** {MY_COMPANY}
**Partner Company:** {PARTNER_COMPANY}
**Partnership Type:** {PARTNERSHIP_TYPE}
**Shared Audience:** {SHARED_AUDIENCE}
**My Goal:** {MY_GOAL}
**Value I Bring:** {MY_VALUE}

### Partnership Proposal:

**1. Executive Summary**
- Why this partnership makes sense (mutual benefit)
- Shared audience and complementary strengths
- Expected outcomes for both parties

**2. Partnership Concept**
- Detailed description of the proposed initiative
- Roles and responsibilities for each partner
- Timeline and milestones

**3. Mutual Benefits**
| Benefit | For {MY_COMPANY} | For {PARTNER_COMPANY} |
|---------|------------------|----------------------|

**4. Proposed Activities**
- Specific co-marketing activities (co-branded content, joint webinar, bundle offer, cross-promotion)
- Content calendar with deadlines
- Resource requirements from each side

**5. Success Metrics**
- How we'll measure success
- Shared KPIs
- Reporting cadence and format

**6. Investment & Resources**
- What each partner contributes (time, budget, audience access, content)
- Cost sharing structure if applicable

**7. Next Steps**
- Proposed kickoff timeline
- Decision-making process
- Point of contact for each side

### Outreach Email
A concise outreach email proposing the partnership (under 200 words), sent from {MY_ROLE} to their equivalent.

## Variables
- {MY_COMPANY} – Your company
- {PARTNER_COMPANY} – Target partner
- {PARTNERSHIP_TYPE} – Type (e.g., "content collaboration", "technology integration", "co-branded product", "referral program")
- {SHARED_AUDIENCE} – The audience you share
- {MY_GOAL} – What you want from the partnership
- {MY_VALUE} – What you bring to the table
- {MY_ROLE} – Your role/title

## Notes
- The best partnerships are genuinely win-win. If you can't clearly articulate what the OTHER party gets, the proposal isn't ready.
- Start with a small, low-risk initiative (a co-authored blog post or joint webinar) before proposing anything large.
- Always propose specific, actionable ideas — not vague "let's collaborate." Partners are busy; make it easy to say yes.
