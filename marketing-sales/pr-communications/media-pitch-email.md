## Purpose
Write personalized, compelling media pitch emails that get journalists to open, read, and cover your story.

## Prompt
Write 5 personalized media pitch email variations for the following story. Each should use a different angle to maximize coverage potential.

**Story/Announcement:** {STORY}
**Company:** {COMPANY}
**Target Journalists:** {JOURNALIST_TYPE}
**Publications Targeting:** {PUBLICATIONS}
**Unique Data/Assets Available:** {ASSETS}
**Timing Hook:** {TIMING}

### For each pitch variation:

**Angle:** A unique story angle (different from the others)

**Subject Line:** Under 40 characters, no clickbait

**Email Body (under 150 words):**
- **Opening sentence:** Personalized hook — reference their beat, a recent article, or why this is relevant to their audience specifically
- **The story in 2–3 sentences:** What happened, why it matters, who cares
- **The "so what" for their readers:** Why their specific audience needs to know this
- **What you're offering:** Exclusive interview, data, early access, expert source
- **Soft ask:** "Would this be worth exploring?" — not "Please cover us"

**Assets Offered:**
- What supplementary materials you can provide (images, data, demos, expert interviews)

### Pitch Strategy:
- Best time of day and day of week to send to journalists
- Follow-up timing and approach
- "No" recovery: how to stay on their radar for future stories
- Relationship building: how to provide value to journalists beyond pitching stories

## Variables
- {STORY} – The news or story you want covered
- {COMPANY} – Your company
- {JOURNALIST_TYPE} – What beat they cover (e.g., "tech/startup reporters", "business journalists", "industry trade press")
- {PUBLICATIONS} – Specific outlets you're targeting
- {ASSETS} – What you can offer (data, quotes, demos, images)
- {TIMING} – Why now (tie to a trend, event, or news cycle)

## Notes
- Journalists get 100+ pitches per day. You have 3 seconds to make them care. Lead with why their readers benefit, not why your company is great.
- Never send a pitch to an editor and reporter at the same outlet simultaneously. It looks sloppy.
- The best PR strategy is building relationships BEFORE you need coverage. Offer yourself as a source for their stories before ever pitching your own.
