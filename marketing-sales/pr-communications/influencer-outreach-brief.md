## Purpose
Write personalized influencer outreach messages and campaign briefs that secure partnerships and produce authentic content.

## Prompt
Help me plan and execute an influencer marketing campaign. Create outreach messages and a campaign brief.

**Brand:** {BRAND}
**Product:** {PRODUCT}
**Campaign Goal:** {GOAL}
**Budget:** {BUDGET}
**Target Influencer Profile:** {INFLUENCER_PROFILE}
**Platform:** {PLATFORM}

### 1. Influencer Selection Criteria
- Follower range that fits the budget
- Engagement rate minimum (not just follower count)
- Content quality indicators
- Audience alignment signals
- Red flags to watch for (fake followers, controversy, misaligned values)

### 2. Outreach Messages (3 variations)

**Approach A: Direct & Professional**
- Clear brand introduction, specific compliment on their work, proposed partnership

**Approach B: Value-First**
- Lead with what you can offer THEM (free product, exposure, revenue share)

**Approach C: Collaborative**
- Propose a creative concept together, not dictate the content

For each: subject line (email) or opening DM, full message, and CTA.

### 3. Campaign Brief Template
- **Campaign overview:** What the brand is, what the campaign aims to achieve
- **Deliverables:** Specific content pieces expected (posts, stories, videos)
- **Key messages:** What must be communicated (max 3 points)
- **Creative freedom:** What they CAN and CANNOT do
- **Timeline:** Deadlines for draft, revisions, and posting
- **Compensation:** Payment structure, product gifting, affiliate codes
- **FTC/Disclosure requirements:** Legal requirements for sponsored content
- **Brand guidelines:** Logo usage, tagging, hashtags
- **Approval process:** Who reviews content and turnaround time

### 4. Compensation Framework
- How to determine fair pricing based on follower count, engagement, and platform
- Payment models: flat fee, performance-based, affiliate, product-only
- Negotiation tips for budget-constrained brands

### 5. Performance Tracking
- Metrics to track: reach, engagement, traffic, conversions, cost per acquisition
- Tracking methods: UTM links, promo codes, affiliate dashboards
- How to evaluate ROI of the campaign

## Variables
- {BRAND} – Your brand
- {PRODUCT} – Product being promoted
- {GOAL} – Campaign goal (e.g., "brand awareness", "product launch buzz", "drive sales")
- {BUDGET} – Total budget
- {INFLUENCER_PROFILE} – Ideal influencer type (e.g., "micro-influencers 10K–50K in fitness", "tech reviewers on YouTube")
- {PLATFORM} – Primary platform

## Notes
- Micro-influencers (10K–100K followers) often deliver higher ROI than mega-influencers due to higher engagement rates and audience trust.
- Creative freedom is key. The more you dictate the content, the less authentic it will feel — and audiences can always tell.
- FTC requires clear disclosure of paid partnerships. "#ad" in the caption, not buried in 30 hashtags. Non-compliance can result in fines for both the brand and the influencer.
