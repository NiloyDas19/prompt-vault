## Purpose
Write professional, newsworthy press releases that follow AP style and are ready to distribute to journalists and media outlets.

## Prompt
Write a press release for the following announcement. Follow AP style guidelines and make it genuinely newsworthy — not just a glorified advertisement.

**Company:** {COMPANY}
**Announcement Type:** {ANNOUNCEMENT_TYPE}
**News:** {NEWS_DETAILS}
**Key Quote (spokesperson):** {SPOKESPERSON}
**Target Media:** {TARGET_MEDIA}
**Embargo Date (if any):** {EMBARGO}

### Press Release Structure:

**Headline:** Strong, factual, attention-grabbing (under 80 characters)
**Subheadline:** Expand on the headline with additional context

**Dateline:** City, State — Date

**Lead Paragraph (Who, What, When, Where, Why):**
- The most important information in the first 2 sentences
- A journalist should be able to write their story from this paragraph alone

**Body Paragraph 2: The Impact**
- Why this matters to the industry/customers/market
- Quantifiable impact if possible

**Body Paragraph 3: Quote**
- A polished quote from {SPOKESPERSON}
- Should sound human, not corporate

**Body Paragraph 4: Details**
- Additional product/feature/event details
- Availability, pricing, or timeline information

**Body Paragraph 5: Context**
- Industry trends that make this announcement timely
- Brief company positioning within the broader market

**Boilerplate:** About {COMPANY} — standard company description (3–4 sentences)

**Media Contact:**
- Name, title, email, phone

### Additional Assets:
- 3 pitch email subject lines for journalist outreach
- 5 potential media angles (different stories journalists could write)
- Social media announcement posts (Twitter, LinkedIn)
- Internal announcement email for employees

## Variables
- {COMPANY} – Your company name
- {ANNOUNCEMENT_TYPE} – Type (e.g., "product launch", "funding round", "partnership", "executive hire", "research study")
- {NEWS_DETAILS} – The actual news
- {SPOKESPERSON} – Who to quote (name, title)
- {TARGET_MEDIA} – Where you want coverage (e.g., "tech trade press", "national business media", "local news")
- {EMBARGO} – Embargo date/time if applicable

## Notes
- The lead paragraph is 80% of a press release's value. If a journalist reads only the first paragraph, they should have the complete story.
- Quotes should add perspective, not restate facts. "We're excited to launch..." is a waste. "This changes how teams collaborate because..." adds value.
- Only send press releases for genuinely newsworthy events. Sending mediocre news burns your relationship with journalists.
