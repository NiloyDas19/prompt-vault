## Purpose
Prepare crisis communication templates and response strategies for potential PR emergencies before they happen.

## Prompt
Help me prepare crisis communication materials for a {CRISIS_TYPE} scenario. I need pre-written responses and a clear action plan so we're not scrambling if this happens.

**Company:** {COMPANY}
**Crisis Scenario:** {CRISIS_TYPE}
**Stakeholders Affected:** {STAKEHOLDERS}
**Brand Values:** {BRAND_VALUES}
**Communication Channels:** {CHANNELS}

### 1. Crisis Response Framework
- **First 1 hour:** What to do immediately (internal)
- **First 4 hours:** First public statement
- **First 24 hours:** Follow-up communications
- **First week:** Ongoing updates and resolution
- **Post-crisis:** Recovery and rebuilding trust

### 2. Initial Holding Statement (first 4 hours)
A brief, honest statement that:
- Acknowledges the situation without admitting unnecessary fault
- Expresses concern for affected parties
- Commits to investigating and communicating updates
- Provides a timeline for the next update
- Specifies where people can get information

### 3. Stakeholder-Specific Communications
For each stakeholder group:
- **Customers:** Email template
- **Employees:** Internal memo
- **Media:** Press statement
- **Investors/Board:** Briefing document
- **Social media:** Public response
- **Partners:** Direct communication

### 4. Q&A Preparation
Anticipate and prepare answers for:
- 10 tough questions journalists will ask
- 10 tough questions customers will ask
- 5 tough questions employees will ask
- For each: the honest answer, the public-ready answer, and what to say if you don't know yet

### 5. Social Media Response Protocol
- Monitoring keywords and hashtags
- Response templates for different sentiment levels (angry, concerned, supportive)
- When to respond publicly vs. take to DM
- When to go silent and let the news cycle pass

### 6. Post-Crisis Recovery Plan
- How to rebuild trust after the crisis passes
- Internal debrief template
- Changes to communicate to prevent recurrence
- Follow-up communications timeline (1 week, 1 month, 3 months)

## Variables
- {COMPANY} – Your company
- {CRISIS_TYPE} – Type of crisis (e.g., "data breach", "product recall", "executive misconduct", "viral negative social media", "customer injury", "service outage")
- {STAKEHOLDERS} – Who is affected
- {BRAND_VALUES} – Core values to anchor messaging
- {CHANNELS} – Communication channels available

## Notes
- The best crisis communication follows the formula: Acknowledge → Apologize (if warranted) → Act → Advance.
- Speed and transparency beat perfection. A genuine, imperfect response within 4 hours is better than a polished response after 48 hours.
- NEVER lie, deflect blame dishonestly, or say "no comment." These responses always make crises worse.
- Prepare these materials BEFORE a crisis happens. You won't have time to think clearly during an active emergency.
