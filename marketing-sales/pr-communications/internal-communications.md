## Purpose
Write clear, engaging internal communications for company announcements, policy changes, and team updates.

## Prompt
Write an internal communication for the following company announcement. The message should be clear, empathetic, and action-oriented.

**Company:** {COMPANY}
**Announcement Type:** {ANNOUNCEMENT_TYPE}
**Key Information:** {KEY_INFO}
**Audience:** {INTERNAL_AUDIENCE}
**Sender:** {SENDER}
**Sentiment:** {SENTIMENT}
**Action Required:** {ACTION_REQUIRED}

### Communication Outputs:

**1. All-Hands Email**
- Subject line (clear, not clickbait)
- Opening: Context and why this matters
- Body: The key information, clearly structured
- Impact: What this means for employees specifically
- Action items: What they need to do (if anything)
- Q&A: Anticipated questions and answers
- Closing: Supportive, forward-looking tone

**2. Slack/Teams Announcement**
- Condensed version (max 200 words)
- Key information + link to full details
- Appropriate emoji and tone

**3. Manager Talking Points**
- 5 key points for managers to cover in team meetings
- How to handle tough questions
- What to say and what NOT to say

**4. FAQ Document**
- 10 anticipated questions with clear answers
- "We don't know yet" responses for uncertain areas
- Where to direct additional questions

## Variables
- {COMPANY} – Company name
- {ANNOUNCEMENT_TYPE} – Type (e.g., "organizational restructure", "new policy", "leadership change", "company milestone", "office closure", "acquisition")
- {KEY_INFO} – The essential facts
- {INTERNAL_AUDIENCE} – Who this is for (e.g., "all employees", "engineering team", "managers only")
- {SENDER} – Who the message comes from (e.g., "CEO", "HR", "team lead")
- {SENTIMENT} – Emotional tone needed (e.g., "celebratory", "serious but optimistic", "sensitive and empathetic")
- {ACTION_REQUIRED} – What employees need to do

## Notes
- For difficult announcements (layoffs, restructures): lead with empathy, be transparent about the "why," and provide specific support resources.
- Employees hear about company news from 3 sources: official communications, their manager, and the rumor mill. Make sure the first two are faster and better than the third.
- Always answer "What does this mean for ME?" — that's what every employee is thinking, even if they don't say it.
