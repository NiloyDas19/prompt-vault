## Purpose
Conduct a comprehensive audit of your current marketing strategy to identify what's working, what's not, and where to focus for maximum impact.

## Prompt
Conduct a thorough marketing strategy audit for my business. Be brutally honest — I need to know what's working, what's wasting money, and where to focus.

**Business:** {BUSINESS}
**Annual Revenue:** {REVENUE}
**Marketing Budget:** {MARKETING_BUDGET}

**Current Channels:** {CURRENT_CHANNELS}
**Top 3 Goals:** {GOALS}
**Current Metrics:**
{CURRENT_METRICS}

### Audit Sections:

**1. Channel Performance Review**
For each active channel, assess:
- ROI estimate: is this channel earning more than it costs?
- Growth trajectory: improving, stable, or declining?
- Verdict: Scale, Maintain, Fix, or Kill
- Specific recommendation

**2. Content & Messaging Audit**
- Is the brand message consistent across channels?
- Does the website clearly communicate the value proposition within 5 seconds?
- Are we talking about what WE want to say, or what CUSTOMERS need to hear?
- Content quality assessment: is our content genuinely useful or just filling a calendar?

**3. Funnel Analysis**
- Where are the biggest drop-offs in the funnel?
- Awareness → Interest → Consideration → Decision — where is the bottleneck?
- What stage needs the most urgent attention?

**4. Competitive Position**
- How does our marketing effort compare to our top 2–3 competitors?
- Are we being outspent, outpositioned, or outexecuted?
- Where can we win even with fewer resources?

**5. Resource Allocation Assessment**
- Are we spreading too thin across too many channels?
- What should we stop doing to free up resources?
- What's the highest ROI activity we should double down on?
- Is our team capacity aligned with our strategy?

**6. Quick Wins (this month)**
- 5 specific, actionable changes that can improve results within 30 days
- Estimated impact of each change

**7. Strategic Recommendations (next quarter)**
- Top 3 strategic priorities for the next 90 days
- What to start, stop, and continue
- Budget reallocation recommendations

## Variables
- {BUSINESS} – Your business
- {REVENUE} – Annual revenue range
- {MARKETING_BUDGET} – Annual or monthly marketing budget
- {TEAM_SIZE} – Marketing team size
- {CURRENT_CHANNELS} – All marketing channels currently active
- {GOALS} – Top 3 business goals
- {CURRENT_METRICS} – Key metrics: website traffic, conversion rate, cost per acquisition, email list size, social followers, etc.

## Notes
- The most common finding in marketing audits: too many channels, not enough depth in any single one. It's better to dominate 2 channels than be mediocre on 7.
- "Killing" an underperforming channel is often the highest-impact decision a marketer can make. It frees up resources for what actually works.
- Run this audit quarterly. Marketing conditions change fast.
