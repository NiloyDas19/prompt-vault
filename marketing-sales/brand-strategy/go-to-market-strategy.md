## Purpose
Design a complete go-to-market strategy for launching a new product, entering a new market, or repositioning an existing offering.

## Prompt
Build a comprehensive go-to-market (GTM) strategy for the following product launch or market entry.

**Product/Service:** {PRODUCT}
**Target Market:** {TARGET_MARKET}
**Launch Type:** {LAUNCH_TYPE}
**Launch Date:** {LAUNCH_DATE}
**Budget:** {BUDGET}
**Team Size:** {TEAM_SIZE}
**Current Brand Awareness:** {AWARENESS_LEVEL}

### GTM Strategy:

**1. Market Analysis**
- Market size and growth trajectory
- Key trends driving demand
- Customer segments to prioritize (rank by attractiveness and accessibility)
- Competitor landscape: who's already here and how entrenched are they?

**2. Positioning & Messaging**
- How to position this product in the market
- Key messages for each customer segment
- The "only" statement: "We are the ONLY {category} that {unique value}"

**3. Pricing Strategy**
- Recommended pricing model and price point
- Competitive pricing context
- Introductory pricing / launch offers
- Pricing psychology tactics

**4. Channel Strategy**
- Primary channels for customer acquisition (ranked by expected ROI)
- Content marketing plan (first 90 days)
- Paid advertising strategy (platforms, budget allocation, targeting)
- Partner and distribution channels
- Sales-led vs. product-led growth recommendation

**5. Launch Timeline**
| Phase | Timeline | Activities | Goals |
|-------|----------|-----------|-------|
| Pre-launch | | | |
| Launch week | | | |
| Post-launch (30 days) | | | |
| Scale (60–90 days) | | | |

**6. Metrics & Success Criteria**
- KPIs for each phase
- How to know if the launch is working
- When to pivot vs. double down
- 30-60-90 day targets

**7. Risk Assessment**
- Top 3 risks and mitigation strategies
- What would cause you to kill or pivot the launch
- Contingency plans

## Variables
- {PRODUCT} – Product or service being launched
- {TARGET_MARKET} – Who it's for
- {LAUNCH_TYPE} – Type (e.g., "new product in existing market", "existing product in new market", "brand new company")
- {LAUNCH_DATE} – Target launch date
- {BUDGET} – Available budget (e.g., "$5K", "$50K", "$500K")
- {TEAM_SIZE} – How many people (e.g., "just me", "small team of 3", "full marketing department")
- {AWARENESS_LEVEL} – Current brand awareness (e.g., "zero — brand new", "known in niche", "established brand")

## Notes
- The best GTM strategies start with the customer, not the product. Lead with the problem you solve, not the features you built.
- Budget constraints force creativity. Some of the best launches were done with almost no budget but exceptional positioning and word-of-mouth strategy.
- The first 90 days after launch are critical. Have a clear decision framework for what metrics tell you to keep going, pivot, or stop.
