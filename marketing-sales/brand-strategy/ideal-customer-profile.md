## Purpose
Define your Ideal Customer Profile at the company level (B2B) to focus your sales and marketing efforts on the highest-value, highest-fit accounts.

## Prompt
Help me define my Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) — the type of company most likely to buy from us, get massive value, stay long-term, and become a referral source.

**Our Product/Service:** {PRODUCT}
**Our Best Customers (describe 3–5):** {BEST_CUSTOMERS}
**Our Worst Customers (describe 2–3):** {WORST_CUSTOMERS}
**Average Deal Size:** {DEAL_SIZE}
**Average Customer Lifetime:** {CUSTOMER_LIFETIME}
**Highest Churn Segments:** {HIGH_CHURN}

### 1. ICP Definition
Based on patterns in your best customers:
- **Industry vertical(s):** Which industries are the best fit?
- **Company size:** Revenue range, employee count
- **Geography:** Where are they located?
- **Technology stack:** What tools do they already use that indicate fit?
- **Business model:** B2B, B2C, SaaS, marketplace, etc.
- **Growth stage:** Startup, growth, mature, enterprise
- **Trigger events:** What recent events signal they're ready to buy? (funding, new hire, expansion, competitor switch)

### 2. Anti-ICP (Disqualification Criteria)
- Company characteristics that signal a BAD fit
- Red flags in the sales process
- "We should NOT sell to companies that..."

### 3. ICP Scoring Framework
| Criteria | Weight | Tier 1 (Perfect) | Tier 2 (Good) | Tier 3 (Marginal) |
|----------|--------|------------------|---------------|-------------------|

### 4. Firmographic + Technographic Signals
- How to find ICP companies using data tools
- Job postings that signal need for your product
- Technology indicators (built with, powered by, integrates with)
- Company signals (funding stage, growth rate, team composition)

### 5. Actionable Target List Criteria
- Specific filters to use in LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Apollo, or ZoomInfo
- Estimated total addressable market (TAM) based on this ICP

## Variables
- {PRODUCT} – What you sell
- {BEST_CUSTOMERS} – Describe your happiest, highest-paying, longest-retained customers
- {WORST_CUSTOMERS} – Describe customers who churned fast, complained, or were unprofitable
- {DEAL_SIZE} – Average contract value
- {CUSTOMER_LIFETIME} – How long customers typically stay
- {HIGH_CHURN} – Which types of customers leave the fastest

## Notes
- Your ICP should be defined by who gets the MOST value from your product, not who is willing to pay. High-value customers stay, expand, and refer. Bad-fit customers churn and leave bad reviews.
- The Anti-ICP is just as important as the ICP. Knowing who NOT to sell to saves enormous time and resources.
- Revisit your ICP every 6 months. As your product evolves, your ideal customer may shift.
