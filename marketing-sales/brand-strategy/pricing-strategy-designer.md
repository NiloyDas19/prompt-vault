## Purpose
Design a pricing strategy that maximizes revenue, communicates value, and aligns with your market positioning and customer expectations.

## Prompt
Help me design a pricing strategy for my product/service. I need pricing that captures the value we deliver without leaving money on the table.

**Product/Service:** {PRODUCT}
**Current Pricing (if any):** {CURRENT_PRICING}
**Target Market:** {TARGET_MARKET}
**Competitor Pricing:** {COMPETITOR_PRICING}
**Cost to Deliver:** {COST_BASIS}
**Customer Value Delivered:** {VALUE_DELIVERED}
**Business Goal:** {GOAL}

### 1. Pricing Model Options
Evaluate and recommend from:
- Per-seat / per-user pricing
- Tiered pricing (good/better/best)
- Usage-based pricing
- Flat-rate pricing
- Freemium + premium
- Value-based pricing
- Outcome-based pricing
For each applicable model: pros, cons, and fit for your business.

### 2. Recommended Pricing Architecture
- Tier names and positioning (not "Basic/Pro/Enterprise" — more creative)
- What's included at each tier
- Price points for each tier with rationale
- The "anchor" tier (designed to make the recommended tier look like a bargain)
- Feature gating strategy: what to limit and what to include everywhere

### 3. Pricing Psychology
- Anchoring strategy
- Decoy pricing option (if applicable)
- Charm pricing ($X.99 vs. $X.00) — recommendation based on positioning
- Annual vs. monthly pricing differential
- Price framing: how to present the price (per day, per user, vs. total)

### 4. Pricing Page Copy
- Headline for the pricing page
- How to frame each tier's value
- CTA text for each tier
- FAQ section addressing pricing concerns
- Trust elements to include (money-back guarantee, free trial details)

### 5. Discounting Strategy
- When to offer discounts (and when NOT to)
- Annual prepayment discount recommendation
- Launch/promotional pricing approach
- Enterprise custom pricing guidelines

### 6. Price Increase Strategy
- How and when to raise prices
- How to communicate price increases to existing customers
- Grandfather clause recommendations

## Variables
- {PRODUCT} – What you sell
- {CURRENT_PRICING} – Current price (or "we haven't priced it yet")
- {TARGET_MARKET} – Who buys this
- {COMPETITOR_PRICING} – What competitors charge
- {COST_BASIS} – Your cost to deliver the product/service
- {VALUE_DELIVERED} – Estimated value to the customer (time saved, revenue generated, cost avoided)
- {GOAL} – Pricing objective (e.g., "maximize revenue", "drive adoption first", "move upmarket")

## Notes
- Most businesses underprice. If no one ever complains about your price, you're probably too cheap.
- The 10x rule: your product should deliver at least 10x the value of its price. If it does, the price feels like a bargain.
- Never compete on price unless cost leadership is your deliberate strategy. Otherwise, you're just training customers to wait for discounts.
