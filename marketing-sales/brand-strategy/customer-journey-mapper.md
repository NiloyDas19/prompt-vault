## Purpose
Map the complete customer journey from first awareness to loyal advocacy, identifying key touchpoints, emotions, and opportunities for improvement.

## Prompt
Map the complete customer journey for my business, from the moment someone first hears about us to becoming a loyal advocate.

**Business:** {BUSINESS}
**Product/Service:** {PRODUCT}
**Customer Persona:** {PERSONA}
**Business Model:** {BUSINESS_MODEL}
**Average Sales Cycle:** {SALES_CYCLE}
**Current Known Pain Points in the Journey:** {KNOWN_ISSUES}

### Journey Map Stages:

For each stage, provide:

**1. Awareness** — "I have a problem"
- How they discover the problem exists
- Where they go for information
- Touchpoints with our brand (ads, content, word of mouth)
- Their emotional state
- Key question on their mind
- Our opportunity: what we should provide at this stage

**2. Consideration** — "I'm exploring solutions"
- What they're comparing
- Research behaviors (Google, reviews, asking peers)
- Touchpoints (website, social proof, demos)
- Their emotional state (hopeful? overwhelmed? skeptical?)
- Key question on their mind
- Our opportunity

**3. Decision** — "I'm choosing between options"
- Decision criteria (price, features, trust, ease)
- Who else is involved in the decision
- Touchpoints (sales calls, proposals, free trials)
- Emotional state (anxious? excited? cautious?)
- Key objections at this stage
- Our opportunity

**4. Onboarding** — "I just bought, now what?"
- First 7 days experience
- Key actions they need to complete
- Emotional state (excited → confused → either delighted or frustrated)
- Biggest risk: what causes early churn
- Our opportunity

**5. Retention** — "Am I getting value?"
- Ongoing touchpoints (check-ins, emails, usage)
- What makes them stay
- What triggers churn
- Emotional state
- Our opportunity

**6. Advocacy** — "I love this and I'm telling others"
- What triggers a referral
- How they share (reviews, social, word of mouth)
- How we can facilitate advocacy
- Emotional state
- Our opportunity

### Deliverables:
- A stage-by-stage journey map table
- Key "moments of truth" — the 3–5 make-or-break moments in the journey
- Quick wins: 3 improvements you can make this week
- Long-term improvements: 3 strategic changes for next quarter

## Variables
- {BUSINESS} – Your business
- {PRODUCT} – Your product/service
- {PERSONA} – Target customer persona
- {BUSINESS_MODEL} – How you sell (B2B SaaS, DTC e-commerce, etc.)
- {SALES_CYCLE} – Average time from awareness to purchase
- {KNOWN_ISSUES} – Pain points you already know about in the customer experience

## Notes
- The most overlooked stages are Onboarding and Retention. Most marketing budgets are spent on Awareness and Acquisition, but the highest ROI improvements are often post-purchase.
- "Moments of truth" are the points where customers either become fans or leave forever. Find and obsess over these.
- Validate this journey map with real customer interviews — your assumptions about the journey are almost certainly wrong in places.
