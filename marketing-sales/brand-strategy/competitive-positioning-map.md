## Purpose
Analyze your competitive landscape and define a unique positioning that differentiates your brand in a crowded market.

## Prompt
Help me map my competitive landscape and find a positioning that makes my brand the obvious choice for my target audience.

**My Brand:** {MY_BRAND}
**My Product/Service:** {MY_PRODUCT}
**My Current Positioning (if any):** {CURRENT_POSITIONING}
**Target Market:** {TARGET_MARKET}
**Competitors:** {COMPETITORS}
**My Strengths:** {STRENGTHS}
**My Weaknesses:** {WEAKNESSES}

### 1. Competitive Landscape Map
Create a 2×2 positioning matrix using the two most important differentiating axes for this market. Example axes:
- Premium vs. Budget
- Simple vs. Full-featured
- Self-serve vs. White-glove
- Generalist vs. Specialist
Place each competitor and my brand on the matrix. Identify the "white space" — positions that are underserved.

### 2. Competitor Deep Dive
For each competitor:
- Their positioning statement (as they present themselves)
- Their target customer
- Their key strengths
- Their biggest vulnerability
- What their customers complain about (common review themes)

### 3. Points of Parity vs. Points of Difference
- **Points of Parity** — Things you MUST have to compete (table stakes)
- **Points of Difference** — Things that make you uniquely valuable
- **Points of Irrelevance** — Things competitors promote that customers don't actually care about

### 4. Positioning Statement
Using the format: "For {target customer} who {need}, {brand} is the {category} that {key benefit} because {reason to believe}. Unlike {competitors}, we {key differentiator}."
Provide 3 variations with different angles.

### 5. Positioning Strategy Recommendation
- Should you position as "better" (compete on the same criteria but outperform) or "different" (create a new category or criteria)?
- Recommended positioning strategy with rationale
- How to communicate this positioning across: website, sales conversations, advertising, and social media

## Variables
- {MY_BRAND} – Your brand name
- {MY_PRODUCT} – What you offer
- {CURRENT_POSITIONING} – How you currently position yourself
- {TARGET_MARKET} – Who you serve
- {COMPETITORS} – Key competitors (3–5)
- {STRENGTHS} – Your genuine advantages
- {WEAKNESSES} – Your honest weaknesses

## Notes
- "Different" beats "better" almost every time. It's easier to be the only one in a category than the best one.
- Your positioning should make some people say "that's not for me" — if everyone likes it, it's not specific enough.
- Positioning is a decision, not a discovery. You choose where to compete and how to be perceived.
