## Purpose
Build a complete brand messaging framework that aligns your value proposition, key messages, proof points, and audience-specific messaging across all channels.

## Prompt
Create a brand messaging framework that serves as the single source of truth for all marketing and sales communications.

**Brand:** {BRAND_NAME}
**What We Do:** {WHAT_WE_DO}
**Who We Serve:** {AUDIENCES}
**Why We Exist (mission):** {MISSION}
**What Makes Us Different:** {DIFFERENTIATORS}

### Messaging Architecture:

**1. Brand Essence** (3–5 words)
- The core idea that everything ladders up to

**2. Positioning Statement**
- For {audience} who {need}, {brand} is the {category} that {benefit} because {reason}.

**3. Elevator Pitch** (30 seconds)
- The "cocktail party" version: what do you say when someone asks "What does your company do?"

**4. Key Messages (3 Pillars)**
For each pillar:
- **Message** — A clear, benefit-driven statement
- **Supporting proof points** — 2–3 pieces of evidence (data, testimonials, features)
- **Customer language** — How customers actually describe this benefit in their own words

**5. Audience-Specific Messaging**
For each audience segment ({AUDIENCES}):
- What they care about most
- Primary message to lead with
- Objection to preempt
- CTA that resonates

**6. Messaging Hierarchy**
- What to say first, second, and third
- The "if you only have one sentence" version
- The "if you have a paragraph" version
- The "if you have a full page" version

**7. Boilerplate Copy**
- 1-sentence company description
- 3-sentence company description
- Full paragraph company description
- Ready for press releases, directories, and "About Us" pages

## Variables
- {BRAND_NAME} – Your brand
- {WHAT_WE_DO} – What your company does
- {AUDIENCES} – 2–3 key audience segments
- {MISSION} – Why the company exists
- {DIFFERENTIATORS} – What makes you unique

## Notes
- A messaging framework prevents the "too many cooks" problem. When everyone has a shared reference, all communications are consistent.
- Update the framework whenever you enter a new market, launch a major product, or rebrand.
- Test your messaging with actual customers — the words that sound best internally are not always the words that resonate externally.
