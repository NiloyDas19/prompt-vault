## Purpose
Define the right growth metrics, KPIs, and dashboard structure to track what matters and stop drowning in vanity metrics.

## Prompt
Help me design a growth metrics framework and dashboard. I need to know exactly what to measure, how to measure it, and what actions to take based on the data.

**Business:** {BUSINESS}
**Business Model:** {BUSINESS_MODEL}
**Stage:** {STAGE}
**Current Tools:** {ANALYTICS_TOOLS}
**Team:** {TEAM}

### 1. North Star Metric
- Recommend ONE North Star Metric for this business
- Why this metric matters more than any other
- How to calculate it
- What "good" looks like (benchmark)

### 3. Leading vs. Lagging Indicators
- Leading indicators that predict future growth
- Lagging indicators that confirm past performance
- Which to use for weekly decision-making vs. monthly/quarterly reporting

### 4. Dashboard Layout
Design a one-page dashboard:
- Top section: North Star Metric (trend over time)
- Middle section: AARRR metrics (current month vs. target)
- Bottom section: Experiment results and active tests
- Alert thresholds: when to panic vs. when it's noise

### 5. Reporting Cadence
- What to review daily (1-2 metrics)
- What to review weekly (team meeting dashboard)
- What to review monthly (strategic review)
- What to review quarterly (board-level metrics)

### 6. Vanity Metrics to Stop Tracking
- Metrics that feel important but don't drive decisions
- What to track instead

## Variables
- {BUSINESS} – Your business
- {BUSINESS_MODEL} – Model (SaaS, e-commerce, marketplace, etc.)
- {STAGE} – Business stage
- {ANALYTICS_TOOLS} – Current tools (e.g., "Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Stripe")
- {TEAM} – Who will use this dashboard (e.g., "just me", "small team", "cross-functional")

## Notes
- If a metric doesn't change your behavior when it goes up or down, stop tracking it. That's a vanity metric.
- Revenue is a lagging indicator. By the time revenue drops, the problem started weeks ago. Track leading indicators to catch problems early.
- Less is more. A dashboard with 5 metrics you actually act on beats a dashboard with 50 metrics you glance at and ignore.
