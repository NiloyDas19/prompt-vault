## Purpose
Design a community-led growth strategy that builds a loyal user community which drives organic acquisition, retention, and product development.

## Prompt
Help me design a community-led growth strategy that turns our users into a thriving community that drives growth organically.

**Product:** {PRODUCT}
**Current Community (if any):** {CURRENT_COMMUNITY}
**Target Members:** {TARGET_MEMBERS}
**Resources Available:** {RESOURCES}
**Community Platform Preference:** {PLATFORM_PREFERENCE}

### 1. Community Strategy Foundation
- Community purpose statement: why should someone join?
- Value proposition for members (what they get)
- Value proposition for your business (what you get)
- Community type: support community, learning community, or social community?

### 2. Platform Selection
Evaluate and recommend:
- Discord, Slack, Circle, Forum, Facebook Group, or Reddit
- Pros and cons for your specific audience
- Migration strategy if switching platforms

### 3. Launch Plan (First 30 Days)
- How to seed the community with initial members
- First 10 pieces of content to post
- Conversation starters that work
- How to avoid the "empty room" problem
- Founding member program: incentives for early adopters

### 4. Content & Programming
- Weekly content calendar for the community
- Event ideas: AMAs, workshops, challenges, member spotlights
- User-generated content prompts
- Gamification: badges, levels, recognition programs

### 5. Growth Flywheel
- How community members become product advocates
- Integration between community insights and product development
- How community content fuels your content marketing
- Referral mechanics within the community

### 6. Moderation & Health
- Community guidelines template
- Moderation strategy (as it scales)
- How to handle negativity, spam, and conflicts
- Health metrics: engagement rate, response time, member satisfaction

## Variables
- {PRODUCT} – Your product
- {CURRENT_COMMUNITY} – What exists today (or "starting from scratch")
- {TARGET_MEMBERS} – Who should join (e.g., "our power users", "industry professionals", "aspiring learners")
- {RESOURCES} – What you can invest (e.g., "1 community manager", "just me part-time")
- {PLATFORM_PREFERENCE} – Preferred platform or "help me decide"

## Notes
- Communities take 6–12 months to build real momentum. This is a long-term investment, not a quick growth hack.
- The best communities are built around a shared identity or goal, not around a product. "A community for growth marketers" > "A community for [Product] users."
- Start small and exclusive. A community of 50 engaged members is more valuable than 5,000 lurkers.
