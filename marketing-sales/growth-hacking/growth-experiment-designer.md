## Purpose
Design structured growth experiments with clear hypotheses, metrics, and success criteria to systematically find what drives growth.

## Prompt
Help me design 5 growth experiments to run this month. Each experiment should have a clear hypothesis, be measurable, and be executable with limited resources.

**Product:** {PRODUCT}
**Current Stage:** {STAGE}
**Primary Growth Metric (North Star):** {NORTH_STAR}
**Current Performance:** {CURRENT_METRICS}
**Team/Budget Constraints:** {CONSTRAINTS}
**Growth Model:** {GROWTH_MODEL}

For each experiment, provide:

### Experiment Template:
- **Name:** Descriptive experiment name
- **Category:** Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Revenue, or Referral (AARRR)
- **Hypothesis:** "We believe that {change} will {impact} for {audience} because {reasoning}"
- **Metric to track:** Primary and secondary metrics
- **Success criteria:** "This experiment is a success if {metric} improves by {X}%"
- **Minimum viable test:** The smallest version of this test you can run
- **Estimated effort:** Hours/days to implement
- **Estimated impact:** Low/Medium/High on the North Star metric
- **ICE Score:** Impact (1-10) × Confidence (1-10) × Ease (1-10) = Total score
- **How to run it:** Step-by-step instructions
- **Timeline:** How long to run before making a decision
- **Decision framework:** What to do if it works vs. if it doesn't

### Experiment Prioritization:
Rank all 5 experiments by ICE score and recommend which to run first.

### Experiment Log Template:
Provide a simple tracking template for recording results over time.

## Variables
- {PRODUCT} – Your product
- {STAGE} – Business stage (e.g., "pre-PMF", "early growth", "scaling", "mature")
- {NORTH_STAR} – Primary growth metric (e.g., "weekly active users", "monthly recurring revenue", "orders per month")
- {CURRENT_METRICS} – Current numbers for key metrics
- {CONSTRAINTS} – Team size and budget limitations
- {GROWTH_MODEL} – How you grow (e.g., "product-led growth", "sales-led", "content/SEO", "viral/referral")

## Notes
- Run experiments for a minimum of 2 weeks or until statistical significance. Stopping early leads to false conclusions.
- Only test ONE variable per experiment. Multi-variable tests are impossible to learn from.
- Document every experiment, even failures. Your experimentation log becomes your most valuable growth asset over time.
