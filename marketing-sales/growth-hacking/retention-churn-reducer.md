## Purpose
Identify churn risks, design retention interventions, and build engagement loops that keep customers active and paying.

## Prompt
Help me reduce churn and increase retention. Analyze my situation and design specific interventions.

**Product:** {PRODUCT}
**Current Monthly Churn Rate:** {CHURN_RATE}
**Average Customer Lifetime:** {AVG_LIFETIME}
**Top Churn Reasons (if known):** {CHURN_REASONS}
**Customer Segments:** {SEGMENTS}
**Current Retention Efforts:** {CURRENT_EFFORTS}

### 1. Churn Risk Scorecard
Design an early warning system:
- 5 behavioral signals that predict churn (e.g., "hasn't logged in for 14 days", "support ticket spike")
- For each signal: risk level, detection method, and recommended intervention

### 2. Retention Interventions
**Immediate (this week):**
- 3 quick wins to reduce churn in the next 30 days

**Short-term (this month):**
- Email sequences triggered by churn risk signals
- In-app interventions for at-risk users
- Customer success playbook for high-value accounts

**Strategic (next quarter):**
- Product changes that address root causes of churn
- Habit loop design: daily/weekly engagement triggers
- Community building strategies for stickiness

### 3. Engagement Loop Design
Design a daily/weekly engagement cycle:
- **Trigger:** What brings the user back (notification, email, habit)
- **Action:** What they do when they return
- **Variable Reward:** What unpredictable value they find
- **Investment:** What they put in that makes leaving harder (data, customization, connections)

### 4. Win-Back Campaign
For users who have already churned:
- 3-email win-back sequence
- Optimal timing post-churn
- What offer or change to highlight
- When to give up on a churned customer

### 5. Retention Metrics Dashboard
- KPIs to track weekly
- Cohort retention curve analysis
- Revenue retention vs. logo retention
- Leading vs. lagging indicators

## Variables
- {PRODUCT} – Your product
- {CHURN_RATE} – Monthly churn rate (e.g., "5%", "8%")
- {AVG_LIFETIME} – Average customer lifespan (e.g., "14 months", "6 months")
- {CHURN_REASONS} – Known reasons people leave
- {SEGMENTS} – Customer segments with different retention patterns
- {CURRENT_EFFORTS} – What you're currently doing to retain customers

## Notes
- Reducing churn by 1% often has a larger revenue impact than increasing acquisition by 10%. Retention is the most underinvested growth lever.
- The first 90 days are critical. Customers who survive their first 90 days are 3–5x more likely to stay for a year.
- "Switching costs" (data invested, integrations set up, team trained) are your most powerful retention mechanism. Design your product to become more valuable the more someone uses it.
