## Purpose
Design a viral loop or referral program that turns your existing customers into your best acquisition channel.

## Prompt
Design a referral program or viral mechanism for my product that makes sharing feel natural and rewarding, not forced.

**Product:** {PRODUCT}
**Business Model:** {BUSINESS_MODEL}
**Current Growth Channel:** {CURRENT_CHANNELS}
**Viral Coefficient (if known):** {VIRAL_COEFFICIENT}
**Average Customer LTV:** {LTV}
**Target Audience:** {AUDIENCE}

### 1. Viral Loop Design
Map the complete loop:
- **Trigger**: What motivates a user to share?
- **Share mechanism**: How do they share? (link, invite, social post, embed)
- **Recipient experience**: What does the invited person see/experience?
- **Conversion**: How does the recipient become a user?
- **Reward**: What does the sharer get when their friend converts?
- **Loop restart**: How does the new user become a sharer themselves?

### 2. Referral Program Options (3 concepts)

**Option A: Double-Sided Incentive**
- What the referrer gets
- What the referee gets
- Implementation details

**Option B: Status/Gamification**
- Leaderboards, tiers, or badges
- Exclusive access or features for top referrers
- Community recognition

**Option C: Product-Native Virality**
- How to build sharing into the core product experience
- Examples: collaborative features, shareable outputs, public profiles
- No explicit "referral program" needed — sharing IS the product

### 3. Incentive Economics
- Recommended referral incentive value (based on LTV and CAC)
- Break-even analysis: "Each referral costs you $X but brings in $Y in LTV"
- Fraud prevention: how to prevent gaming and self-referrals

### 4. Messaging & UX
- When and where to prompt referrals (post-purchase, after value moment, milestone)
- Referral page copy and CTA
- Share message templates (email, text, social)
- The "moment of delight" — when users are happiest and most likely to share

### 5. Growth Projections
- If each user refers {X} friends at {Y}% conversion rate, here's the projected growth curve
- What viral coefficient you need to achieve self-sustaining growth

## Variables
- {PRODUCT} – Your product
- {BUSINESS_MODEL} – How you sell (SaaS, e-commerce, marketplace, etc.)
- {CURRENT_CHANNELS} – How you currently acquire users
- {VIRAL_COEFFICIENT} – Current viral coefficient if known (how many new users each user brings)
- {LTV} – Average customer lifetime value
- {AUDIENCE} – Who uses your product

## Notes
- The best referral programs reward BOTH sides. One-sided rewards feel extractive.
- "Product-native virality" (Option C) is the most powerful and sustainable form of growth. Dropbox (shared folders), Figma (collaborative design), and Notion (shared workspaces) all grew this way.
- Time your referral ask to the "aha moment" — the point when users first experience core value. Asking before this point has near-zero response rates.
