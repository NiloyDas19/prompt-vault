## Purpose
Create a competitive sales battlecard that arms your sales team with quick-reference talking points, positioning, and win strategies against specific competitors.

## Prompt
Create a competitive battlecard for my sales team to use when prospects mention or are evaluating {COMPETITOR}.

**Our Product:** {OUR_PRODUCT}
**Competitor:** {COMPETITOR}
**Competitor's Product:** {COMPETITOR_PRODUCT}
**Our Target Market:** {TARGET_MARKET}
**Common Deal Scenarios:** {DEAL_SCENARIOS}

### Battlecard Sections:

**1. Quick Overview**
| | Us | {COMPETITOR} |
|---|---|---|
| Best for | | |
| Pricing model | | |
| Key strength | | |
| Key weakness | | |
| Typical customer | | |

**2. Where We Win**
- 3 scenarios where we consistently beat this competitor
- The specific features or capabilities that give us the edge
- Proof points: customer quotes, data, or case studies

**3. Where They Win**
- 2 scenarios where they have an advantage (be honest)
- How to mitigate their advantage in the conversation
- When to walk away from a deal if their solution is genuinely a better fit

**4. Landmine Questions**
- 5 questions our reps should ask the prospect that expose the competitor's weaknesses
- Example: "How important is {capability we have and they don't} to your team?"
- These should be genuine questions, not leading or manipulative

**5. Counter Their Pitch**
- What their sales team typically says about us (their common attacks)
- Our response to each attack
- Framework: "When they say {X}, we say {Y}, because {evidence}"

**6. Objection Reframes**
- "But {competitor} is cheaper" → Our response
- "But {competitor} has feature X" → Our response
- "We've always used {competitor}" → Our response

**7. Win Story**
- A 2-minute customer story of someone who switched from {COMPETITOR} to us
- The problem, the switch, and the result

## Variables
- {OUR_PRODUCT} – Your product name
- {COMPETITOR} – Competitor name
- {COMPETITOR_PRODUCT} – Their product name
- {TARGET_MARKET} – Who you both sell to
- {DEAL_SCENARIOS} – Common competitive situations (e.g., "head-to-head evaluation", "rip and replace", "greenfield")

## Notes
- Honesty is the best competitive strategy. Acknowledging where competitors are strong builds trust with prospects.
- Update battlecards quarterly — competitors change fast.
- The best salespeople never bad-mouth competitors. They ask great questions that let the prospect discover the differences themselves.
