## Purpose
Build a flexible sales call script with discovery questions, objection handling, and closing techniques tailored to your product and buyer persona.

## Prompt
Create a complete sales call script for a {CALL_TYPE} call. The script should feel natural and conversational, not robotic — it's a guide, not a teleprompter.

**Product/Service:** {PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE}
**Price Point:** {PRICE}
**Call Type:** {CALL_TYPE}
**Prospect Persona:** {PROSPECT_PERSONA}
**Average Call Length:** {CALL_LENGTH}
**Sales Methodology:** {METHODOLOGY}

### Script Sections:

**1. Opening (30 seconds)**
- Pattern interrupt: something unexpected that isn't "How are you today?"
- Establish credibility in one sentence
- Set the agenda: "Here's what I'd like to cover today..."
- Get permission: "Does that sound good?"

**2. Discovery Questions (5–10 minutes)**
Write 10 discovery questions in this order:
- **Situation** (2): Understand their current state
- **Problem** (3): Uncover pain points and frustrations
- **Impact** (3): Quantify the cost of the problem
- **Future State** (2): Understand their desired outcome
For each question, provide a follow-up probe and what buying signal to listen for.

**3. Presentation / Demo (5–10 minutes)**
- How to transition from discovery to presentation
- 3 key talking points, each tied to a pain point uncovered in discovery
- Framework: "{Pain they mentioned} → Here's how we solve it → {Proof/result}"
- Questions to ask during the demo to keep it interactive

**4. Objection Handling**
Prepare responses for these common objections:
- "It's too expensive" / "We don't have the budget"
- "We need to think about it" / "Let me talk to my team"
- "We're already using {competitor}"
- "The timing isn't right"
- "Can you send me some information?"
For each: acknowledge, reframe, bridge back to value.

**5. Closing (2–3 minutes)**
- 3 closing techniques appropriate for this sale type
- Assumptive close: "Should we start with the annual or monthly plan?"
- Summary close: Recap the pains and how you solve them, then ask for the commitment
- Next-step close: Define the specific next action with a date

**6. Post-Call Follow-Up**
- Follow-up email template (within 2 hours)
- What to include: summary of discussion, next steps, relevant resources

## Variables
- {PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE} – What you're selling
- {PRICE} – Price range
- {CALL_TYPE} – Type (e.g., "discovery call", "demo", "closing call", "cold call")
- {PROSPECT_PERSONA} – Who you're calling (title, company size, industry)
- {CALL_LENGTH} – Expected duration (e.g., "15 minutes", "30 minutes", "1 hour")
- {METHODOLOGY} – Sales framework (e.g., "SPIN Selling", "Challenger Sale", "MEDDIC", "Sandler", "consultative")

## Notes
- The best salespeople talk less than 40% of the time on discovery calls. Your script should be mostly questions.
- Never present before you discover. Presenting features to someone who hasn't told you their problems is the #1 sales mistake.
- Record your calls (with permission) and compare your actual performance to this script weekly.
