## Purpose
Transform raw meeting notes into structured summaries with action items, buying signals, and CRM-ready updates.

## Prompt
Convert my raw meeting notes into a structured summary, actionable follow-up plan, and CRM update. Extract every relevant detail.

**Meeting Type:** {MEETING_TYPE}
**Prospect:** {PROSPECT}
**Attendees:** {ATTENDEES}
**Date:** {DATE}
**My Raw Notes:**
{RAW_NOTES}

### Generate:

**1. Executive Summary** (3–4 sentences)
- What was discussed, key decisions made, overall sentiment

**2. Key Pain Points Identified**
- List each pain point mentioned
- For each: how urgent it is (1–5) and whether our product addresses it

**3. Buying Signals**
- Positive signals (interest, urgency, budget confirmation, timeline mentions)
- Negative signals (hesitation, objections, competing priorities)

**4. Stakeholder Map Update**
- Who was in the meeting and their role in the decision
- Who was mentioned but not present (and whether we need to engage them)
- Champion identification: who is most likely to advocate for us internally?

**5. Competitive Intelligence**
- Any competitors mentioned? What was said about them?
- What alternatives are they considering?

**6. Action Items**
| # | Action | Owner | Deadline | Priority |
|---|--------|-------|----------|----------|
| 1 | | | | |

**7. Follow-Up Email Draft**
- Professional email summarizing the meeting and confirming next steps
- Attached resources or materials discussed
- Calendar invite for the next meeting

**8. CRM Update**
- Recommended pipeline stage change
- Updated deal value (if new info)
- Key fields to update
- Notes to add to the contact record

## Variables
- {MEETING_TYPE} – Type (e.g., "discovery call", "demo", "negotiation", "QBR")
- {PROSPECT} – Company and contact names
- {ATTENDEES} – Who was in the meeting
- {DATE} – Meeting date
- {RAW_NOTES} – Paste your unstructured meeting notes

## Notes
- The best time to process meeting notes is within 1 hour of the meeting ending — before you forget nuances.
- A structured follow-up email sent within 2 hours of a meeting signals professionalism and builds trust.
- Always identify and document action items for BOTH sides (your team AND the prospect). Mutual action plans keep deals moving.
