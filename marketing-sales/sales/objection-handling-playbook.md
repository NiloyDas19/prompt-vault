## Purpose
Build a comprehensive objection handling playbook with scripted responses for every common sales objection your team encounters.

## Prompt
Create a complete objection handling playbook for my sales team. For every common objection, provide multiple response strategies.

**Product/Service:** {PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE}
**Price Point:** {PRICE}
**Sales Cycle Length:** {SALES_CYCLE}
**Main Competitors:** {COMPETITORS}
**Target Buyer:** {BUYER_PERSONA}

For each of the following objection categories, provide 3 response frameworks:

### Price Objections
1. "It's too expensive"
2. "We don't have the budget right now"
3. "Your competitor is cheaper"
4. "Can you give us a discount?"

### Timing Objections
5. "We're not ready yet"
6. "Call me back next quarter"
7. "We just signed a contract with someone else"

### Authority Objections
8. "I need to run this by my boss/team"
9. "I'm not the decision maker"
10. "Our procurement process takes months"

### Need Objections
11. "We're happy with what we have"
12. "We don't see the need for this"
13. "We tried something similar and it didn't work"

### Trust Objections
14. "I've never heard of your company"
15. "Can you guarantee results?"
16. "We need more references"

For each objection, provide:
- **Why they're really saying this** (the real concern behind the words)
- **Response A: Feel-Felt-Found** — Empathize, relate, redirect
- **Response B: Isolate & Resolve** — "If we solved this concern, would you move forward?"
- **Response C: Reframe** — Change the perspective entirely
- **Red flag indicator** — When this objection is genuine and you should walk away
- **Follow-up question** to ask after addressing the objection

## Variables
- {PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE} – What you sell
- {PRICE} – Price range
- {SALES_CYCLE} – How long deals take
- {COMPETITORS} – Main competitors
- {BUYER_PERSONA} – Who you're selling to

## Notes
- Objections are not rejections — they're requests for more information. The worst response is "let me send you a brochure."
- Most objections boil down to 4 root causes: price, trust, timing, or need. Diagnose the root cause before responding.
- Practice responses out loud. Reading them isn't the same as saying them naturally.
