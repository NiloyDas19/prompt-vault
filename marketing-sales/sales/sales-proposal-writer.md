## Purpose
Write a compelling, professional sales proposal that positions your solution as the obvious choice and moves the deal toward close.

## Prompt
Write a professional sales proposal based on the following discovery information. The proposal should feel custom and consultative, not templated.

**Prospect Company:** {PROSPECT_COMPANY}
**Prospect Contact:** {CONTACT_NAME}, {CONTACT_TITLE}
**Our Company:** {OUR_COMPANY}
**Our Solution:** {OUR_SOLUTION}
**Key Pain Points Discovered:** {PAIN_POINTS}
**Desired Outcomes:** {DESIRED_OUTCOMES}
**Budget Discussed:** {BUDGET}
**Timeline:** {TIMELINE}
**Decision Criteria:** {CRITERIA}
**Stakeholders Involved:** {STAKEHOLDERS}

### Proposal Structure:

**1. Executive Summary** (1 paragraph)
- Restate their problem and desired outcome in their own words
- Position your solution as the path from problem to outcome
- One sentence on why you're uniquely qualified

**2. Understanding Your Challenges**
- Mirror back the specific pain points they shared
- Quantify the cost of inaction: "Based on our conversation, this is costing you approximately {estimated cost}"
- Show you understand their world better than they expected

**3. Proposed Solution**
- What you're recommending (scope, deliverables, timeline)
- For each deliverable, tie it to a specific pain point: "This addresses your challenge with {X}"
- Implementation approach: phases, milestones, responsibilities

**4. Expected Outcomes**
- Specific, measurable results they can expect
- Timeline for each outcome
- How you'll measure success together

**5. Why Us**
- 3 reasons to choose your company over alternatives (without naming competitors directly)
- Relevant case study or result from a similar company
- Team expertise relevant to their situation

**6. Investment**
- Pricing presented clearly with options (if applicable)
- ROI calculation: "Based on {pain point cost}, this investment pays for itself in {timeframe}"
- Payment terms and conditions

**7. Next Steps**
- Specific, time-bound next actions
- Who does what by when
- "We'd like to schedule a kickoff call for {suggested date}"

**8. Appendix**
- Team bios, additional case studies, technical details

## Variables
- {PROSPECT_COMPANY} – Their company name
- {CONTACT_NAME} – Primary contact
- {CONTACT_TITLE} – Their title
- {OUR_COMPANY} – Your company
- {OUR_SOLUTION} – Your product/service
- {PAIN_POINTS} – Problems they shared during discovery
- {DESIRED_OUTCOMES} – What success looks like for them
- {BUDGET} – Budget range discussed
- {TIMELINE} – When they want to start/finish
- {CRITERIA} – How they'll decide (e.g., "price, ease of use, support")
- {STAKEHOLDERS} – Who else is involved in the decision

## Notes
- A great proposal feels like it was written specifically for this prospect. Use their exact language, their company name on every page, and reference specific conversations.
- The "Understanding Your Challenges" section is the most important. If the prospect reads it and thinks "they get us," the deal is 80% closed.
- Always include an ROI calculation. Make the math so obvious that the price feels like a bargain.
