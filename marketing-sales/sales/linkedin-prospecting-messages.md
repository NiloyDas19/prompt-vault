## Purpose
Write personalized LinkedIn connection requests and follow-up messages that start genuine conversations and generate sales opportunities.

## Prompt
Write LinkedIn prospecting message sequences for different scenarios. Each message should feel personal and genuine — not like an automated sales blast.

**My Role:** {MY_ROLE}
**My Company:** {MY_COMPANY}
**What I Sell:** {PRODUCT}
**Target Prospects:** {PROSPECT_TYPE}

### Sequence 1: Cold Connection Request
**Connection Note (300 character limit):**
- Personalized reason for connecting (mutual connection, their content, shared interest)
- NO pitch in the connection request

**Follow-up Message 1 (Day 2 after accepted):**
- Thank them for connecting
- Reference something specific from their profile or recent post
- Ask a genuine question about their work
- NO pitch yet

**Follow-up Message 2 (Day 5):**
- Share something valuable: article, insight, or resource relevant to their role
- Brief positioning of what you do (1 sentence max)
- Soft ask: "Would it make sense to connect for 15 minutes?"

**Follow-up Message 3 (Day 10, if no response):**
- Brief, light-hearted final message
- Leave the door open without being pushy

### Sequence 2: Warm Introduction (Mutual Connection)
- Mention the mutual connection by name
- Brief context on why you're reaching out
- Specific value you can offer
- CTA: coffee chat or quick call

### Sequence 3: Engaging with Their Content First
- Comment strategy: what to say on their posts before reaching out (3 example comments)
- Connection request after engaging: reference your comments
- Follow-up: build on the conversation you started in the comments

For each message:
- Full text, ready to send
- Character count (LinkedIn has limits)
- Why this approach works psychologically
- What NOT to say (common LinkedIn outreach mistakes)

## Variables
- {MY_ROLE} – Your job title
- {MY_COMPANY} – Your company
- {PRODUCT} – What you sell (briefly)
- {PROSPECT_TYPE} – Who you're targeting (e.g., "VP of Marketing at B2B SaaS companies")

## Notes
- The #1 rule of LinkedIn outreach: give before you ask. Your first 2 touchpoints should be 100% value, 0% pitch.
- Connection requests with a personalized note have a 40% higher acceptance rate than blank requests.
- NEVER send a pitch in the connection request. It's the LinkedIn equivalent of proposing marriage on the first date.
