## Purpose
Develop a clear, differentiated value proposition that communicates why a customer should choose your product over every alternative.

## Prompt
Help me craft a compelling value proposition for my business. I need a message so clear that a 10-year-old could understand what we do and why it matters.

**Business:** {BUSINESS_DESCRIPTION}
**What we sell:** {PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE}
**Who we serve:** {TARGET_CUSTOMER}
**The problem we solve:** {CORE_PROBLEM}
**How we're different:** {DIFFERENTIATORS}
**Competitors:** {COMPETITORS}

Please provide:

1. **One-liner value prop** — A single sentence: "We help {who} achieve {what} by {how}, without {pain}."
2. **Elevator pitch** (30 seconds) — Expand the one-liner into a concise paragraph
3. **Homepage headline + subheadline** — What should go at the top of our website
4. **Three supporting pillars** — The 3 key reasons to believe (with one-line proof for each)
5. **"So what?" test** — For each claim, explain why the customer should care
6. **Competitive positioning statement** — "Unlike {competitor}, we {key difference} which means {benefit}."
7. **Before/After/Bridge** — Describe the customer's life before, after, and how you bridge the gap

Also flag:
- Any claims that sound generic and suggest how to make them more specific
- Whether the positioning is "better" (incremental) vs "different" (category-creating) — and recommend which to pursue

## Variables
- {BUSINESS_DESCRIPTION} – Brief description of the business
- {PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE} – What you offer
- {TARGET_CUSTOMER} – Who your ideal customer is
- {CORE_PROBLEM} – The #1 problem you solve
- {DIFFERENTIATORS} – What makes you unique (e.g., speed, price, experience, technology)
- {COMPETITORS} – Key competitors in the space

## Notes
- A great value proposition answers: "Why should I buy from YOU instead of anyone else — or instead of doing nothing?"
- The "So what?" test is brutal but essential: keep asking "so what?" after every claim until you reach an emotional truth the customer cares about.
