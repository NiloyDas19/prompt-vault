## Purpose
Generate complete, high-converting landing page copy structured around proven sales page frameworks like PAS, AIDA, or StoryBrand.

## Prompt
Write complete landing page copy for the following product/service. Use the {FRAMEWORK} copywriting framework and structure the page for maximum conversions.

**Product/Service:** {PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE}
**Target Audience:** {TARGET_AUDIENCE}
**Primary Pain Point:** {PAIN_POINT}
**Key Transformation:** {TRANSFORMATION}
**Price Point:** {PRICE}
**Social Proof Available:** {SOCIAL_PROOF}
**Unique Selling Proposition:** {USP}

Structure the landing page with these sections:
1. **Hero Section** — Headline, subheadline, and primary CTA button text
2. **Problem Agitation** — Describe the pain the reader is experiencing (make them feel understood)
3. **Solution Introduction** — Present your product as the answer
4. **Benefits Section** — 5–7 benefit-driven bullet points (focus on outcomes, not features)
5. **How It Works** — 3-step simplified process
6. **Social Proof** — Testimonial-style copy and trust indicators
7. **Objection Handling** — Address the top 3 reasons someone might hesitate
8. **Pricing Section** — Frame the price against the value of the transformation
9. **Final CTA** — Urgency-driven closing with a strong call to action
10. **FAQ Section** — 5 common questions and persuasive answers

For each section, also provide:
- A brief note on the psychological principle being used (e.g., loss aversion, social proof, anchoring)
- Suggested A/B test variations for the headline and CTA

## Variables
- {PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE} – What you're selling
- {TARGET_AUDIENCE} – Ideal customer profile
- {PAIN_POINT} – The core problem your audience faces
- {TRANSFORMATION} – The desired end state after using your product
- {PRICE} – The price point (e.g., "$97/month", "$497 one-time")
- {SOCIAL_PROOF} – What proof you have (e.g., "500+ customers", "featured in Forbes")
- {USP} – What makes you different from competitors
- {FRAMEWORK} – The copywriting framework to use: "PAS" (Problem-Agitate-Solve), "AIDA" (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action), or "StoryBrand"

## Notes
- The PAS framework is best for pain-heavy products (health, finance, productivity). AIDA works well for aspirational products. StoryBrand works best when positioning the customer as the hero.
- After generating, ask: "Now rewrite the hero section to be 30% more emotionally urgent."
