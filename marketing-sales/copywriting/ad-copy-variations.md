## Purpose
Generate multiple ad copy variations for paid advertising campaigns across platforms like Facebook, Google, Instagram, and LinkedIn.

## Prompt
Write ad copy variations for a paid advertising campaign. Generate platform-optimized copy that stops the scroll and drives action.

**Product/Service:** {PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE}
**Campaign Goal:** {CAMPAIGN_GOAL}
**Target Audience:** {TARGET_AUDIENCE}
**Key Offer:** {OFFER}
**Platform:** {PLATFORM}
**Budget Level:** {BUDGET_CONTEXT}

Generate the following:

### For Facebook/Instagram Ads (if applicable):
- **3 Primary Text variations** (short: 1–2 sentences, medium: 3–4 sentences, long: story-style 5–7 sentences)
- **3 Headline variations** (under 40 characters)
- **3 Description variations** (under 30 characters)
- For each, specify the hook type: question, bold claim, statistic, story opener, or pain point

### For Google Search Ads (if applicable):
- **3 Responsive Search Ad variations** with:
  - 5 headlines (max 30 characters each)
  - 3 descriptions (max 90 characters each)
  - All incorporating the target keyword: {TARGET_KEYWORD}

### For LinkedIn Ads (if applicable):
- **3 Sponsored Post copy variations** — professional tone, benefit-driven, with a clear CTA

For ALL variations:
- Highlight the specific psychological angle used (scarcity, social proof, authority, reciprocity, etc.)
- Note which variation you'd test first and why
- Suggest an A/B testing plan: which element to test first, second, and third

## Variables
- {PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE} – What you're advertising
- {CAMPAIGN_GOAL} – Goal (e.g., "lead generation", "product sales", "app installs", "brand awareness")
- {TARGET_AUDIENCE} – Who you're reaching (demographics and psychographics)
- {OFFER} – The hook (e.g., "50% off first month", "free trial", "free shipping", "downloadable guide")
- {PLATFORM} – Where the ads will run
- {TARGET_KEYWORD} – Primary keyword for search ads
- {BUDGET_CONTEXT} – Budget level (e.g., "bootstrapped startup", "scaling with $10K/month", "enterprise budget")

## Notes
- The #1 rule of ad copy: lead with the benefit or the pain, never the feature.
- On Facebook, the first line must stop the scroll. On Google, the headline must match the search intent.
- Always write more variations than you think you need — the winner is often the one you least expect.
