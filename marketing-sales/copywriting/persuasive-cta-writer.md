## Purpose
Generate high-converting call-to-action button text and surrounding microcopy that drives clicks, sign-ups, and purchases.

## Prompt
Generate 15 high-converting CTA (Call-to-Action) variations for the following scenario. Go beyond generic "Submit" or "Buy Now" buttons.

**What happens when they click:** {ACTION_RESULT}
**Page/context:** {PAGE_CONTEXT}
**Target audience:** {AUDIENCE}
**Biggest objection:** {OBJECTION}
**Desired emotion:** {EMOTION}

For each CTA, provide:
1. **Button text** (max 5 words)
2. **Supporting microcopy** — The line of text directly above or below the button (e.g., "No credit card required" or "Join 10,000+ marketers")
3. **Psychological trigger** used (e.g., urgency, curiosity, social proof, loss aversion, identity)

Organize into these categories:
- **Benefit-driven** (3 CTAs): Focus on what the user gets
- **Action-driven** (3 CTAs): Focus on what the user will do
- **Urgency-driven** (3 CTAs): Create time pressure
- **Identity-driven** (3 CTAs): Appeal to who the user wants to become
- **Fear-of-loss** (3 CTAs): Focus on what they'll miss

Then recommend your **top pick** with reasoning.

## Variables
- {ACTION_RESULT} – What happens after clicking (e.g., "they start a 14-day free trial", "they download a PDF guide", "they book a demo call")
- {PAGE_CONTEXT} – Where the CTA lives (e.g., "SaaS pricing page", "webinar registration", "e-commerce product page")
- {AUDIENCE} – Who's clicking (e.g., "small business owners", "enterprise CTOs", "fitness enthusiasts")
- {OBJECTION} – The #1 reason they hesitate (e.g., "not sure if it's worth the money", "worried about commitment")
- {EMOTION} – What you want them to feel (e.g., "excited and empowered", "relieved", "urgent")

## Notes
- Great CTAs complete the sentence: "I want to ___." ("I want to... Start Growing" beats "I want to... Submit.")
- Always pair the button with objection-handling microcopy (e.g., "Cancel anytime" or "100% money-back guarantee").
- Test your top 2 picks as an A/B test — CTA changes are the highest-ROI A/B tests you can run.
