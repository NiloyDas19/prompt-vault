## Purpose
Craft emotionally compelling, story-driven sales copy that uses narrative techniques to persuade and convert.

## Prompt
Write a story-driven sales narrative for the following product/service. Use storytelling to create an emotional connection that makes the reader feel something before they buy.

**Product/Service:** {PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE}
**Hero of the Story:** {CUSTOMER_PERSONA}
**The Villain (Problem):** {PROBLEM_OR_ENEMY}
**The Guide (Your Brand):** {YOUR_BRAND}
**The Transformation:** {DESIRED_OUTCOME}
**Tone:** {TONE}

Structure the narrative using the Hero's Journey adapted for marketing:

1. **The Ordinary World** (2–3 sentences) — Describe the reader's current frustrating reality. Make them nod and think "that's me."
2. **The Inciting Incident** (2 sentences) — The moment they realized something had to change
3. **Meeting the Guide** (2–3 sentences) — How they discovered your product (position yourself as the mentor, not the hero)
4. **The Plan** (3 bullet points) — Show them it's simple: Step 1, Step 2, Step 3
5. **The Climax** (2–3 sentences) — Paint a vivid picture of their life AFTER the transformation
6. **The Stakes** (2 sentences) — What happens if they do nothing (fear of staying the same)
7. **The Call to Adventure** (1–2 sentences) — A clear, emotionally resonant CTA

Also provide:
- **A "cold open" alternative** — An attention-grabbing first line that drops the reader into the middle of the story
- **Key emotional beats** — Map the emotional journey: frustration → hope → clarity → confidence → action
- **Sensory details** — At least 3 specific sensory details woven into the narrative

## Variables
- {PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE} – What you're selling
- {CUSTOMER_PERSONA} – Who the ideal customer is (be specific: name, age, job, frustration)
- {PROBLEM_OR_ENEMY} – What they're fighting against (e.g., "burnout", "wasted ad spend", "slow website")
- {YOUR_BRAND} – Your brand as the "guide" (not the hero)
- {DESIRED_OUTCOME} – What their life looks like after
- {TONE} – Storytelling tone (e.g., "inspiring", "raw and honest", "humorous")

## Notes
- In StoryBrand methodology, the customer is always the hero, never your brand. Your brand is Yoda, not Luke.
- The most powerful stories have a specific, relatable protagonist — not a vague "our customers."
- Use present tense for the transformation scene ("You wake up, check your phone, and see...") to make it feel real.
