## Purpose
Write compelling, benefit-focused e-commerce product descriptions that transform features into emotional buying triggers.

## Prompt
Write a high-converting product description for the following item. Make the reader feel the product in their hands and see it in their life.

**Product:** {PRODUCT_NAME}
**Category:** {PRODUCT_CATEGORY}
**Key Features:** {FEATURES_LIST}
**Target Buyer:** {TARGET_BUYER}
**Price Range:** {PRICE_RANGE}
**Brand Voice:** {BRAND_VOICE}
**Platform:** {PLATFORM}

Provide:
1. **Attention-grabbing opening line** — Hook the reader in one sentence
2. **Emotional benefit paragraph** (3–4 sentences) — Paint a picture of the buyer's life WITH this product
3. **Feature-to-benefit bullets** — For each feature, translate it into a customer benefit using the format: "✅ {Feature} → so you can {benefit}"
4. **Sensory description** — Help the reader feel, see, or experience the product through vivid language
5. **Social proof line** — A sentence that implies popularity or trust (even if generic)
6. **Urgency/scarcity line** — A closing nudge
7. **SEO-optimized version** — Rewrite the description with {TARGET_KEYWORDS} naturally woven in

## Variables
- {PRODUCT_NAME} – The product name
- {PRODUCT_CATEGORY} – Category (e.g., "wireless earbuds", "organic skincare", "standing desk")
- {FEATURES_LIST} – Technical features and specs
- {TARGET_BUYER} – Who buys this (e.g., "busy professionals", "college students", "new parents")
- {PRICE_RANGE} – Price positioning (e.g., "premium $200+", "budget-friendly under $30")
- {BRAND_VOICE} – Tone (e.g., "playful and youthful", "premium and minimalist", "rugged and adventurous")
- {PLATFORM} – Where it'll be listed (e.g., "Amazon", "Shopify store", "Etsy")
- {TARGET_KEYWORDS} – SEO keywords to include

## Notes
- The #1 mistake in product descriptions is listing features without translating them into benefits. "10,000 mAh battery" means nothing. "Never run out of charge on a cross-country flight" means everything.
- For Amazon: front-load the most important keywords in the title and first bullet.
