## Purpose
Generate memorable, sticky brand taglines and slogans that capture the essence of a brand in just a few words.

## Prompt
Generate 25 tagline and slogan options for my brand. I need something memorable enough that people repeat it to their friends.

**Brand Name:** {BRAND_NAME}
**Industry:** {INDUSTRY}
**What we do (simply):** {SIMPLE_DESCRIPTION}
**Brand personality:** {BRAND_PERSONALITY}
**Our audience:** {AUDIENCE}
**Key emotion to evoke:** {EMOTION}
**Competitor taglines to AVOID resembling:** {COMPETITOR_TAGLINES}

Generate taglines using these categories:

**Benefit-Focused (5):** What the customer gets
**Aspirational (5):** Who the customer becomes
**Clever/Witty (5):** Wordplay, double meanings, or humor
**Minimalist (5):** 3 words or fewer
**Provocative (5):** Challenge the status quo

For each tagline:
- Note the rhetorical device used (alliteration, metaphor, contrast, rhyme, repetition, etc.)
- Rate memorability (1–10)
- Explain why it works or doesn't

Then select your **top 5 overall** and explain how each would perform across different touchpoints: logo lockup, billboard, social media bio, and radio ad.

## Variables
- {BRAND_NAME} – Your brand name
- {INDUSTRY} – Your industry (e.g., "sustainable fashion", "B2B SaaS", "premium coffee")
- {SIMPLE_DESCRIPTION} – What you do in plain language
- {BRAND_PERSONALITY} – Brand traits (e.g., "bold and rebellious", "warm and nurturing", "sleek and technical")
- {AUDIENCE} – Who you serve
- {EMOTION} – How you want people to feel (e.g., "empowered", "curious", "safe")
- {COMPETITOR_TAGLINES} – Taglines of competitors to differentiate from

## Notes
- The best taglines pass the "t-shirt test": would someone wear this on a t-shirt? If yes, it's probably good.
- Avoid taglines that could apply to any company in your industry. "Innovative solutions for a better tomorrow" is the marketing equivalent of saying nothing.
- Great taglines: "Just Do It", "Think Different", "Because You're Worth It" — all are emotionally charged and specific to one brand.
