## Purpose
Generate dozens of high-converting, attention-grabbing headlines for landing pages, ads, emails, or blog posts using proven copywriting frameworks.

## Prompt
You are a world-class direct response copywriter who has studied the work of David Ogilvy, Eugene Schwartz, and Gary Halbert. Generate 20 powerful headline variations for the following:

**Product/Service:** {PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE}
**Target Audience:** {TARGET_AUDIENCE}
**Key Benefit:** {KEY_BENEFIT}
**Tone:** {TONE}
**Where it will be used:** {PLACEMENT}

For each headline, use a different proven copywriting formula:
1. **How-To** — "How to {benefit} without {pain point}"
2. **Number List** — "{Number} Ways to {achieve result}"
3. **Question** — "Are You Still {common mistake}?"
4. **Command** — "{Strong verb} Your {noun} in {timeframe}"
5. **Testimonial** — "How {persona} {achieved result} in {timeframe}"
6. **Curiosity Gap** — Hint at a secret or surprising fact
7. **Fear of Missing Out** — Urgency and scarcity angle
8. **Before/After** — Contrast the current pain with the desired outcome
9. **Controversial** — Challenge a common belief in the industry
10. **Specificity** — Use exact numbers and data points

For each headline:
- Mark it with the formula name used
- Rate it 1–10 for emotional impact
- Add a one-line explanation of why it works

Then pick your **top 3** and explain why they would outperform the rest for this specific audience.

## Variables
- {PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE} – What you're selling (e.g., "an online course on freelance copywriting")
- {TARGET_AUDIENCE} – Who you're targeting (e.g., "aspiring freelance writers aged 25–40")
- {KEY_BENEFIT} – The primary promise (e.g., "earn $5K/month writing from home")
- {TONE} – Voice (e.g., "bold and urgent", "friendly and conversational", "professional and authoritative")
- {PLACEMENT} – Where it appears (e.g., "Facebook ad", "email subject line", "landing page hero section")

## Notes
- Headlines account for 80% of whether someone reads further (Ogilvy's rule). This prompt treats headline writing with the seriousness it deserves.
- Run this prompt multiple times with different tones to build a large headline swipe file.
- Follow up with: "Now write 5 subheadlines that pair with headline #3."
