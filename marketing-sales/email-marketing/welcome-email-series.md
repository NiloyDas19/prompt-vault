## Purpose
Create a strategic welcome email series that onboards new subscribers, builds trust, and guides them toward their first purchase or key action.

## Prompt
Design a 5-email welcome series for new subscribers/customers. Each email should build on the last, gradually moving the reader from "who are you?" to "take my money."

**Brand:** {BRAND_NAME}
**What We Do:** {BUSINESS_DESCRIPTION}
**New Subscriber Source:** {SIGNUP_CONTEXT}
**Lead Magnet / Offer They Signed Up For:** {LEAD_MAGNET}
**Primary Goal of the Series:** {SERIES_GOAL}
**Brand Voice:** {BRAND_VOICE}

### Email 1: The Welcome (Sent immediately)
- Deliver what was promised (lead magnet link, discount code, etc.)
- Introduce the brand story in 2–3 sentences
- Set expectations: "Here's what you'll get from us"
- Ask one question to encourage a reply (increases deliverability)

### Email 2: The Story (Day 2)
- Share your founder story or brand origin
- Make it relatable and human — why does this brand exist?
- Subtle positioning: what makes you different from competitors

### Email 3: The Value Bomb (Day 4)
- Your single best piece of content: a guide, checklist, video, or tip
- Pure value, no selling — earn trust through generosity
- Position yourself as the expert they should listen to

### Email 4: The Social Proof (Day 6)
- Customer testimonials, case studies, or impressive metrics
- "Here's what happened when {customer type} used {product}"
- Include visual elements: star ratings, before/after, screenshots

### Email 5: The Soft Sell (Day 8)
- Present your core offer for the first time
- Frame it as the logical next step based on the value you've already delivered
- Include a time-sensitive incentive if possible
- Clear, single CTA — don't give multiple options

For each email, provide:
- **Subject line** (max 50 characters) + one emoji variant
- **Preview text** (the text that shows after the subject in the inbox)
- **Full body copy**
- **CTA button text**
- **Send timing rationale**

## Variables
- {BRAND_NAME} – Your brand name
- {BUSINESS_DESCRIPTION} – What you do in one sentence
- {SIGNUP_CONTEXT} – How they subscribed (e.g., "downloaded a free guide", "signed up for a free trial", "entered a giveaway")
- {LEAD_MAGNET} – What they were promised
- {SERIES_GOAL} – Ultimate goal (e.g., "convert to paid plan", "book a demo", "make first purchase")
- {BRAND_VOICE} – Tone (e.g., "warm and encouraging", "witty and irreverent", "professional and knowledgeable")

## Notes
- Welcome emails have the highest open rates of any email type (50–80%). Make them count.
- Email 1 should arrive within 5 minutes of signup — delays kill engagement.
- Asking a question in Email 1 ("What's your biggest challenge with {topic}?") trains Gmail to put you in the Primary tab, not Promotions.
