## Purpose
Write a complete cold email outreach sequence that gets opened, read, and replied to — using personalization, trigger events, and consultative selling.

## Prompt
Write a 5-email cold outreach sequence for B2B lead generation. Each email should feel like it was written by a real human, not a mass blast. Prioritize relevance and value over hard selling.

**My Company:** {YOUR_COMPANY}
**What We Sell:** {PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE}
**Target Prospect:** {PROSPECT_PERSONA}
**Industry:** {INDUSTRY}
**Trigger Event (why reaching out NOW):** {TRIGGER_EVENT}
**Key Pain Point We Solve:** {PAIN_POINT}
**Desired Action:** {CTA_ACTION}
**Tone:** {TONE}

Write these 5 emails:

### Email 1: The Opener (Day 1)
- Hook with a personalized observation or trigger event
- Demonstrate you've done research on their company
- One sentence about how you help companies like theirs
- Soft CTA: question, not a pitch

### Email 2: The Value Add (Day 3)
- Lead with a valuable insight, stat, or resource relevant to their pain point
- No ask — just pure value
- Position yourself as a helpful expert, not a salesperson

### Email 3: The Social Proof (Day 7)
- Share a brief case study or result from a similar company
- Specific metrics: "We helped {similar company} achieve {result} in {timeframe}"
- Ask if they're experiencing the same challenge

### Email 4: The Breakup Tease (Day 12)
- Acknowledge you've reached out a few times
- Share one final insight or relevant resource
- Create a subtle sense of urgency without being pushy

### Email 5: The Breakup (Day 18)
- Short, human, and final
- "Should I close your file?" style — triggers loss aversion
- Leave the door open for future contact

For each email, provide:
- **Subject line** (and one A/B variant)
- **Body** (under 100 words per email)
- **Psychology principle** being used
- **Personalization tokens** — where to insert prospect-specific details

## Variables
- {YOUR_COMPANY} – Your company name
- {PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE} – What you sell
- {PROSPECT_PERSONA} – Job title and type of person (e.g., "VP of Marketing at mid-market SaaS companies")
- {INDUSTRY} – Their industry
- {TRIGGER_EVENT} – Why now (e.g., "they just raised a Series B", "they posted a job listing for a marketing role", "they announced a product launch")
- {PAIN_POINT} – The problem you solve for them
- {CTA_ACTION} – What you want them to do (e.g., "book a 15-minute call", "reply with their biggest challenge")
- {TONE} – Email voice (e.g., "casual and friendly", "executive and direct", "consultative")

## Notes
- The best cold emails are short (under 100 words), personalized, and end with a question — not a pitch.
- "Breakup" emails consistently have the highest reply rates in cold outreach campaigns.
- Always comply with CAN-SPAM / GDPR: include an unsubscribe option and your physical address.
