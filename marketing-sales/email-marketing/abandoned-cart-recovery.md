## Purpose
Write a 3-email abandoned cart recovery sequence that brings shoppers back to complete their purchase without feeling spammy.

## Prompt
Write a 3-email abandoned cart recovery sequence for an e-commerce store. The emails should feel helpful and human, not desperate.

**Store Name:** {STORE_NAME}
**Product Category:** {PRODUCT_CATEGORY}
**Average Order Value:** {AOV}
**Brand Voice:** {BRAND_VOICE}
**Discount Strategy:** {DISCOUNT_STRATEGY}

### Email 1: The Friendly Reminder (1 hour after abandonment)
- Subject line: conversational, not corporate
- Tone: "Hey, you forgot something!" — helpful, not pushy
- Show the abandoned product with an image placeholder
- Simple CTA: "Complete your order"
- No discount yet

### Email 2: The Objection Handler (24 hours)
- Address the #1 reason people abandon carts in this category
- Include trust signals: shipping info, return policy, reviews
- Add social proof: star ratings, "X people bought this today"
- Optional: introduce a small incentive if {DISCOUNT_STRATEGY} allows

### Email 3: The Final Nudge (72 hours)
- Create gentle urgency: "Your cart is about to expire"
- Lead with the best customer review of the product
- If applicable, introduce the discount: "Here's {X}% off to sweeten the deal"
- Final CTA with clear urgency
- "If you've changed your mind, no hard feelings" — reduce unsubscribes

For each email, provide:
- **Subject line** + preview text
- **Full body copy** (under 150 words each)
- **CTA button text**
- **Psychological principle** used
- **Conversion optimization tip**

## Variables
- {STORE_NAME} – Your store name
- {PRODUCT_CATEGORY} – What you sell (e.g., "premium skincare", "electronics", "fashion apparel")
- {AOV} – Average order value (affects discount strategy)
- {BRAND_VOICE} – Tone (e.g., "playful and witty", "premium and understated")
- {DISCOUNT_STRATEGY} – Incentive approach (e.g., "no discounts ever", "10% off in email 3", "free shipping in email 2")

## Notes
- Cart abandonment rate averages 70%. Even a 5% recovery rate can significantly boost revenue.
- Email 1 should NOT include a discount — you don't want to train customers to abandon carts for discounts.
- Always include a clear product image and direct link back to their specific cart, not the homepage.
