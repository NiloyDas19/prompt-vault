## Purpose
Design an automated drip campaign that warms up cold leads over time, educating them and building trust until they're ready to buy.

## Prompt
Create a 6-email lead nurture drip campaign that moves a cold lead through the buyer's journey: Awareness → Consideration → Decision.

**Business:** {BUSINESS}
**Product/Service:** {PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE}
**Lead Source:** {LEAD_SOURCE}
**Buyer's Journey Stage at Entry:** {ENTRY_STAGE}
**Average Sales Cycle:** {SALES_CYCLE_LENGTH}
**Target Persona:** {PERSONA}
**Biggest Competitor:** {COMPETITOR}

### Awareness Stage
**Email 1 (Day 0): The Educator**
- Teach something valuable about the problem space
- No mention of your product
- Position yourself as a thought leader

**Email 2 (Day 5): The Myth Buster**
- Challenge a common misconception in your industry
- "Most people think {X}, but the data shows {Y}"
- Build credibility through contrarian insight

### Consideration Stage
**Email 3 (Day 12): The Comparison Guide**
- "How to evaluate {product category}: the honest buyer's guide"
- Include criteria that favor your strengths without directly naming competitors
- Be genuinely helpful — don't be a thinly veiled sales pitch

**Email 4 (Day 20): The Case Study**
- In-depth customer success story
- Before → Challenge → Solution → Results → Quote
- Focus on a customer similar to the lead's profile

### Decision Stage
**Email 5 (Day 30): The Objection Crusher**
- Address the top 3 objections head-on with evidence
- Include a comparison chart if appropriate
- Offer a risk-reversal: "money-back guarantee", "free trial", "pilot program"

**Email 6 (Day 40): The Personalized Ask**
- Direct but not aggressive: "Based on what you've been reading about, I think we should talk"
- Include a calendar link for easy booking
- P.S. with a final piece of social proof

For each email: subject line, full copy, CTA, and the specific behavioral trigger that should send it (e.g., "send if they opened Email 3 but didn't click the CTA").

## Variables
- {BUSINESS} – Your company
- {PRODUCT_OR_SERVICE} – What you sell
- {LEAD_SOURCE} – How the lead entered the funnel (e.g., "downloaded a whitepaper", "attended a webinar")
- {ENTRY_STAGE} – Where they are (e.g., "just learned about the problem", "actively comparing solutions")
- {SALES_CYCLE_LENGTH} – How long deals typically take (e.g., "2 weeks", "3 months")
- {PERSONA} – Who the lead is (job title, company size, pain points)
- {COMPETITOR} – Main competitor they're likely also evaluating

## Notes
- Drip campaigns work because they respect the buyer's timeline. Not everyone is ready to buy today — but they might be in 30 days.
- Behavioral triggers beat timed intervals. If someone opens an email 4 times, they're hot — skip ahead to the decision stage.
- Always include an "I'm not interested" unsubscribe option that removes them from the drip but keeps them on your general list.
