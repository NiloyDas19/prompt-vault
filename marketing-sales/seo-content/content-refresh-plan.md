## Purpose
Audit and refresh existing content that has lost rankings or traffic, updating it to reclaim and improve search positions.

## Prompt
Help me refresh and optimize an existing piece of content that has declined in search performance. Diagnose the issues and provide a specific action plan to recover and improve rankings.

**Content URL:** {CONTENT_URL}
**Original Publish Date:** {PUBLISH_DATE}
**Current Content (paste or summarize):** {CURRENT_CONTENT}
**Target Keyword:** {TARGET_KEYWORD}
**Current Ranking Position:** {CURRENT_POSITION}
**Peak Ranking Position:** {PEAK_POSITION}
**Traffic Trend:** {TRAFFIC_TREND}

### Analysis:

**1. Freshness Audit**
- Identify all outdated statistics, references, and claims
- Flag dead links or broken resources
- Note any tools, products, or companies mentioned that no longer exist
- Identify screenshots or images that look dated

**2. Competitive Gap Analysis**
- What are the current top 3 results covering that this article doesn't?
- What new subtopics have emerged since publication?
- What new data or research should be incorporated?

**3. Content Improvement Plan**
- Sections to add (new H2s or H3s)
- Sections to remove or consolidate (trim the fluff)
- Sections to rewrite for clarity or depth
- Updated statistics and data points to include
- New examples or case studies to add
- Internal links to add (newer articles to cross-link)

**4. SEO Optimization**
- Title tag update recommendation (keep URL equity — don't change the slug)
- Meta description refresh
- Header hierarchy review
- Keyword density check — any missing secondary keywords?
- Featured snippet optimization for current PAA questions

**5. Refresh Checklist**
A step-by-step checklist to execute the update:
- [ ] Update publish date to today
- [ ] Update all statistics to current year
- [ ] Add {N} new sections
- [ ] Remove/consolidate {N} weak sections
- [ ] Update all screenshots
- [ ] Add {N} new internal links
- [ ] Refresh meta tags
- [ ] Submit to Google Search Console for re-indexing

## Variables
- {CONTENT_URL} – URL of the content to refresh
- {PUBLISH_DATE} – When originally published
- {CURRENT_CONTENT} – Paste the content or key sections
- {TARGET_KEYWORD} – Primary keyword
- {CURRENT_POSITION} – Where it ranks now
- {PEAK_POSITION} – Where it used to rank
- {TRAFFIC_TREND} – Traffic trend (e.g., "dropped 60% over 6 months", "gradually declining")

## Notes
- Refreshing existing content is often 3–5x more effective than writing new content. You already have backlinks, authority, and URL equity.
- Always update the published date when you make substantial changes — Google favors fresh content.
- Don't change the URL slug. Changing URLs loses all accumulated backlinks and authority.
