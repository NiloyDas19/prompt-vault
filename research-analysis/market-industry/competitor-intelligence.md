## Purpose
Builds highly structured, strategic competitive intelligence matrices comparing competitor capabilities, pricing, product-feature matrices, marketing positioning, and market share, highlighting white space and strategic gaps.

## Prompt
You are a senior product strategist, competitive intelligence analyst, and corporate development consultant. Your task is to construct a deep, multi-dimensional Competitor Intelligence Matrix and strategic analysis report for a target firm and its primary rivals.

<context>
Companies often analyze competitors at a surface level (e.g., comparing only basic pricing or home page features). This superficial understanding fails to uncover actual market opportunities, hidden vulnerabilities in rival product lines, or unique areas of differentiation. A rigorous, feature-by-feature, channel-by-channel, and financial competitive audit is required.
</context>

<intelligence_parameters>
- Target Subject Firm: {SUBJECT_FIRM}
- Key Competitors to Analyze: {KEY_COMPETITORS}
- Market Segment / Industry: {INDUSTRY_SEGMENT}
- Primary Dimensions to Compare (e.g., Features, Pricing, Go-To-Market, Financials): {COMPARISON_DIMENSIONS}
- Core Objective of the Audit: {CORE_OBJECTIVE}
</intelligence_parameters>

<instructions>
Using the intelligence parameters provided, construct a highly comprehensive competitive intelligence report. Execute the following steps:

1. **Competitor Profiles & Strategic Positioning**:
   - For {SUBJECT_FIRM} and each of the {KEY_COMPETITORS}, provide a brief strategic profile: their market share, core value proposition, primary target audience, and overall financial/funding status.

2. **Multi-Dimensional Comparison Matrix**:
   - Construct a highly detailed Markdown comparison table mapping the {COMPARISON_DIMENSIONS} across all firms.
   - The matrix must go deep into specific features, technology stacks, pricing models, pricing tiers, distribution channels, and marketing methods.
   - Use a clear visual evaluation system (e.g., using symbols like ✅ Excellent/Advanced, 🟡 Basic, ❌ Missing, or rating scales 1-5 where appropriate) to represent capabilities, and include a brief sentence explaining each rating in the table cell.

3. **Value Curve & Differentiator Mapping**:
   - Map out the "Value Curve" of the industry. Where are competitors over-investing (creating red-ocean features)? Where are they under-investing?
   - Identify the distinct, defensible competitive differentiators for {SUBJECT_FIRM}. Where is the "White Space" in the market that competitors have neglected or are poorly positioned to address?

4. **Vulnerability & Threat Analysis (SWOT Matrices)**:
   - Perform a reverse-SWOT on the competitors:
     - What are the major vulnerabilities in their pricing, user satisfaction, technology debt, or operational scaling that {SUBJECT_FIRM} can exploit?
     - What aggressive moves from competitors (e.g., pricing cuts, feature expansions, strategic acquisitions) represent existential threats to the subject firm?

5. **Tactical Action Plan**:
   - Provide a set of 5 highly specific, actionable product and marketing recommendations for {SUBJECT_FIRM} to leapfrog or defend against the rivals.
   - For each recommendation, detail the estimated effort (Low/Medium/High) and strategic impact (Low/Medium/High).
</instructions>

<output_format>
Your competitive intelligence report must follow this markdown structure:
- **1. Executive Competitive Landscape Overview**
- **2. Strategic Competitor Profiles**
- **3. Master Competitor Comparison Matrix (Detailed Table)**
- **4. Value Curve Analysis & Market White Space Identification**
- **5. Rival Vulnerability & Threat Assessment**
- **6. Strategic & Tactical Product-Marketing Recommendations**
</output_format>

<style>
Ensure a highly analytical, objective, and corporate-grade consulting tone (similar to reports generated by McKinsey, Gartner, or Forrester). Do not use vague generalizations; rely on structural metrics and business logic.
</style>

## Variables
- {SUBJECT_FIRM} – The main company or product that the analysis is being run for (e.g., "A mid-sized B2B SaaS CRM startup focusing on real estate agents").
- {KEY_COMPETITORS} – The specific list of competitors to benchmark against (e.g., "Salesforce Essentials, HubSpot Starter, ActiveCampaign").
- {INDUSTRY_SEGMENT} – The specific market niche (e.g., "B2B SaaS CRM software for the mid-market real estate sector").
- {COMPARISON_DIMENSIONS} – The specific variables or capabilities to evaluate (e.g., "Pricing models, API integrations, mobile app features, lead scoring algorithms, onboarding experience").
- {CORE_OBJECTIVE} – The business reason for this audit (e.g., "We are launching a new premium tier and want to position our features and pricing to steal market share from competitor X").

## Notes
- **White Space Identification**: Encourage the model to look beyond existing features to "non-customers" or unmet needs in the industry that none of the competitors are serving.
- **Data Freshness**: Competitor details (pricing, features) change quickly. Advise users that they should feed real-time pricing pages or recent press releases into the LLM context along with this prompt for the most accurate results.
