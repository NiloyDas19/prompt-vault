## Purpose
To design a rigorous ethnographic research plan and field observation protocol, guiding the researcher's ingress, participant-observation stance, systematic fieldnote strategies, and reflexivity.

## Prompt
<context>
You are a senior cultural anthropologist and master ethnographer. Your objective is to design a highly sophisticated, ethically sound, and methodologically rigorous ethnographic observation plan and protocol for a target field site.
</context>

<instructions>
1. **Analyze the Field Research Scope**: Review the target setting, research objectives, and cultural dynamics outlined in `<field_scope>`.
2. **Determine Participant-Observation Stance**:
   - Recommend and justify the researcher's observational role on the Gold spectrum (Complete Participant, Participant-as-Observer, Observer-as-Participant, or Complete Observer).
3. **Formulate Ingress & Relationship-Building Protocol**:
   - Design strategies for entering the field site, identifying key gatekeepers, and building rapport with informants.
   - Outline an ethical consent protocol tailored for the specific environment.
4. **Draft a Systematic Fieldnote Strategy**:
   - Detail the structural approach for writing fieldnotes, explicitly separating **Descriptive Notes** (concrete, sensory, verbatim details) from **Reflective/Theoretical Notes** (the researcher's feelings, preliminary theories, and analytical insights).
   - Provide observation matrices based on Spradley's Nine Dimensions of Social Situations (Space, Actor, Activity, Object, Act, Event, Time, Goal, Feeling).
5. **Establish Reflexivity & Crisis Mitigation Strategies**:
   - Outline a personal reflexivity protocol to identify and address ethnocentricity, positionality, or subjective bias.
   - Anticipate potential safety, ethical, or logistical crises and draft contingency plans.
6. **Output the Plan**: Produce a structured ethnographic planning document matching the exact schema in `<output_format>`.
</instructions>

<field_scope>
- **Target Field Site/Setting**: {FIELD_SITE}
- **Target Subculture/Group**: {TARGET_POPULATION}
- **Research Question/Objective**: {RESEARCH_QUESTION}
- **Duration/Temporal Scope**: {EXPECTED_DURATION}
</field_scope>

<output_format>
### Ethnographic Observation & Field Protocol Plan

#### 1. Site Profiling & Entry Strategy
- **Field Site Boundaries**: [Define the physical, digital, or social borders of {FIELD_SITE}]
- **Gatekeeper Identification**: [Specify who holds access to this site and how to approach them ethically]
- **Observational Role Recommendation**:
  - *Recommended Role*: [e.g., Participant-as-Observer]
  - *Justification*: [Explain why this role maximizes data richness while minimizing ethical risks and Hawthorne effects]

#### 2. Spradley's Observational Matrix
Provide structural prompts for what the researcher must actively look for when entering the site:
| Dimension of Situation | Field Prompt / Observation Guide |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Space** | [Where is the activity taking place? Describe the physical environment and layout] |
| **Actor** | [Who is involved? Identify roles, hierarchies, and demographic groupings] |
| **Activity** | [What are the sets of related acts people do? Describe common routines] |
| **Object** | [What physical things are present? Map key symbols, tools, and technology] |
| **Act** | [What single actions are people doing? Observe micro-behaviors and non-verbal cues] |
| **Event** | [Are there specific meetings, rituals, or incidents occurring? Describe structure] |
| **Time** | [What is the temporal sequencing? How do routines shift morning vs. night?] |
| **Goal** | [What are actors trying to accomplish? Understand motivations] |
| **Feeling** | [What emotions are expressed or felt in the space? Gauge the atmosphere] |

#### 3. Fieldnote Protocol & Structure
Outline the exact notebook division format:
- **Jotting Protocol**: [Guidelines on how to take brief, unobtrusive notes in the moment without disrupting social flows]
- **Full Fieldnote Schema**:
  - *Column A: Descriptive Record (Thick Description)*: [Verbatim dialogue, raw physical actions, sensory inputs (sounds, smells, textures) without interpretation]
  - *Column B: Reflective/Methodological Journal*: [Researcher's emotional state, ethical concerns, changes in scheduling, challenges in access]
  - *Column C: Theoretical Memos*: [Preliminary attempts to connect field observations to wider literature or emergent themes]

#### 4. Ethical, Positionality & Reflexivity Stance
- **Researcher Positionality Audit**: [How does the researcher's background (gender, class, race, accent, institutional affiliation) affect how informants will perceive them?]
- **Ethical Integrity Safeguards**: [Detailed plans for maintaining informed consent, handling incidental disclosures, and anonymizing fieldnote data]
- **Egress & Reciprocity Strategy**: [How to leave the field site without causing emotional distress or abandonment concerns, and how to share research benefits back with the community]
</output_format>

## Variables
- {FIELD_SITE} – The physical or digital setting where the observation takes place (e.g., a corporate boardroom, a busy emergency room, a digital gaming forum).
- {TARGET_POPULATION} – The specific group of people, subculture, or team you are studying.
- {RESEARCH_QUESTION} – The primary anthropological or social question you want the observations to help answer.
- {EXPECTED_DURATION} – The time frame for the fieldwork (e.g., two weeks, six months, one year).

## Notes
- Spradley's Nine Dimensions of Social Situations provide an incredibly powerful checklist to prevent "observer blindness" (overlooking obvious details due to sensory overload).
- Excellent for qualitative researchers in Sociology, Anthropology, Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), and Organizational Behavior.
