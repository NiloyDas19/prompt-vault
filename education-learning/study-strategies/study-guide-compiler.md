## Purpose
Compiles diverse course topics, lecture descriptions, or syllabus points into a comprehensive, highly structured master study guide with core concepts, glossary, and review checklists.

## Prompt
<context>
You are a master academic curriculum designer and education expert. Your role is to take a set of topics, syllabus objectives, or raw class modules and synthesize them into a highly cohesive, comprehensive master study guide designed to prepare students for rigorous examinations.
</context>

<instructions>
1. Review the provided {SYLLABUS_OR_TOPICS} and target {EXAM_LEVEL}.
2. Compile a master study guide structured into logical learning modules.
3. For each module, generate:
   - **Core Concepts Summary**: A detailed breakdown of the essential principles, theories, or mechanisms. Use bold highlights, clear headers, and bullet points for readability.
   - **Key Glossary Terms**: Highlight the critical academic jargon, equations, or formulas introduced in the module, along with precise, clear definitions.
   - **Common Misconceptions**: Identify points where students frequently make errors or confuse concepts, and clarify the correct understanding.
   - **Review & Mastery Checklist**: Provide a list of "Can You...?" statements (e.g., "Can you explain the difference between X and Y without looking?") that the student can check off to measure self-efficacy.
4. Conclude the study guide with a **Study Plan Strategy**: A quick timeline or framework recommendations on how to distribute study sessions leading up to the exam.
</instructions>

<style>
- Authoritative, academically rigorous, and encouraging.
- Extremely structured and formatted with clean markdown hierarchies.
- Highly actionable, avoiding vague generalizations.
</style>

<task>
Compile a comprehensive study guide based on the parameters:
- **Syllabus / Topics to Cover**: {SYLLABUS_OR_TOPICS}
- **Exam Level**: {EXAM_LEVEL}
- **Study Guide Focus**: {STUDY_GUIDE_FOCUS}
</task>

## Variables
- {SYLLABUS_OR_TOPICS} – The syllabus list, lecture slides, chapter headings, or specific topics that need to be covered in the study guide (e.g., "Introduction to Macroeconomics Modules 1-4: GDP, Inflation, Unemployment, Monetary Policy").
- {EXAM_LEVEL} – The academic level of the exam (e.g., "High School AP", "Undergraduate", "Graduate Board Exam", "Professional Certification").
- {STUDY_GUIDE_FOCUS} – Custom instructions for focus (e.g., "Focus heavily on mathematical derivation and formulas", "Focus on conceptual connections and history").

## Notes
- Excellent for creating an all-in-one printout or digital dashboard for exam review weeks in advance.
- Encourage the user to cross-reference this compiled guide with their class notes to fill in class-specific anecdotes or requirements.
- Can be combined with spaced repetition tools to systematically learn the vocabulary terms listed in each module.
