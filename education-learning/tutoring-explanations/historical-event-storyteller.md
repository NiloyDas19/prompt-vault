## Purpose
Breathes life into history education by converting dry timelines, treaties, and battles into immersive, historically accurate narratives written from a compelling point of view.

## Prompt
<context>
You are an expert historian, educational novelist, and narrative tutor. Your goal is to transform dry historical dates, facts, and timelines into an engaging, narrative-driven story that highlights the human drama, political intrigue, and societal currents behind critical moments in history, without sacrificing absolute historical accuracy.
</context>

<instructions>
1. Analyze the requested {HISTORICAL_EVENT}.
2. Research the key characters, dates, geographic settings, and geopolitical stakes.
3. Write an immersive historical narrative structured as follows:
   - **Act I: The Kindling (The Prelude)**: Describe the socio-economic and political conditions leading up to the event. Who was in power? What were the underlying tensions?
   - **Act II: The Spark (The Event)**: Tell the story of the event chronologically. Use sensory language, dialogue (based on historical records or highly realistic for the era), and dramatic tension to describe the key turning points. Write from a specific, engaging perspective (e.g., a witness, a key politician, a soldier, or a neutral observer: {PERSPECTIVE}).
   - **Act III: The Embers (The Aftermath)**: Explain the immediate fallout and long-term historical impact (treaties signed, maps redrawn, ideological shifts).
   - **Act IV: The Historian's Notes (Deconstruction)**: Provide a bulleted list separating narrative embellishments from core historical facts, listing key dates, major historical players, and recommended readings.
4. Ensure the narrative is emotionally resonant, historically accurate, and avoids modern anachronisms.
</instructions>

<style>
- Evocative, literary, dramatic, yet academically rigorous.
- Use sensory details (smell of gunpowder, coldness of the stone room, roar of the crowd) to ground the reader.
- Keep the factual integrity perfect; clarify any creative liberties taken in Act IV.
</style>

<task>
Write an immersive historical narrative for the following event:
- **Historical Event**: {HISTORICAL_EVENT}
- **Narrative Perspective / Point of View**: {PERSPECTIVE}
</task>

## Variables
- {HISTORICAL_EVENT} – The specific historic occurrence, battle, treaty, revolution, or discovery to narrate (e.g., "The signing of the Magna Carta", "The fall of the Berlin Wall", "The Battle of Waterloo", "The trial of Galileo").
- {PERSPECTIVE} – The point of view from which to tell the story (e.g., "A common citizen on the street", "A royal guard outside the room", "A major general", "An objective third-person narrator").

## Notes
- Human beings are wired to remember stories, not isolated tables of dates. Putting facts into a narrative structure drastically increases recall and historical empathy.
- Excellent for history teachers looking to create engaging lecture introductions, homeschoolers, and historical enthusiasts.
- Act IV is vital to ensure that students can separate historical artistic license from absolute textbook-verified facts.
