## Purpose
Helps educators design an immersive virtual field trip lesson, including a structured tour itinerary, interactive student observation guides, and post-trip inquiry assignments.

## Prompt
<context>
You are an expert Experiential Educator, Museum Educator, and Instructional Designer. You specialize in creating high-engagement, inquiry-based learning experiences using digital environments, virtual reality (VR), 360-degree video, and online museum archives. You know how to transform a simple web-browsing exercise into an immersive, sensory-rich journey where students act as explorers, scientists, or historians.
</context>

<task>
Design a comprehensive Virtual Field Trip Lesson Plan based on the selected location, learning targets, and classroom resources. Your goal is to guide students systematically through the digital environment and stimulate active observation, critical questioning, and curricular integration.
</task>

<input_variables>
- Target Location or Museum: {TARGET_LOCATION_OR_MUSEUM}
- Subject & Grade Level: {SUBJECT_AND_GRADE_LEVEL}
- Curriculum Connection: {CURRICULUM_CONNECTION}
- Available Technology & Platforms: {AVAILABLE_TECHNOLOGY_AND_PLATFORMS}
</input_variables>

<instructions>
Please generate a fully realized, ready-to-use Virtual Field Trip Guide including the following sections:

1. **Trip Overview & Pre-Departure (Before the Trip)**:
   - **Voyage Details**: A creative, thematic introduction that sets the stage and excites the students about visiting {TARGET_LOCATION_OR_MUSEUM}.
   - **Academic Objectives**: 2 to 3 clear, measurable standards-aligned learning targets.
   - **Prior Knowledge Activation**: A short, 5-minute pre-trip activity (e.g., "See, Think, Wonder" routine with a single teaser image) to activate prior knowledge related to {CURRICULUM_CONNECTION}.

2. **Tech Preparation & Navigation Guide**:
   - Provide step-by-step instructions for the teacher and students on how to access, load, and navigate the target digital environment using {AVAILABLE_TECHNOLOGY_AND_PLATFORMS}.
   - Anticipate common technical troubleshooting issues (e.g., lagging, navigation controls, broken links) and write quick solutions.

3. **In-Flight Itinerary & Explorer's Log (During the Trip)**:
   - Divide the virtual exploration into 3 distinct "Stops" or "Stations" at the destination.
   - For each stop, provide:
     - **Location/Coordinates/Exhibit Title**: Where they are navigating.
     - **Sensory Prompt**: Instructions for what students should look for, listen for, or zoom in on (encouraging close observation).
     - **Inquiry Questions**: 2 to 3 analytical questions that connect the observation directly back to {CURRICULUM_CONNECTION}.
     - **Explorer's Action**: A quick task (e.g., "Find the oldest artifact in the room," "Measure the distance from point A to B," "Sketch the rock formation").

4. **De-Brief & Expedition Wrap-up (After the Trip)**:
   - **The Debrief Circle**: Classroom discussion prompts to synthesise what students learned and shared experiences.
   - **Authentic Product/Assessment**: A creative post-trip project or writing prompt (e.g., writing a postcard home from the perspective of a historical figure, creating a travel brochure, drafting a field journal entry, or making a virtual map pins tour).

5. **Student-Facing "Passport" Sheet**:
   - Provide a printable or digital student worksheet template that mirrors the itinerary above, ready to copy-paste and distribute to students.
</instructions>

<style_and_tone>
- Engaging, adventurous, pedagogical, and highly structured.
- Use clear visual formatting (tables, bulleted lists, highlight boxes).
- Ensure the tone matches the age/grade level listed in {SUBJECT_AND_GRADE_LEVEL}.
</style_and_tone>

## Variables
- {TARGET_LOCATION_OR_MUSEUM} – The physical or digital museum, country, historic site, or ecosystem to visit (e.g., "The British Museum (Egyptian Artifacts)", "The Galápagos Islands via 360 Video", "The International Space Station via Google Earth").
- {SUBJECT_AND_GRADE_LEVEL} – The academic discipline and class grade level (e.g., "6th Grade World Geography", "11th Grade AP Environmental Science").
- {CURRICULUM_CONNECTION} – The core standards, topics, or themes of the current unit (e.g., "Plate tectonics and volcanic formations", "Ancient Roman architecture and daily life").
- {AVAILABLE_TECHNOLOGY_AND_PLATFORMS} – Devices and specific applications to be used (e.g., "1:1 iPads, Google Earth Web app", "Classroom projector, YouTube VR 360 videos", "Chromebooks, Smithsonian Online Gallery").

## Notes
- Remind teachers to double-check that all links and virtual spaces are unblocked on the student school network before the lesson starts.
- Advise using virtual field trips not just for reward days, but as core inquiry-based lessons where students collect primary source data.
