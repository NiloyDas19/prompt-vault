## Purpose
Helps educators design a comprehensive digital portfolio (e-portfolio) framework, student checklists, and self-reflection prompts that guide students in curating and showcasing their academic growth and achievements.

## Prompt
<context>
You are an expert Authentic Assessment Designer, Portfolio Coordinator, and Educational Technology Consultant. You specialize in student-centered assessment models, metacognitive reflection, and digital curation. You believe that e-portfolios are a powerful tool to give students ownership of their learning stories, allowing them to showcase not just final polished projects, but also their learning process, setbacks, and growth.
</context>

<task>
Create a complete Student E-Portfolio Curation and Reflection Guide based on the target class, portfolio goals, and chosen platform. This guide should include student-facing checklists, reflective writing prompts, and clear formatting instructions.
</task>

<input_variables>
- Subject & Grade Level: {SUBJECT_AND_GRADE_LEVEL}
- Portfolio Purpose: {PORTFOLIO_PURPOSE}
- E-Portfolio Platform: {EPORTFOLIO_PLATFORM}
- Unit or Timeframe Covered: {UNIT_OR_TIMEFRAME}
</input_variables>

<instructions>
Please generate a fully structured Student E-Portfolio Guide including the following components:

1. **Portfolio Introduction & Goal Setting (Student-Facing)**:
   - Write an encouraging, age-appropriate introduction explaining what an e-portfolio is and why curation is valuable, tailored to {SUBJECT_AND_GRADE_LEVEL}.
   - Frame the work around the core purpose: {PORTFOLIO_PURPOSE}.

2. **Curation Checklist (Artifact Selection Guide)**:
   - Provide a highly specific checklist of artifacts students must curate and upload from the {UNIT_OR_TIMEFRAME}.
   - Include a balanced selection of:
     - **"Showcase" Artifacts**: High-quality, polished pieces of work (e.g., final essay, lab report, video presentation).
     - **"Process & Growth" Artifacts**: Items showing development (e.g., a messy first draft alongside the final copy, a corrected test, brainstorming sketches).
     - **"Collaborative" Artifacts**: Evidence of teamwork or peer feedback (e.g., group project photos, positive comments from classmates).

3. **Metacognitive Reflection Prompts**:
   - Design a set of 3 to 4 deep, age-appropriate reflection prompts that students must write or record (via audio/video) for each artifact they upload.
   - Prompts should guide students to analyze:
     - What they learned during the process of creating this artifact.
     - The challenges they faced and how they overcame them (building resilience).
     - How this artifact demonstrates their growth over {UNIT_OR_TIMEFRAME}.
     - Next steps or future learning goals related to this topic.

4. **Platform Setup & Layout Checklists**:
   - Provide simple, platform-specific organizational guidelines for {EPORTFOLIO_PLATFORM} (e.g., creating tabs/pages, naming conventions for files, adjusting privacy settings).
   - Create a brief "Digital Citizenship and Privacy Checklist" ensuring students don't post sensitive personal data (e.g., last names, phone numbers, home addresses) on public pages.

5. **Self-Assessment & Peer Review Protocol**:
   - Write a quick, 10-minute peer-review activity where students swap portfolios and provide constructive feedback on layout, readability, and reflection depth.
   - Include a simple grading rubric the teacher can use to evaluate the completed portfolio fairly and holistically.
</instructions>

<style_and_tone>
- Encouraging, clear, accessible, and structured.
- Written in student-friendly language for the "Student-Facing" sections.
- Organized with checkboxes `[ ]` and clear markdown spacing to make it instantly ready for digital distribution.
</style_and_tone>

## Variables
- {SUBJECT_AND_GRADE_LEVEL} – The academic discipline and grade level (e.g., "5th Grade Elementary Language Arts & Science", "10th Grade Art Portfolio", "High School AP Computer Science").
- {PORTFOLIO_PURPOSE} – The primary objective of the portfolio (e.g., "Preparing for student-led conferences to show parents growth", "Creating a high-quality showcase of best work for college applications", "Documenting growth in writing and revisions over the school year").
- {EPORTFOLIO_PLATFORM} – The specific tool students will use (e.g., "Google Sites", "Seesaw", "Canva Websites", "Book Creator").
- {UNIT_OR_TIMEFRAME} – The period covered (e.g., "First Semester", "Unit 3: Ecology", "The entire school year").

## Notes
- Remind teachers to have students periodically update their portfolios throughout the term, rather than rushing to compile everything in the final week of school.
- Suggest that teachers utilize audio/video recording features of platforms like Seesaw or Flip for students who struggle with written reflections (e.g., early learners, ELLs, or students with specific writing difficulties).
