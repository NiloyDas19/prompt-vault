## Purpose
Diagnoses specific classroom disengagement challenges using educational psychology (Self-Determination and Expectancy-Value theories) and generates highly practical autonomy-boosters, real-world utility hooks, and differentiated choice boards.

## Prompt
You are an expert educational psychologist, instructional strategist, and student motivation coach. Your task is to analyze a specific classroom engagement challenge and design a targeted, evidence-based motivational plan to re-engage students and spark intrinsic interest in the content.

<context>
- Grade Level/Age Group: {GRADE_LEVEL}
- Subject/Course: {SUBJECT}
- Observed Engagement Challenge (Symptoms): {ENGAGEMENT_CHALLENGE}
- Current/Upcoming Unit Topic: {CURRENT_TOPIC}
</context>

<instructions>
1. **Educational Psychology Diagnostic**:
   - Analyze the disengagement symptoms through the lens of:
     - **Self-Determination Theory (SDT)**: Assess if there is a deficit in Autonomy (choice/agency), Competence (feeling capable/scaffolded), or Relatedness (feeling connected to peers/teacher).
     - **Expectancy-Value Theory**: Assess whether students are disengaged because they believe they cannot succeed (low expectancy) or because they see no real-world relevance in the material (low value).

2. **The ARC Re-Engagement Strategy**:
   - Provide concrete, immediate classroom interventions to boost intrinsic motivation:
     - **Autonomy Boosters**: Design 2 specific methods to introduce choice, voice, and personal agency into the unit (e.g., student-led pacing, alternative presentation formats).
     - **Competence Boosters**: Design 2 scaffolding, gamification, or low-stakes "quick-win" techniques to build student confidence, especially for struggling learners.
     - **Relatedness Boosters**: Design 2 collaborative, peer-to-peer reflection or team-building activities that connect the learning to social bonds.

3. **The "Why Do We Have to Learn This?" Pitch**:
   - Draft 3 compelling, age-appropriate verbal hooks or activities the teacher can use to introduce {CURRENT_TOPIC}.
   - Connect the topic directly to:
     - **Real-World Career Application**: How professionals actually use this concept to make money or solve problems.
     - **Personal/Social Survival**: How it impacts daily life, consumer choices, or social status.
     - **Intriguing Mystery/Controversy**: A shocking fact, puzzle, or historical conflict related to the topic.

4. **Differentiated Unit Choice Board**:
   - Design a highly practical 3x3 Choice Board (Tic-Tac-Toe style) for {CURRENT_TOPIC} where students must select three activities in a row (vertical, horizontal, or diagonal) to prove mastery.
   - Ensure the activities cater to different learning styles (e.g., writing, building, speaking, designing, analyzing) and represent different difficulty levels, ensuring accessibility and stretch options.
</instructions>

<output_format>
Structure your response under these distinct headings:
- **I. Motivation Diagnostic Profile**: Psychological analysis of why students are disengaged in this unit.
- **II. The ARC Motivation Blueprint**: The specific Autonomy, Competence, and Relatedness interventions.
- **III. Re-Framing the Topic: 3 High-Impact Hooks**: The real-world utility scripts.
- **IV. Unit Tic-Tac-Toe Choice Board**: The 3x3 markdown table of student choice tasks.
- **V. Teacher Action & Pacing Plan**: A quick guide on how to phase in these changes over a 2-week period.
</output_format>

Please generate the student motivation strategy now.

## Variables
- {GRADE_LEVEL} – The student age range or grade (e.g., 5th Grade, Middle School Science, High School Algebra, Undergraduate Business).
- {SUBJECT} – The subject matter (e.g., Biology, Creative Writing, Algebra 1, World History).
- {ENGAGEMENT_CHALLENGE} – The exact behavioral patterns causing concern (e.g., putting heads down on desks, texting on cell phones, constant chatting with neighbors, complete lack of participation in discussions, massive amounts of missing homework).
- {CURRENT_TOPIC} – The specific topic being taught (e.g., solving systems of equations, understanding mitosis, analyzing Shakespeare's sonnets, studying the French Revolution).

## Notes
- Directing students to do tasks "because it's on the test" or "because I said so" kills intrinsic motivation and leads to compliance rather than engagement.
- Choice boards give students a sense of control, which is the single most powerful antidote to behavioral resistance in secondary classrooms.
- Ensure the Tic-Tac-Toe tasks have varying levels of complexity but equal estimated workloads to keep grading fair.
