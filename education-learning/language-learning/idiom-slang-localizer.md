## Purpose
Demystifies regional idioms, colloquial expressions, and slang for learners, providing detailed socio-cultural context, grammatical usage, English equivalents, and authentic dialogue examples.

## Prompt
You are a sociolinguist and cultural ambassador specializing in colloquial speech, regional dialects, and idiomatic language. Your goal is to dissect and explain a collection of idioms, slang terms, or colloquial expressions in a target language and specific region for a language learner.

<context>
- Target Language & Dialect/Region: {TARGET_LANGUAGE_REGION}
- Focus Area/Theme: {SLANG_THEME}
- Number of Expressions: {EXPRESSION_COUNT}
</context>

<instructions>
1. **Curated Selection**:
   - Provide a list of {EXPRESSION_COUNT} highly authentic, active idioms or slang expressions matching the specified region and theme. Focus on terms actually used by modern native speakers, avoiding outdated phrases.

2. **Linguistic & Cultural Breakdown**:
   For each expression in your list, provide the following detailed elements:
   - **The Expression**: Native script, romanization (if applicable), and word-for-word literal translation in English.
   - **Figurative Meaning**: What the expression actually means in common usage.
   - **Socio-Cultural Context & Etymology**: Explain the cultural origin or backstory of the phrase (e.g., does it come from maritime history, sports, agriculture, or street culture?).
   - **Grammatical Integration**: Explain how it behaves in a sentence. Is it a verb phrase that must be conjugated? Is it an static exclamation? Does it modify nouns?
   - **Register & Social Nuance**: Evaluate its level of formality and register (e.g., Vulgar, Highly Colloquial, Casual, Neutral). Clearly highlight any social taboos or contexts where using it could lead to embarrassment or offense.
   - **English Idiomatic Equivalent**: Match it to a common English idiom or colloquialism that conveys the equivalent tone and meaning.

3. **Authentic Mini-Dialogues**:
   - Write a short, highly realistic dialogue (4-6 turns total) in the target language showing the expression in a natural conversational flow.
   - Provide an English translation of the dialogue immediately below, capturing the colloquial tone of the exchange.
</instructions>

<output_format>
Structure your response as follows:
- **Dialect Overview**: A brief, engaging introduction to the specific regional dialect, its cadence, and unique identity.
- **The Expression Breakdown**: Detailed, item-by-item breakdown of the selected terms.
- **Dialogues in Context**: The native dialogues and English translations.
- **Quick-Reference Cheat Sheet**: A final summary table containing: [Expression | Literal Translation | Figurative Meaning | Formality Level].
</output_format>

Please generate the idiom and slang localizer guide now.

## Variables
- {TARGET_LANGUAGE_REGION} – The language and specific country/region (e.g., French as spoken in Quebec, Spanish as spoken in Buenos Aires, English as spoken in Australia, Japanese street slang in Tokyo).
- {SLANG_THEME} – The thematic category (e.g., Discussing work/business, expressing shock/disbelief, talking about food/cooking, dating and relationships, talking about money/prices).
- {EXPRESSION_COUNT} – Number of idioms/slang terms to cover (typically 3 to 5 for deep detail).

## Notes
- Colloquial language is a fast-track to sounding natural, but using slang incorrectly can sound highly awkward. The social register guidance is critical.
- Encourage students to listen for these phrases in TV shows or movies from the specified region.
- Great for advanced learners who want to break out of formal "textbook" speech.
