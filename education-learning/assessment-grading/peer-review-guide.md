## Purpose
Constructs a highly structured, step-by-step student guide and teacher protocol for running effective, low-stress peer-review activities in the classroom.

## Prompt
You are an expert collaborative learning researcher and classroom instructional designer. Your goal is to draft a comprehensive Student Peer-Review Activity Guide and Teacher Protocol designed to transform peer feedback from a superficial review into a rigorous, helpful, and collaborative learning experience.

<context>
Peer review is a powerful formative learning strategy, but when students are simply told to "read each other's work and give feedback," it almost always fails. Students either offer superficial praise (e.g., "It looks great!") or overly harsh critique that shuts down communication. To succeed, peer review requires a structured, safe protocol, clear sentence frames, specific dimensions of focus, and guidance on how to receive feedback constructively.
</context>

<inputs>
- **Assignment / Project Being Reviewed:** {PROJECT_BEING_REVIEWED}
- **Grade Level & Target Subject:** {GRADE_LEVEL_AND_SUBJECT}
- **Available Time & Format (e.g., 45-min classroom workshop):** {PEER_REVIEW_TIME_FORMAT}
- **Feedback Protocol Preference (e.g., TAG, Warm/Cool, Critical Friends):** {FEEDBACK_PROTOCOL}
- **Specific Elements to Focus On (e.g., thesis, visual design):** {FEEDBACK_FOCUS_AREAS}
</inputs>

<instructions>
1. **Design a Safe Classroom Culture Protocol:** Outline rules of engagement to establish a supportive, high-trust environment before feedback begins.
2. **Apply a Structured Feedback Protocol:** Detail a step-by-step peer feedback system based on {FEEDBACK_PROTOCOL} (e.g., the TAG model: **T**ell something you like, **A**sk a question, **G**ive a suggestion).
3. **Formulate Target Feedback Prompts:** Write specific, guided questions for students based on {FEEDBACK_FOCUS_AREAS} to keep them from focusing purely on typos.
4. **Draft Sentence Frames & Communication Guides:** Provide concrete sentence starters to help students phrase critiques respectfully and accept feedback gracefully.
5. **Schedule a Step-by-Step Workshop Agenda:** Budget the available {PEER_REVIEW_TIME_FORMAT} down to the minute.
</instructions>

<output_format>
Please present the completed Peer-Review Guide in the following student-facing and teacher-facing format:

# PEER-REVIEW ACTIVITY GUIDE & PROTOCOL
### **Target Task:** [Assignment from {PROJECT_BEING_REVIEWED}] | **Grade Level:** [Grade from {GRADE_LEVEL_AND_SUBJECT}] | **Format:** [Modality from {PEER_REVIEW_TIME_FORMAT}]

---

## 1. Teacher Implementation Protocol
* **Objective:** Students will provide actionable, specific, and kind feedback to their peers while practicing critical evaluation skills.
* **Grouping Strategy:** [How to pair students, e.g., similar performance pairs or mixed-ability pairs]
* **Pre-Activity Warm-up (5 mins):** [Brief activity to model good vs. bad feedback before students begin]

## 2. Step-by-Step Activity Agenda (Total Time: [Duration])
* **Phase 1: Setup & Ground Rules (5 mins):** Introduce the protocol and group agreements.
* **Phase 2: Silent Reading & Note-Taking (10 mins):** Partners swap work and read silently, marking key areas using the focus guide.
* **Phase 3: The Feedback Exchange (15 mins):** Partners take turns delivering and discussing feedback using the {FEEDBACK_PROTOCOL} protocol.
* **Phase 4: Revision Planning (10 mins):** Students return to their own work and draft an immediate revision checklist based on peer suggestions.
* **Phase 5: Class debrief (5 mins):** Brief reflection on the peer review process.

---

## 3. Student-Facing Handout: Peer-Review Guidelines

### **Our Community Agreements:**
1. **Be Specific:** Vague comments do not help. Point directly to a line or section.
2. **Be Kind:** Focus on the work, not the person. Use a supportive, helpful tone.
3. **Be Helpfully Honest:** Don't just say "It's good" if you spot a clear area of confusion. Help your partner succeed!

### **The {FEEDBACK_PROTOCOL} Feedback Protocol:**
When reviewing your partner's work, structured your written and verbal feedback using this system:
* **T - Tell them one thing you love:** Point out a specific strength (e.g., "Your hook in paragraph 1 was really exciting!").
* **A - Ask a thoughtful question:** Prompt deep thinking (e.g., "What did you mean when you wrote [Phrase]? Could you explain that further?").
* **G - Give a constructive suggestion:** Propose a specific improvement (e.g., "I think adding a graph here would make your data much clearer.").

---

## 4. Specific Feedback Focus Areas
When reading your partner's work, focus *only* on these dimensions (do not worry about spelling/grammar unless it blocks your understanding):

* **Focus Area 1: [e.g., The Thesis/Argument]**
  - *Look for:* Is the main claim easy to find in the first paragraph?
  - *Ask yourself:* Do I understand exactly what their central point is?
* **Focus Area 2: [e.g., Evidence & Proof]**
  - *Look for:* Do they support their claims with at least one citation or data point?
  - *Ask yourself:* Is the evidence convincing?
* **Focus Area 3: [e.g., Organization]**
  - *Look for:* Does each paragraph flow logically into the next?

---

## 5. Communication Starter Kits (Sentence Frames)

### **How to deliver feedback constructively:**
* *"I really appreciated how you explained..."*
* *"I got a bit confused in this section because... what if you tried..."*
* *"To make your argument even stronger, you might consider adding..."*

### **How to receive feedback gracefully:**
* *"Thank you for pointing that out. I hadn't noticed that section was confusing."*
* *"Could you tell me more about what you think is missing here?"*
* *"That's a really interesting suggestion. I will think about how to integrate that."*
</output_format>

## Variables
- {PROJECT_BEING_REVIEWED} – The student project under review (e.g., "A digital slide presentation pitching a business plan for an eco-friendly local cafe").
- {GRADE_LEVEL_AND_SUBJECT} – Target audience (e.g., "10th Grade Business & Entrepreneurship").
- {PEER_REVIEW_TIME_FORMAT} – Duration and setup (e.g., "45-minute in-person classroom session, working in pairs").
- {FEEDBACK_PROTOCOL} – The chosen feedback routine (e.g., "TAG Protocol (Tell, Ask, Give)").
- {FEEDBACK_FOCUS_AREAS} – Specific areas for student focus (e.g., "The clarity of the business value proposition, the feasibility of the budget slide, and the visual layout of the presentation").

## Notes
- Instructors should model the peer review protocol live in front of the class using a "wobbly" anonymous sample from a previous year before letting students review each other's work.
- Remind students that they are not the teacher; their job is to act as a "test audience" for their peer's ideas.
- Highly useful for project-based classrooms, language arts workshops, science lab write-up reviews, and university seminar courses.
