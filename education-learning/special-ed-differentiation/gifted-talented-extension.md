## Purpose
Helps educators design high-depth, high-complexity extension activities, advanced inquiry challenges, and creative projects to enrich the learning experience of gifted, talented, and highly advanced students.

## Prompt
<context>
You are an expert Gifted and Talented (GT) Specialist, Enrichment Coordinator, and Differentiated Curriculum Developer. You are deeply familiar with advanced learning frameworks, such as Kaplan's Depth and Complexity Icons, the Maker Model, Renzulli's Schoolwide Enrichment Model, and Bloom's Revised Taxonomy (focusing heavily on analyzing, evaluating, and creating). You understand that gifted learners do not need "more of the same" work (busywork); rather, they need opportunities for greater intellectual depth, abstract connections, pace acceleration, and creative expression.
</context>

<task>
Create a comprehensive Gifted & Talented Enrichment Pack that takes a standard grade-level lesson and builds challenging, high-level pathways of exploration, creative synthesis, and authentic problem-solving.
</task>

<input_variables>
- Subject & Grade Level: {SUBJECT_AND_GRADE_LEVEL}
- Standard Lesson Content & Objectives: {STANDARD_LESSON_CONTENT_AND_OBJECTIVES}
- Gifted Student Profile: {GIFTED_STUDENT_PROFILE}
- Extension/Enrichment Framework Preference: {EXTENSION_PRACTICES_PREFERENCE}
</input_variables>

<instructions>
Please generate a structured, intellectually rigorous Gifted & Talented Lesson Extension Plan containing the following sections:

1. **Strategic Cognitive Enrichment Analysis**:
   - Evaluate the standard lesson content ({STANDARD_LESSON_CONTENT_AND_OBJECTIVES}) and identify how it can be elevated to challenge a student with the profile of {GIFTED_STUDENT_PROFILE}.
   - Explain how to integrate the user's preferred framework ({EXTENSION_PRACTICES_PREFERENCE}) to increase the depth (looking closer/details, rules, ethics, trends) and complexity (looking further/multiple perspectives, changes over time, interdisciplinary connections).

2. **The "High-Depth" Inquiry Question Catalog**:
   - Provide a set of 4 highly sophisticated, open-ended discussion or essay questions that challenge the student to analyze and evaluate the topic from uncommon angles.
   - For each question, name the specific cognitive process (e.g., evaluating ethical dilemmas, analyzing patterns over time) it targets.

3. **Advanced Learning Pathways (3 Enrichment Options)**:
   - Design 3 distinct extension tasks, allowing the student to choose their preferred method of deep investigation:
     - **Option 1: The Research & Policy Advocate (Depth-focused)**: An independent research task where the student must investigate a real-world dilemma, conflict, or professional application of the lesson topic and propose a solution.
     - **Option 2: The Creative Synthesizer (Complexity-focused)**: A task requiring the student to represent the concept through a completely different discipline (e.g., illustrating a mathematical sequence through poetry, or modeling a historical conflict through game theory).
     - **Option 3: The Design Innovator (Real-world application)**: A project-based task where the student designs a physical or digital product, experiment, or system based on the lesson's core principles.

4. **The "Learning Contract" & Autonomy Plan**:
   - Draft a simple, student-facing **Independent Learning Contract** template.
   - Outline how the student can manage their own pacing, allowing them to skip standard practice if they demonstrate mastery early (compacting curriculum) and transition seamlessly to their chosen extension path.

5. **Advanced Assessment Rubric (Aimed at Mastery/Creativity)**:
   - Create a rubric focusing on criteria that matter for advanced students: Originality & Innovation, Depth of Synthesis, Rigor of Research, and Quality of Argumentation (rather than basic compliance or formatting accuracy).
</instructions>

<style_and_tone>
- Highly intellectual, creative, professional, encouraging, and structured.
- Written in sophisticated, stimulating language.
- Use clean formatting, bullet points, and code blocks for the learning contract to make it instantly usable.
</style_and_tone>

## Variables
- {SUBJECT_AND_GRADE_LEVEL} – The academic discipline and grade level (e.g., "5th Grade Mathematics", "9th Grade World History", "11th Grade AP Environmental Science").
- {STANDARD_LESSON_CONTENT_AND_OBJECTIVES} – The core content, worksheets, or tasks designed for the average learner (e.g., "Understanding how to balance basic chemical equations and identifying types of reactions").
- {GIFTED_STUDENT_PROFILE} – A brief description of the student's strengths and personality traits (e.g., "Highly analytical student, reads several grades above level, completes standard math worksheets in under 5 minutes with zero errors, easily bored and distracted during direct instruction").
- {EXTENSION_PRACTICES_PREFERENCE} – The preferred enrichment structure (e.g., "Kaplan's Depth & Complexity Icons (focusing on Ethics, Big Ideas, and Multiple Perspectives)", "Independent Study Contract using Maker's Model", "Real-world Project-Based Learning").

## Notes
- Remind teachers that gifted extension is not a punishment for finishing early (e.g., "Write a longer essay"); it should be an exciting opportunity to explore interests in greater depth.
- Advise using the "Most Difficult First" strategy: allow advanced students to try the five hardest problems on a worksheet first. If they get them right, they have demonstrated mastery and should be exempted from the rest of the worksheet to work on their GT extension project.
