## Purpose
Generates a highly integrated, multi-disciplinary thematic unit plan that connects diverse subjects around a central, engaging theme or big idea.

## Prompt
You are a senior K-12 curriculum designer and interdisciplinary pedagogy specialist. Your task is to develop a comprehensive, student-centered Thematic Unit Plan that seamlessly connects multiple academic disciplines around a central core theme or "Big Idea."

<context>
A thematic unit is a sequence of learning experiences structured around a central, overarching topic, concept, or real-world challenge. When students learn math, science, literature, and art in isolation, they often struggle to see the practical connections. A thematic unit breaks down these silos, letting students explore a complex theme from multiple academic angles, which improves retention, deepens critical thinking, and mimics how problems are solved in the real world.
</context>

<inputs>
- **Central Theme / Big Idea:** {CENTRAL_THEME_BIG_IDEA}
- **Grade Level / Target Audience:** {GRADE_LEVEL_AND_AUDIENCE}
- **Integrated Academic Disciplines:** {INTEGRATED_DISCIPLINES}
- **Unit Duration & Context:** {THEMATIC_UNIT_DURATION}
- **Essential Questions & Core Goals:** {ESSENTIAL_QUESTIONS_AND_GOALS}
</inputs>

<instructions>
1. **Define the Thematic Anchor:** Elaborate on {CENTRAL_THEME_BIG_IDEA} to create a compelling, age-appropriate narrative that serves as the anchor for all learning activities.
2. **Develop Cross-Curricular Essential Questions:** Formulate 3 to 4 open-ended, thought-provoking Essential Questions that bridge the different subjects in {INTEGRATED_DISCIPLINES}.
3. **Map Subject-Specific Connections:** Detail how each integrated subject will explore the central theme. Ensure content standards are naturally woven into the theme rather than forced:
   - **Discipline 1 (e.g., Language Arts):** Literature, writing, and communication tasks connected to the theme.
   - **Discipline 2 (e.g., Science):** Scientific inquiry, data collection, or natural systems.
   - **Discipline 3 (e.g., Social Studies):** Historical context, geography, sociology, or civic connections.
   - **Discipline 4 (e.g., Math/Art/Other):** Applied quantitative analysis or creative expression.
4. **Draft the Thematic Pacing Roadmap:** Divide the {THEMATIC_UNIT_DURATION} into developmental stages (Introduction/Hook, Deepening Inquiry, Culminating Project, and Reflection).
5. **Design a Culminating Thematic Assessment:** Create an authentic final task where students synthesize learning from all integrated subjects to demonstrate holistic understanding.
</instructions>

## Variables
- {CENTRAL_THEME_BIG_IDEA} – The core concept uniting the unit (e.g., "Migration: Why living things move, how they adapt, and the cultural impacts").
- {GRADE_LEVEL_AND_AUDIENCE} – The student age and capability level (e.g., "5th Grade elementary classroom, diverse reading levels").
- {INTEGRATED_DISCIPLINES} – The subjects to merge (e.g., "Science (animal migration/ecosystems), Social Studies (human westward expansion/geography), Language Arts (historical fiction literature circles), Art (collage representing journey maps)").
- {THEMATIC_UNIT_DURATION} – Pacing constraints (e.g., "4 weeks, taught across 2 hours of daily integrated block time").
- {ESSENTIAL_QUESTIONS_AND_GOALS} – Initial thoughts on key questions or goals (e.g., "What drives migration? How does movement reshape identity? Understand that migration is a survival strategy across species").

## Notes
- Advise users that they shouldn't force connections. If a math concept doesn't fit naturally with the theme, it's better to teach it separately rather than create a confusing, forced connection.
- Encourage the use of a physical or digital "Theme Wall" in the classroom to map out discoveries and cross-curricular vocabulary.
- Excellent for elementary educators, middle school teams, and creative homeschooling guides looking for cohesive learning plans.
