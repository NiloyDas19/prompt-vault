## Purpose
Designs a single, highly integrated interdisciplinary lesson (such as STEM, STEAM, or Humanities/Arts integration) that merges two or more distinct subject areas within a 1-to-2 day classroom block.

## Prompt
You are an expert interdisciplinary educator and instructional developer. Your task is to design a detailed, highly practical 1-to-2 day Interdisciplinary Lesson Plan that blends two or more distinct academic disciplines (e.g., Math and Art, History and Literature, Science and Music) to explore a specific phenomenon, solve a problem, or analyze a rich text.

<context>
In the real world, problems are not sorted into neat academic cubbyholes. To build robust critical thinking, students need opportunities to apply concepts from different fields simultaneously. An interdisciplinary lesson isn't just about teaching two things at once; it is about leveraging the synergy between subjects (such as using mathematical ratios to create musical harmony or using scientific data to write convincing environmental policy) to deepen understanding in both domains.
</context>

- **Available Class Duration (e.g., 90-min block):** {CLASS_DURATION}
- **Key Concepts / Standards from Each Subject:** {INTEGRATED_STANDARDS}
</inputs>

<instructions>
1. **Define the Interdisciplinary Bridge:** Clearly explain how the chosen {CORE_SUBJECT_AREAS} naturally intersect to explore {CENTRAL_LESSON_TOPIC}. Avoid superficial connections (e.g., just coloring a picture of a cell); ensure both subjects require authentic cognitive effort.
2. **Establish Dual-Subject Learning Objectives:** Draft clear, observable learning objectives. You must include at least one objective for each integrated subject, plus one synthesis objective showing how they connect.
3. **Apply a Structured Lesson Design (e.g., Hook, Inquiry, Integration, Presentation, Reflection):** Outline a step-by-step pacing roadmap fitting exactly within {CLASS_DURATION}.
4. **Detail the Dual-Assessment Method:** Create a formative assessment check that evaluates student performance and content mastery in *both* disciplines individually.
5. **Compile the Materials & Tech Requirements:** Detail physical or digital tools required to successfully bridge these subjects.
</instructions>

## Variables
- {CORE_SUBJECT_AREAS} – The specific disciplines to merge (e.g., "Algebra (Linear Equations) and Physics (Kinematics/Motion)").
- {TARGET_GRADE_LEVEL} – The grade and ability cohort (e.g., "10th Grade High School, standard algebra track").
- {CENTRAL_LESSON_TOPIC} – The main theme or issue to tackle (e.g., "Calculating and graphing braking distances under different weather conditions to design safer traffic stop zones").
- {CLASS_DURATION} – The time frame (e.g., "One 90-minute block session").
- {INTEGRATED_STANDARDS} – Specific standards or targets to hit (e.g., "CCSS.MATH.CONTENT.HSA.CED.A.2: Create equations in two variables, NGSS HS-PS2-1: Analyze data to support Newton's second law").

## Notes
- Advise instructors to collaborate with colleagues in other departments when designing these lessons to ensure high-quality accuracy in both subject areas.
- Suggest creating shared rubrics so that students receive credit in both classes if taught by separate subject teachers.
- Highly optimized for STEM/STEAM coordinators, curriculum supervisors, and teachers looking for creative project-based day lessons.
