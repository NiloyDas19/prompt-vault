## Purpose
Generates a highly structured, step-by-step active learning lesson plan based on pedagogical frameworks like the 5E Model or Gagne's Nine Events of Instruction.

## Prompt
You are an expert instructional strategist and master teacher. Your task is to generate a comprehensive, highly engaging, and structured lesson plan designed to maximize student engagement, active participation, and deep understanding of the specified topic.

<context>
An effective lesson plan goes beyond delivering content; it intentionally scaffolds learning experiences. By utilizing evidence-based instructional frameworks, teachers can transform passive listening into active, reflective learning. A solid lesson plan anticipates student misconceptions, plans for checks for understanding, and provides clear differentiation strategies for diverse learners.
</context>

<inputs>
- **Grade Level / Target Audience:** {GRADE_LEVEL_AND_AUDIENCE}
- **Lesson Topic & Key Concepts:** {LESSON_TOPIC_AND_CONCEPTS}
- **Class Duration & Format:** {CLASS_DURATION_AND_FORMAT}
- **Specific Learning Objectives:** {LEARNING_OBJECTIVES}
- **Available Resources & Technology:** {RESOURCES_AND_TECHNOLOGY}
</inputs>

<instructions>
1. **Define the Lesson Scope:** Synthesize {LESSON_TOPIC_AND_CONCEPTS} and {LEARNING_OBJECTIVES} into clear, observable student-facing targets.
2. **Apply a Proven Instructional Framework (5E Model):** Structure the lesson sequence using the 5E framework:
   - **Engage (Hook):** A stimulating opening activity to capture student interest and activate prior knowledge.
   - **Explore:** An active, student-centered inquiry task where students work directly with concepts.
   - **Explain:** Direct instruction or structured discussion to clarify concepts and introduce vocabulary.
   - **Elaborate:** An application task where students apply what they've learned to a new context or problem.
   - **Evaluate:** Formative checks and a wrap-up/exit ticket activity to assess mastery.
3. **Budget and Pace the Activities:** Allocate specific time segments for each of the 5E phases to fit exactly within the {CLASS_DURATION_AND_FORMAT}.
4. **Plan Checks for Understanding (CFUs):** Embed specific formative assessment questions and techniques throughout the lesson.
5. **Incorporate Differentiation Strategies:** Provide concrete adaptations for advanced learners, English language learners (ELL), and students requiring additional support.
</instructions>

<output_format>
Please present the completed Lesson Plan in the following professional format:

# LESSON PLAN: [Refined Lesson Title]
### **Target Audience:** [Grade Level from {GRADE_LEVEL_AND_AUDIENCE}] | **Duration:** [Duration from {CLASS_DURATION_AND_FORMAT}] | **Modality:** [Modality, e.g., In-person, Hybrid]

---

## 1. Lesson Foundations
* **Central Focus & Core Standards:** [Summary of how this lesson aligns with standards]
* **Learning Objectives:** By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
  - [Objective 1 (Observable & Measurable)]
  - [Objective 2 (Observable & Measurable)]
* **Key Vocabulary & Academic Language:** [Key terms and how they will be introduced]
* **Anticipated Misconceptions:** [1 or 2 common misunderstandings students might have and how to address them]

## 2. Materials & Setup Checklist
* [Resource A from {RESOURCES_AND_TECHNOLOGY}]
* [Resource B from {RESOURCES_AND_TECHNOLOGY}]
* **Physical/Digital Setup:** [How the classroom or virtual space should be prepared]

## 3. Instructional Sequence (The 5E Roadmap)

### **Phase 1: ENGAGE (The Hook)** | **Time: [e.g., 5-10 mins]**
* **Teacher Actions:** [What the teacher says, asks, and does to capture interest]
* **Student Actions:** [What the students are doing/responding]
* **Formative Check:** [How to verify student engagement and prior knowledge activation]

### **Phase 2: EXPLORE (Inquiry / Investigation)** | **Time: [e.g., 15-20 mins]**
* **Teacher Actions:** [How the teacher facilitates, prompts, and monitors groups]
* **Student Actions:** [Collaborative or individual inquiry activity description]
* **Guiding Questions:** [Specific questions the teacher will ask to nudge student thinking]

### **Phase 3: EXPLAIN (Direct Instruction & Clarification)** | **Time: [e.g., 15 mins]**
* **Teacher Actions:** [Structured explanation, mini-lecture points, vocabulary introduction]
* **Student Actions:** [Note-taking, answering conceptual questions, participating in discussion]
* **Concept Check:** [Rapid CFU technique, e.g., cold calling, mini-whiteboards]

### **Phase 4: ELABORATE (Application & Practice)** | **Time: [e.g., 20 mins]**
* **Teacher Actions:** [Setting up the independent or small group application task]
* **Student Actions:** [Solving a complex problem, writing, designing, or practicing a skill]
* **Support/Scaffolding:** [How the teacher circulates and helps struggling learners]

### **Phase 5: EVALUATE (Exit Ticket & Wrap-Up)** | **Time: [e.g., 5-10 mins]**
* **Teacher Actions:** [Brief summary of the lesson, assigning the exit ticket]
* **Student Actions:** [Answering the exit ticket questions, packing up]
* **Exit Ticket Prompt:** [Specific question/problem to assess individual student mastery]

---

## 4. Differentiated Support Matrix
* **For Advanced Learners (Extensions):** [Specific challenge or deeper inquiry task]
* **For Struggling Learners (Scaffolding):** [Graphic organizers, sentence frames, simplified data, or guided group]
* **For ELL/Language Support:** [Visual aids, key term translations, bilingual glossary access]

## 5. Post-Lesson Reflection Guide
* *[A 3-question checklist for the teacher to reflect on after lesson delivery: what went well, who struggled, what needs re-teaching]*
</output_format>

## Variables
- {GRADE_LEVEL_AND_AUDIENCE} – The grade level or age group, along with special student characteristics (e.g., "9th Grade Biology, mixed-ability classroom with 15% English Language Learners").
- {LESSON_TOPIC_AND_CONCEPTS} – The core educational content to be taught (e.g., "Photosynthesis: light-dependent reactions, chlorophyll, carbon dioxide absorption, oxygen production").
- {CLASS_DURATION_AND_FORMAT} – The timeframe and style (e.g., "60 minutes, in-person lab/classroom format").
- {LEARNING_OBJECTIVES} – Specific learning outcomes (e.g., "Students should be able to diagram the inputs/outputs of photosynthesis and explain the role of sunlight").
- {RESOURCES_AND_TECHNOLOGY} – Available physical and digital materials (e.g., "Interactive smartboard, students have individual tablets/chromebooks, colored markers, blank paper").

## Notes
- Remind users that pacing is the most common failure point in lesson execution, so keeping track of time allocations is critical.
- Encourage teachers to print the "Differentiated Support Matrix" separately for quick reference while circulating during Phase 4.
- Highly optimized for LLMs with strong analytical abilities that can detail concrete instructional steps instead of vague descriptions.
